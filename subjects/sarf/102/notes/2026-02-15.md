## Session Overview

This session focused on understanding Arabic verb patterns (awzan) through dictionary usage and the systematic study of harakat (vowel markings) on the middle letter of three-letter verb roots. The class emphasized practical dictionary skills and the mathematical patterns underlying Arabic verb conjugation, particularly examining how past tense middle letter harakat determine present/future tense possibilities.

---

## Word Patterns (Awzan) Covered

### Basic Verb Pattern Structure
- **Fa'ala** - Past tense, singular, masculine, active voice pattern
- **Mudari'** - Present/future tense forms
- **Root Pattern**: Fa-Ayn-Lam (ف-ع-ل) as the template for three-letter verbs

### Six Valid Combinations
The Arabic language allows only 6 combinations (not 9) for verb patterns:
1. **Fatha in past → Dhamma in present**: fa'ala → yaf'ulu
2. **Fatha in past → Fatha in present**: fa'ala → yaf'alu  
3. **Fatha in past → Kasra in present**: fa'ala → yaf'ilu
4. **Kasra in past → Fatha in present**: fa'ila → yaf'alu
5. **Kasra in past → Kasra in present**: fa'ila → yaf'ilu
6. **Dhamma in past → Dhamma in present**: fa'ula → yaf'ulu

## Root Analysis

### Dictionary Usage Pattern:
1. Enter root letters without harakat
2. Identify middle letter haraka from transliteration
3. Check present tense pattern indicator
4. Verify masdar forms in brackets
5. Note attached prepositions (harf mutalakka)

### Harakat Restrictions:
- **Middle letter fatha** in past → allows all three harakat in present
- **Middle letter kasra** in past → only allows fatha or kasra in present (no dhamma)
- **Middle letter dhamma** in past → only allows dhamma in present (no fatha or kasra)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Always start with dictionary research** - don't guess harakat patterns
2. **Past tense determines present tense possibilities** - not all combinations exist
3. **Only 6 valid combinations exist** out of theoretically possible 9
4. **Language has organic restrictions** - certain patterns simply don't exist in Arabic
5. **Dictionary efficiency is crucial** - use transliteration to identify harakat
6. **Multiple masadir possible** - one verb can have several infinitive forms
7. **Prepositions change meaning** - same verb + different harf = different meaning
