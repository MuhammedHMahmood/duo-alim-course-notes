## Grammar Rules Covered

### 1. Maf'ool Fihi (مفعول فيه) — The Fifth Type of Maf'ool

- **Definition:** An ism (noun) that denotes the **time (zaman)** or **place (makan)** in which an action took place.
- It is **identical** to Zarf — the terms are interchangeable.
- Since it is a Maf'ool, it is always **Mansoob** (in the Nasb case).
- In Arabic, no preposition is needed where English uses "for": سَافَرْتُ شَهْرًا (*I traveled for a month*) — not لِشَهْرٍ.

---

### 2. Types of Zarf al-Zaman (ظرف الزمان) — Adverbs of Time

Both Zarf al-Zaman and Zarf al-Makan are divided into two subtypes:

| Type | Meaning | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Mahdood** (محدود) | Limited / Restricted | Denotes a specific, defined period of time |
| **Mubham** (مبهم) | Unlimited / Unrestricted / Ambiguous | Denotes a vague or open-ended period of time |

**Zarf al-Zaman Mahdood examples:**
- يَوْم — a day (24 hours; defined)
- لَيْل — a night (defined)
- شَهْر — a month (29–30 days)
- سَنَة — a year

*Examples in sentences:*
- صُمْتُ يَوْمًا — I fasted for one day
- عَمِلْتُ لَيْلًا — I worked for one night
- صُمْتُ شَهْرًا — I fasted for a month

**Zarf al-Zaman Mubham examples:**
- دَهْر — a long (unspecified) period of time (could be decades, centuries, millennia)
- حِين — some time (usually short, but unspecified)

*Examples:*
- صُمْتُ دَهْرًا — I fasted for a long time (exact duration unknown)
- دَعَا نُوحٌ قَوْمَهُ حِينًا مِنَ الدَّهْرِ — Nuh called his people for some time
  - Note: Nuh (AS) called his people for ~910 years; the Quran uses حِين because relative to all of human civilization, even this is a short span.

---

### 3. Types of Zarf al-Makan (ظرف المكان) — Adverbs of Place

Same two subtypes apply: Mahdood and Mubham.

**Key rule:**
- **Zarf al-Makan Mahdood** — the preposition **فِي** is **explicitly stated** in speech/writing. The following word is then Majroor (governed by the harf jarr فِي), not Mansoob.
- **Zarf al-Makan Mubham** — the preposition **فِي** is **implied/assumed**, not written. The word remains Mansoob as Maf'ool Fihi.

**Zarf al-Makan Mahdood examples** (فِي is mentioned — limited/defined space):
- صَلَّيْتُ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ — I prayed in the mosque
- جَلَسْتُ فِي الدَّارِ — I sat in the house
- فِي السُّوقِ ، فِي السَّمَاءِ ، فِي الْأَرْضِ

**Zarf al-Makan Mubham examples** (فِي is implied — directional/unlimited):
- أَمَامَ — in front of
- خَلْفَ — behind
- يَمِينَ — to the right of
- شِمَالَ — to the left of
- فَوْقَ — above
- تَحْتَ — beneath/under

These words denote direction but the actual distance/space is unspecified (in front of a mountain = hundreds of miles; in front of me = a few feet). Therefore فِي is not written, and these words are Mansoob as Maf'ool Fihi.

> Do NOT say فِي خَلْفِ or فِي أَمَامِ — just say خَلْفَ and أَمَامَ.

### 4. All Five Maf'ools in One Sentence

A combined example sentence containing all five types of Maf'ool:

**حَمِدْتُ حَمْدًا حَمِيدًا وَحَمِيدًا رِعَايَةَ شُكْرِهِ دَهْرًا مَدِيدًا**

*I praised Hamid a lot, along with Hamid, out of regard for thanking him, for an extended period of time.*

| Word | Type of Maf'ool |
|------|----------------|
| حَمْدًا | Maf'ool Mutlaq (masdar of the same verb) |
| حَمِيدًا (first) | Maf'ool Bihi (the one being praised) |
| وَحَمِيدًا (with وَاو المعية) | Maf'ool Ma'ahu |
| رِعَايَةَ شُكْرِهِ | Maf'ool Lahu (reason/purpose — out of gratitude) |
| دَهْرًا مَدِيدًا | Maf'ool Fihi / Zarf al-Zaman Mubham |

Notes on the irab of رِعَايَةَ شُكْرِهِ:
- رِعَايَةَ = Mudaf
- شُكْرِ = Mudaf ilayhi (and also itself a Mudaf)
- هِ = Mudaf ilayhi of شُكْر

Notes on دَهْرًا مَدِيدًا:
- دَهْرًا = Mawsoof (described noun)
- مَدِيدًا = Sifa (adjective/description)

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Example 1: سَافَرْتُ شَهْرًا
- سَافَرْتُ — Fi'l + Fa'il (doer implied: I)
- شَهْرًا — Maf'ool Fihi / Zarf al-Zaman Mahdood → Mansoob

### Example 2: صَلَّيْتُ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ
- صَلَّيْتُ — Fi'l + Fa'il
- فِي الْمَسْجِدِ — Jar Majroor; فِي is Harf Jarr, الْمَسْجِدِ is Majroor
- Type: Zarf al-Makan Mahdood (فِي is explicit)

### Example 3: جَلَسْتُ أَمَامَ الْبَيْتِ
- جَلَسْتُ — Fi'l + Fa'il
- أَمَامَ — Maf'ool Fihi / Zarf al-Makan Mubham → Mansoob (فِي is implied)
- الْبَيْتِ — Mudaf ilayhi of أَمَامَ

### Example 4 (from exercise): تُوقَدُ الْمَصَابِيحُ لَيْلًا
- تُوقَدُ — Fi'l (passive)
- الْمَصَابِيحُ — Na'ib al-Fa'il (substitute for the Fa'il in passive)
- لَيْلًا — Maf'ool Fihi / Zarf al-Zaman Mahdood → Mansoob

### Example 5 (from exercise): قَرَأْتُ الدَّرْسَ صَبَاحًا أَمَامَ الْمُعَلِّمِ
- قَرَأْتُ — Fi'l + Fa'il
- الدَّرْسَ — Maf'ool Bihi → Mansoob
- صَبَاحًا — Maf'ool Fihi / Zarf al-Zaman Mahdood → Mansoob
- أَمَامَ الْمُعَلِّمِ — Maf'ool Fihi / Zarf al-Makan Mubham → Mansoob

### Example 6 (from exercise): تَدُورُ الْأَرْضُ دَوْرَةً فِي الْيَوْمِ
- تَدُورُ — Fi'l
- الْأَرْضُ — Fa'il → Marfoo'
- دَوْرَةً — Maf'ool Mutlaq → Mansoob
- فِي الْيَوْمِ — Jar Majroor; also functions as Maf'ool Fihi / Zarf al-Zaman Mahdood

## Key Takeaways

1. **Maf'ool Fihi = Zarf** — they are the same thing. Zarf is Mansoob because it is a Maf'ool.

2. **No preposition needed in Arabic** where English uses "for" with time expressions: سَافَرْتُ شَهْرًا, not سَافَرْتُ لِشَهْرٍ.

3. **Mahdood vs. Mubham** applies to both Zarf al-Zaman and Zarf al-Makan:
   - Mahdood = defined, limited (يَوْم، لَيْل، شَهْر، سَنَة / a specific room, mosque, house)
   - Mubham = undefined, open-ended (دَهْر، حِين / directional words like أَمَامَ، خَلْفَ، تَحْتَ)

4. **Critical rule for Zarf al-Makan:**
   - Mahdood → use فِي explicitly → following noun is Majroor
   - Mubham → فِي is implied → word stays Mansoob as Maf'ool Fihi

5. **All five Maf'ools** can appear in a single sentence — the combined example حَمِدْتُ حَمْدًا حَمِيدًا وَحَمِيدًا رِعَايَةَ شُكْرِهِ دَهْرًا مَدِيدًا demonstrates this.
