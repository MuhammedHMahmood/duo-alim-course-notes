## Grammar Rules Covered

### Mabani (المبني) - Indeclinable Words
Words that do not change their ending regardless of their grammatical case (Rafa, Nasb, or Jar).

**Five Types of Mabani:**

1. **All Particles (Huruf)** - Originally Mabani (Al-Mabniyyu al-Asl)
   - Examples: ila, ala, fi, an, min

2. **Al-Fi'lul-Madi** - Past tense verbs (all 14 forms)
   - Examples: fa'ala, fa'alu, fa'alat, fa'alata, fa'alna, etc.

3. **Al-Amru al-Hadir al-Ma'roof** - Present imperative commands (6 forms)
   - Examples: if'al, if'ala, if'alu, if'ali, if'ala, if'alna

4. **Specific Fi'l Mudareh forms** - Present tense verbs with:
   - Jama' Mu'annas Ghaib (feminine plural absent)
   - Jama' Mu'annas Hadir (feminine plural present)
   - Forms with Noon Taqeed (thaqeela and khafifah)

5. **Nouns with resemblance to original Mabani** in four ways:
   - **Meaning resemblance**: Nouns that give meaning of verbs (Ismul Fi'il)
   - **Dependency resemblance**: Dependent on other words like particles (e.g., Hadha, Dhalika)
   - **Letter count resemblance**: Having less than three letters (e.g., Man)
   - **Original Harf presence**: Originally contained a particle (e.g., Ahada Ashara from Ahadun wa Ashrun)

### Mu'rab (المعرب) - Declinable Words
Words that change their ending according to their grammatical case.

**Two Types of Mu'rab:**

1. **Among Fi'l**: 12 forms of Fi'l Mudareh (excluding Jama' Mu'annas Ghaib and Hadir)

2. **Among Ism**: Most nouns (Mutamakkin) that accept I'rab changes when used in sentences

**Important Note**: Nouns in dictionaries are Mabni by default (with Dhamma), but become Mu'rab when used in sentences.

| Jama' Mu'annas (جمع مؤنث) | Feminine plural |

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Mabani Examples:
- **Ja'a Hadha** - Hadha (subject) remains unchanged despite being Marfu'
- **Ra'aytu Hadha** - Hadha (object) remains unchanged despite being Mansub
- **Marartu bi Hadha** - Hadha remains unchanged despite being Majrur

### Mu'rab Examples:
- **Ja'a Rajulun** - Rajulun (Marfu' with Dhamma)
- **Ra'aytu Rajulan** - Rajulan (Mansub with Fatha)
- **Marartu bi Rajulin** - Rajulin (Majrur with Kasra)

## Practice Examples

### Exercise 1 - Identifying Mabani vs Mu'rab:
1. **Sami'a** (He heard) - Mabani (Fi'l Madi)
2. **Mundhu** (Since) - Mabani (Harf/Particle)
3. **Yamsuru** (He helps) - Mu'rab (Fi'l Mudareh)
4. **Yadribna** (They [fem] hit) - Mabani (Jama' Mu'annas Ghaib)
5. **Yaqsirna** (They [fem] shorten) - Mabani (Jama' Mu'annas Ghaib)
6. **Lakinna** (But) - Mabani (Harf)
7. **Dhalika** (That) - Mabani (Ismul Ishara - dependency resemblance)
8. **Isma'i** (Listen!) - Mabani (Fi'l Amr Hadir)

### Exercise 2 - Sentence Translation and Analysis:
1. **Nazartu ila az-Zahra** - "I looked at the flower"
   - Nazartu (Mabani - Fi'l Madi), ila (Mabani - Harf)
2. **Akhadhtu al-Kitab min as-Sadeeq** - "I took the book from the friend"
   - Akhadhtu (Mabani - Fi'l Madi), min (Mabni - Harf)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Mabani Criteria**: A word is Mabni if it's a particle, past tense verb, imperative command, specific present tense forms, or a noun resembling original Mabani.

2. **Resemblance Types**: Nouns become Mabni through resemblance in meaning, dependency, letter count, or original Harf presence.

3. **Default State**: Nouns are Mabni in dictionaries but become Mu'rab in sentences when they can accept different Harakat.

4. **Identification Method**: To determine if a noun is Mabni, check if it matches any of the five Mabni categories; if not, it's Mu'rab.
