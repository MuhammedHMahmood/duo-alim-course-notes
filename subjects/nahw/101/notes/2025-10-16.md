## Grammar Rules Covered

### Types of Pronouns (Al-Dhamir)

**Primary Classification:**
1. **Bariz** (Visible) - pronouns that can be seen and pronounced
2. **Mustatir** (Invisible/Hidden) - pronouns that are grammatically present but not visible

**Secondary Classification of Bariz:**
1. **Muttasil** (Attached) - pronouns attached to other words
2. **Munfasil** (Detached) - pronouns written separately

### Grammatical Cases for Each Type:

**Damir Bariz Muttasil (Visible Attached Pronouns):**
- Used in all three cases: Rafa, Nasab, Jar
- Rafa case: fa'alta (you did) - pronoun serves as fa'il (doer)
- Nasab case: darabaka (he hit you) - pronoun serves as maf'ul (object)
- Jar case: kitabuhu (his book) - pronoun in idafa construction

**Damir Bariz Munfasil (Visible Detached Pronouns):**
- Used in only two cases: Rafa and Nasab
- Rafa case: hua Abdullah (he is Abdullah) - pronoun as mubtada (subject)
- Nasab case: iyyaka na'budu (You we worship) - pronoun as maf'ul for emphasis

**Damir Mustatir (Hidden Pronouns):**
- Used only in Rafa case
- Found in verbs where the subject pronoun is implied
- Example: qara'a (he read) - "he" is understood but not written

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

**fa'alta** (you did)
- fa'alta: fi'l madi (past verb)
- ta: damir bariz muttasil fi mahal rafa fa'il

**darabaka** (he hit you)
- daraba: fi'l madi (past verb)
- (invisible pronoun): damir mustatir fi mahal rafa fa'il
- ka: damir bariz muttasil fi mahal nasab maf'ul bih

**kitabuhu** (his book)
- kitabu: mudhaf marfu bi'l-dhamma
- hu: damir bariz muttasil fi mahal jar mudhaf ilayh

**hua Abdullah** (he is Abdullah)
- hua: damir bariz munfasil fi mahal rafa mubtada
- Abdullah: khabar marfu bi'l-dhamma

## Practice Examples

**Visible vs. Hidden Pronouns:**
- kitabuhu (his book) - "hu" is visible (bariz)
- qatala (he killed) - "he" is hidden (mustatir)

**Attached vs. Detached:**
- kitabuhu (his book) - "hu" attached to "kitab" (muttasil)
- hua Abdullah (he is Abdullah) - "hua" separate from "Abdullah" (munfasil)

**Case Identification:**
- Look at grammatical function, not just vowel marks
- fa'il and mubtada → Rafa case
- maf'ul → Nasab case  
- mudhaf ilayh and after harf jar → Jar case

## Key Takeaways

1. **Memorization Required:** Students must memorize both pronoun tables from lesson 6 - unattached pronouns (table 6.1) and attached pronouns

2. **Case Recognition:** Vowel marks (dhamma, fatha, kasra) are common but not exclusive indicators of grammatical cases. Context and grammatical function determine the actual case

3. **Pronoun Distribution:**
   - Bariz Muttasil: can be in all three cases (Rafa, Nasab, Jar)
   - Bariz Munfasil: only in two cases (Rafa, Nasab) 
   - Mustatir: only in Rafa case

4. **Practical Application:** Understanding pronouns is essential for sentence construction and translation between Arabic and English

5. **Future Learning:** Detailed charts for mustatir pronouns will be covered in the next textbook (Tasheer Al-Nahu)

6. **Study Method:** Repeat pronoun conjugations 50+ times for memorization, following the pattern: hua, huma, hum, hiya, huma, hunna, etc.
