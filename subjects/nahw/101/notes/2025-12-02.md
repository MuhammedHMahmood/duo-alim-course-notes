## Grammar Rules Covered

### Dual Form (Tasniya) Rules:
- **Rafa' case**: Add الف نون (alif noon) with kasra under noon
- **Nasab/Jar case**: Add يا نون (ya noon) with kasra under noon
- Examples: كتابان (kitabani) → كتابين (kitabayni)

### Sound Masculine Plural (Jama' Mudhakkar Salim) Rules:
- **Rafa' case**: Add واو نون (waw noon) with fatha under noon
- **Nasab/Jar case**: Add يا نون (ya noon) with fatha under noon
- Examples: مسلمون (muslimuna) → مسلمين (muslimina)

### Case Recognition:
- In dual forms: الف indicates rafa', يا indicates nasab/jar
- In masculine plural: واو indicates rafa', يا indicates nasab/jar
- Short vowels (damma, fatha, kasra) don't determine case in dual/plural forms

### Mudaf Rule:
- When dual or masculine plural words become mudaf, the noon is dropped
- Examples: كتابا زيد (kitaba Zayd), مسلمو المدينة (muslimi al-Madina)

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

1. **ذهب رجلان إلى السوق** (Two men went to the market)
   - رجلان: فاعل مرفوع بالألف (fa'il in rafa' case, marked by alif)

2. **خطب العالمون في المسجد** (Scholars gave a speech in the mosque)
   - العالمون: فاعل مرفوع بالواو (fa'il in rafa' case, marked by waw)

3. **نصر خالد مظلومين** (Khalid helped two oppressed persons)
   - مظلومين: مفعول منصوب بالياء (maf'ul in nasab case, marked by ya)

4. **ضرب نصير الظالمين** (Nasir hit the oppressors)
   - الظالمين: مفعول منصوب بالياء (maf'ul in nasab case, marked by ya)

## Practice Examples

### Dual Examples:
- كتب الطالب بقلمين (The student wrote with two pens)
- قرأت كتابي زيد (I read Zayd's two books) - mudaf construction

### Masculine Plural Examples:
- جاء المسلمون (The Muslims came) - rafa' case
- رأيت المسلمين (I saw the Muslims) - nasab case
- مسلمو مصر (Muslims of Egypt) - mudaf construction

### Quranic References:
- إن المؤمنين والمؤمنات - demonstrating back-to-back masculine and feminine plurals in nasab case due to إن

## Key Takeaways

1. Arabic uniquely has three number forms: singular, dual, and plural
2. Dual formation is consistent across all nouns (unlike irregular plurals)
3. Long vowels (الف، واو، يا) determine grammatical case in dual and masculine plural forms
4. When dual or masculine plural becomes mudaf, the final noon is dropped
5. Sound masculine plural (جمع مذكر سالم) follows predictable patterns unlike broken plurals (جمع مكسر)
6. These grammatical structures appear frequently in Quranic Arabic and formal texts
