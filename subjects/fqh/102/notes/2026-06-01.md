## Session Overview

This session covered two major sections of Kitab al-Taharah (Book of Purification): the etiquettes (adab) of wudu, followed by the disliked actions (makruhat) of wudu, and then a full chapter on the categories of wudu — fard, wajib, and mustahab — including when each applies and the extensive list of situations where wudu is desirable.

---

## Key Themes

- **Adab al-Wudu** — 13 recommended etiquettes that a person should observe when performing wudu
- **Makruhat al-Wudu** — 6 disliked actions to avoid during wudu
- **Aqsam al-Wudu** — The three categories of wudu: obligatory (fard), necessary (wajib), and desirable (mustahab)
- When wudu is fard (4 cases), wajib (1 case), and mustahab (22+ cases)

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Ring During Wudu
- **Loose ring**: It is an etiquette (mustahab) to move it around so water reaches the skin beneath.
- **Tight ring**: It is **necessary (lazim)** to move it around so water reaches the skin, otherwise wudu is invalid.
  - Exception: If a person is certain water has already reached under the ring, moving it is not required even if tight.

### Touching the Qur'an Without Wudu
- Wudu is fard (obligatory) before touching the Qur'an.
- This applies to an ayah written on:
  - A wall
  - Paper (قِرْطَاسٌ)
  - A coin (دِرْهَمٌ)
- **For books containing ayahs alongside other text**: If the majority of content is Qur'anic ayahs, the entire book carries the ruling of the Qur'an. If the majority is non-Qur'anic, one must still avoid touching the specific pages or parts that contain ayahs.

### Laughing Loudly
- **Outside of salah**: Wudu remains intact; however, it is mustahab to make wudu afterwards.
- **During salah**: Both wudu and salah are invalidated.

### Wudu Upon Wudu (Wudu 'ala Wudu)
- Performing wudu again while already in a state of wudu is mustahab **only** when a person uses the new wudu for an act of ibadah (e.g., praying salah, reciting Qur'an) and then remakes wudu.
- Simply repeating wudu in quick succession without performing an ibadah in between falls under **israf** (wasteful extravagance in water).

### The Ma'dhur
- A **ma'dhur** is a person with a chronic condition that continuously breaks wudu (e.g., chronic bleeding, incontinence).
- For such a person, wudu is **necessary for each prayer time** — i.e., the wudu lasts only for that prayer's time.
- The etiquette of making wudu before the prayer time enters does **not** apply to the ma'dhur.

### Wudu While in State of Janabah
- A junub (person in major ritual impurity) performing wudu does **not** remove the state of janabah.
- However, it is mustahab before eating, drinking, and sleeping, as it raises the person to a degree of purity.

## Conditions & Categories

### Adab al-Wudu — 13 Etiquettes

1. **Sit in an elevated place** — so that droplets of used water (*ma' musta'mal*) do not fall back onto the person.
2. **Face the qiblah** while performing wudu.
3. **Do not seek assistance** from others — unless physically unable to perform wudu alone, in which case it is permissible.
4. **Do not engage in conversation** (words of worldly talk) during wudu. One should focus on dhikr and adhkar.
5. **Recite the da'awat al-ma'thurah** — the supplications narrated from the Prophet ﷺ during wudu.
6. **Combine intention of the heart with verbal expression** — make the niyyah internally and also verbalize it with the tongue.
7. **Recite Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Raheem** at the washing of every single body part.
8. **Insert the wet pinky finger into the ear hole** (*simakh*) when wiping the ears.
9. **Move a loose-fitting ring** around during wudu.
10. **Take water for madmadah and istinshaq with the right hand.**
11. **Use the left hand for imtikhat** — cleaning/blowing the nose.
12. **Perform wudu before the prayer time enters** — except for the ma'dhur.
13. **After completing wudu**, stand facing the qiblah and recite:
    > أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ سَيِّدَنَا مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنِي مِنَ التَّوَّابِينَ وَاجْعَلْنِي مِنَ الْمُتَطَهِّرِينَ

### Makruhat al-Wudu — 6 Disliked Actions

1. **Israf** — Being extravagant or wasteful in water usage.
2. **Stinginess with water** — Using so little water that one is uncertain it reached all required body parts.
3. **Striking the face with water** — Throwing or splashing water harshly onto one's face.
4. **Talking during wudu** — Speaking worldly matters (*kalam al-nas*) during wudu.
5. **Seeking assistance without need** — Taking help from others when physically capable of performing wudu independently.
6. **Wiping the head three times with new water each time** — The masah of the head is performed once only; repeating it multiple times with new water each time is makruh.

---

### Aqsam al-Wudu — Three Categories

#### 1. Fard (Obligatory) — 4 Cases
Wudu is fard upon a muhdith for any of the following:

| # | Situation |
|---|---|
| 1 | Performing any salah — whether fard or nafl |
| 2 | Performing Salat al-Janazah (funeral prayer) |
| 3 | Performing Sujud al-Tilawah (prostration of recitation) |
| 4 | Touching the Holy Qur'an (or an ayah written on a wall, paper, or coin) |

#### 2. Wajib (Necessary) — 1 Case
Wudu is wajib upon a muhdith for:

| # | Situation |
|---|---|
| 1 | Performing Tawaf (circumambulation) of the Ka'bah |

#### 3. Mustahab (Desirable) — 22 Cases

| # | Situation |
|---|---|
| 1 | Sleeping in a state of purity (with wudu) |
| 2 | Upon waking from sleep |
| 3 | Constantly maintaining the state of wudu (*mudawamah 'ala al-wudu*) |
| 4 | Performing wudu upon wudu with the intention of thawab (after performing an ibadah with the first wudu) |
| 5 | After committing backbiting (*ghibah*) |
| 6 | After committing slander (*namimah*) or lying (*kidhb*) |
| 7 | After committing any sin |
| 8 | After reciting sinful/obscene poetry (*shi'r qabeeh*) |
| 9 | After laughing loudly outside of salah |
| 10 | To wash a deceased person (*ghusl al-mayyit*) |
| 11 | To carry a deceased person |
| 12 | Before every salah time |
| 13 | Before performing the obligatory bath (*ghusl al-janabah*) |
| 14 | For a junub before eating |
| 15 | For a junub before drinking |
| 16 | For a junub before sleeping |
| 17 | To verbally recite the Qur'an |
| 18 | To recite or narrate ahadith of the Prophet ﷺ |
| 19 | While studying Islamic knowledge (*'ilm shari'i*) |
| 20 | To call the adhan |
| 21 | To call the iqamah |
| 22 | To deliver the khutbah of Jumu'ah |
| 23 | To visit the Prophet ﷺ (*ziyarat al-Nabi ﷺ*) |
| 24 | While standing at Arafah (*wuquf bi Arafah*) |
| 25 | While performing sa'i between Safa and Marwah |

## Scholarly Opinions

- The Hanafi position on wudu categories divides them into fard, wajib, and mustahab — reflecting the classic Hanafi distinction between fard (established by definitive evidence) and wajib (established by probable evidence).
- The ruling of the ma'dhur (person with chronic excuse) is an established Hanafi ruling: their wudu is valid for the duration of that prayer's time, regardless of their condition continuing.
- Tawaf is listed as wajib (not fard) in this madhhab, reflecting the Hanafi position that tawaf requires wudu but its obligation is at a lower degree than salah.

## Lessons & Practical Application

1. **Elevation when performing wudu** is a practical hygiene and ritual concern — used water is considered impure (in terms of ritual usage), and sitting at a higher level prevents it from splashing back.

2. **Facing the qiblah** during wudu reflects the broader principle of orienting acts of worship toward the qiblah. Even preparatory acts like wudu carry this spiritual dimension.

3. **Maintaining constant wudu** was highlighted through the example of a scholar's elder who maintained wudu for 40 years — immediately renewing it whenever it broke. This is a high level of spiritual discipline and is deeply rewarded.

4. **The niyyah should be both internal and verbal** — combining the intention of the heart (*niyyat al-qalb*) with verbalization (*talaffuz bil-lisan*) is the fuller, more complete form of intention for wudu.

5. **Bismillah at every limb** — not just at the start of wudu, but at each individual body part being washed. This is a level of detail many people overlook.

6. **Using the correct hands**: Right hand for taking water for the mouth and nose (madmadah/istinshaq), left hand for cleaning/blowing the nose (imtikhat) — following the general Sunnah principle of using the right for noble acts and left for cleansing.

7. **Ring awareness**: Anyone wearing a ring should be conscious of whether it is loose or tight. A tight ring **must** be moved during wudu for validity; this is not merely etiquette but a condition of the wudu.

8. **Performing wudu after sins** (ghibah, namimah, lying, sinful speech) is spiritually meaningful — as the hadith indicates, the droplets of water are like sins falling off the body, making minor sins fall away. This provides a practical spiritual reset after slipping into sin.

9. **Wudu before Islamic study and ibadah** — the recommendation to be in wudu while studying shari'i knowledge, reciting hadith, making adhan, delivering khutbah, and performing Hajj rituals underscores that wudu is not merely for salah but is a state of spiritual readiness and respect.
