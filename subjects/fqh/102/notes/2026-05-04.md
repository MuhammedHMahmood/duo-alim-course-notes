**Date:** 2026-05-04

## Session Overview

This session focused on the methods of removing najasa (impurities) from clothing, objects, and surfaces. The class covered two main categories of impurities: visible najasa (najasa mar'iya) that can be clearly identified, and invisible/non-discernible najasa (najasa ghair mar'iya) that might be confused with other substances. Various purification methods were discussed, including washing, scrubbing, burning, and transformation of the impurity's essence.

---

## Key Themes

- **Two categories of najasa**: Visible (mar'iya) and invisible/non-discernible (ghair mar'iya)
- **Primary goal**: Remove the physical body (jirm) of the impurity
- **Different purification methods**: Washing, scrubbing, burning, essence transformation
- **Material-specific rules**: Different items require different purification approaches
- **Animal products**: Complex rules regarding skin, bones, and other parts after death

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Visible Impurities (Najasa Mar'iya)**
- Examples: blood (dam), excrement (gha'it)
- Method: Remove the physical body of the impurity through washing
- Goal: Remove the impurity itself, whether it takes one wash or multiple washes
- Traces (athar) remaining after body removal do not matter if removal is difficult

**Invisible Impurities (Najasa Ghair Mar'iya)**
- Examples: urine (bawl) - called invisible because it can be confused with water or sweat
- Method: Four-step process:
  1. Wash three times
  2. Squeeze/wring out completely each time
  3. Continue squeezing until dripping stops
  4. Use fresh, pure water for each wash

**Water and Cleaning Agents**
- Water is the primary purifier for actual najasa (najasa haqiqiya)
- Any flowing liquid that can remove filth may be used (vinegar, rose water, detergents, soap, bleach)
- **Important limitation**: Vinegar and rose water cannot be used for wudu - only for removing physical impurities

**Shoes and Leather Socks**
- Can be purified through washing (ghusal) or scrubbing (dalak) on pure ground
- Scrubbing method only works when impurity has a physical body (jirm)
- Works whether impurity is wet (ratba) or dry (jaffa)

**Hard Surfaces**
- Swords, knives, mirrors, and oiled utensils: purified by wiping off the impurity

**Ground Purification**
- Ground becomes pure when it dries and traces of najasa disappear
- Salah is permissible on such ground
- However, tayammum from such ground is not permissible

## Conditions & Categories

**Transformation of Impurity Essence**
- When the essence (ayn) of najasa changes into something else (e.g., becomes salt), it becomes pure
- Burning impurity with fire also purifies it

**Seminal Fluid (Mani) Rules**
- **If dry and thick**: Can be purified by scraping (fark)
- **If wet or not thick**: Must be washed, scraping is insufficient
- **Note**: Modern diet makes fluid less thick, so washing is usually required today

**Animal Skin Tanning**
- Dead animal skin becomes pure through tanning (dabagha)
- **Two types of tanning**:
  - Chemical tanning (haqiqiya)
  - Sun/natural tanning (hukmiya)
- **Exception**: Pig skin never becomes pure regardless of tanning
- **Human skin**: Becomes pure through tanning but using it is forbidden as it violates human dignity (karama) and honor (sharaf)

**Non-Edible Animals**
- Skin of animals whose meat is not consumed becomes pure through proper Islamic slaughter (dhabh shar'i)

**Parts Without Flowing Blood**
- Hair, plucked feathers, horns, hooves, and bones do not become impure upon animal's death
- **Condition**: They must not have fat (dasam) on them
- **If fat is present**: They become impure

**Special Cases**
- Animal nerves (asab): Considered impure
- Musk pouch (nafijat al-misk): Pure, and musk consumption is halal

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Modern Cleaning Agents**
- Contemporary detergents, bleach, and cleaning sprays fall under the category of "flowing substances that can remove filth"
- These may be used for removing physical najasa but not for ritual purification (wudu/ghusl)

**Practical Guidance for Parents**
- When a child's diaper leaks onto clothing: wash three times, squeeze each time, use fresh water
- This does not break the adult's wudu as it's external contamination
- Only the soiled clothing needs cleaning

**Contemporary Applications**
- Taxidermy: Potentially problematic (makruh) due to resemblance to living beings and potential interference with prayer
- Modern diet affects consistency of bodily fluids, requiring washing rather than scraping
- Industrial tanning methods fall under "chemical tanning" (dabagha haqiqiya)

**Key Principle**
The fundamental goal is removing the physical substance (jirm) of the impurity. Traces that remain after good faith effort to remove the substance do not invalidate purification when removal is genuinely difficult.
