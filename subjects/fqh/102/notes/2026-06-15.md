## Session Overview

This session covered **Aqsam al-Ghusl** (the types/categories of ritual bath). The lesson systematically categorized ghusl into three levels of obligation — fard, sunnah, and mandub/mustahab — and detailed the specific occasions that fall under each category. A brief discussion also addressed the ruling on washing a martyr (shaheed) and the ruling on a new Muslim performing ghusl.

---

## Key Themes

- The three-tier classification of ghusl: obligatory, recommended (sunnah), and preferred (mustahab)
- The four causes that make ghusl obligatory (fard)
- The four occasions that make ghusl a sunnah
- The eighteen occasions where ghusl is mustahab/mandub
- The distinction between the ruling on a shaheed dying in battle vs. other forms of martyrdom
- The ruling on a new Muslim performing ghusl based on their state of purity at the time of conversion

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Classification of Ghusl
Ghusl is divided into **three categories**:
1. **فَرْض (Fard)** — Obligatory
2. **سُنَّة (Sunnah)** — Recommended
3. **مَنْدُوب / مُسْتَحَب (Mandub / Mustahab)** — Preferred/desirable

---

### Fard Ghusl — The Four Causes

*يُفْتَرَضُ الْغُسْلُ بِوَاحِدٍ مِنْ أَرْبَعَةِ أُمُورٍ*
"Ghusl is obligatory due to one of the following four reasons:"

| # | Cause | Arabic |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | A person is in the state of janabah (major ritual impurity) | إِذَا كَانَ جُنُوبًا |
| 2 | A woman becomes pure from her menstrual cycle (haydh) | إِذَا طَهُرَتْ مِنَ الْحَيْض |
| 3 | A woman becomes pure from postnatal bleeding (nifas) | إِذَا طَهُرَتْ مِنَ النِّفَاس |
| 4 | It is obligatory upon the living to wash a deceased Muslim | يُفْتَرَضُ تَغْسِيل الْمَيِّت عَلَى الْأَحْيَاء |

**Note on washing the deceased (Cause #4):**
- The obligation of washing the deceased falls on **the living**, not the deceased person himself.
- A **shaheed (martyr) who dies in battle** is **not washed**; he is buried in his clothes and salah is performed over him.
- There are **two types of shaheed**: one who died in actual battlefield combat (kukman shaheed) and one who died through other means (e.g., drowning, collapsing building, killed in self-defense). Their rulings differ regarding washing, salah, and burial — to be confirmed.

---

### Sunnah Ghusl — The Four Occasions

*يُسَنَّ الْغُسْلُ لِأَرْبَعَةِ أَشَاءٍ*
"Ghusl is sunnah for four things:"

| # | Occasion |
|---|---|
| 1 | For Salat al-Jumu'ah (Friday prayer) |
| 2 | For Eid prayer (Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha) |
| 3 | For a person entering into the state of Ihram |
| 4 | For a pilgrim (haji) in the plain of Arafah after zawal al-shams (mid-afternoon) |

---

### Mustahab/Mandub Ghusl — The Eighteen Occasions

| # | Occasion |
|---|---|
| 1 | On the 15th night of Sha'ban (Laylat al-Nisf min Sha'ban) |
| 2 | On Laylat al-Qadr (the Night of Power — on the nights most likely to be it) |
| 3 | Before the solar eclipse prayer (Kusuf) |
| 4 | Before the lunar eclipse prayer (Khusuf) |
| 5 | Before Salat al-Istisqa (prayer for rain during drought) |
| 6 | During a time of fear (عند فَزَع) |
| 7 | During a time of darkness (عند ظُلْمَة) |
| 8 | During severe winds (عند رِيح شَدِيدَة) |
| 9 | When wearing new clothing (عند لُبْس ثَوْب جَدِيد) |
| 10 | When making tawbah (repentance) from a sin |
| 11 | Upon returning from a journey (للَّذِي قَدِمَ مِنْ سَفَر) |
| 12 | When intending to enter al-Madinah al-Munawwarah |
| 13 | When intending to enter Makkah al-Musharrafah |
| 14 | When staying in Muzdalifah on the morning of Yawm al-Nahr (10th of Dhul Hijjah) |
| 15 | Before performing Tawaf al-Ziyarah (the obligatory tawaf of Hajj) |
| 16 | After washing a deceased person (للَّذِي غَسَّلَ مَيِّتًا) |
| 17 | After hijamah (cupping therapy) |
| 18 | Upon recovering from insanity, fainting, or intoxication |

### Ruling on the New Muslim Performing Ghusl

| Scenario | Ruling |
|---|---|
| A person accepts Islam while already in a state of **purity** | Ghusl is **mustahab** (preferred, not obligatory) |
| A person accepts Islam while in a state of **janabah** (major ritual impurity) | Ghusl is **fard** (obligatory) upon him |

**Arabic text:**
- *أَمَّا إِذَا كَانَ الَّذِي أَسْلَمَ جُنُوبًا فَيُفْتَرَضُ عَلَيْهِ الْغُسْلُ*
- "As for the one who accepts Islam while in a state of janabah, ghusl is obligatory upon him."

## Conditions & Categories

### Summary Table — Three Levels of Ghusl

| Level | Arabic | Number of Occasions |
|---|---|---|
| Obligatory | فَرْض | 4 causes |
| Sunnah | سُنَّة | 4 occasions |
| Preferred | مُسْتَحَب / مَنْدُوب | 18 occasions |

### Key Conditions to Note

- The obligation to wash the deceased (Cause #4 of fard ghusl) is a **communal obligation (fard kifayah)** — it falls on the living as a community, not on the deceased.
- The mustahab occasions of ghusl are largely connected to worship, acts of obedience, or entry into sacred spaces — indicating that a person should present himself in a state of complete cleanliness before acts of worship or drawing near to Allah.
- Non-Muslims do not have Islamic purification procedures for janabah. If they embrace Islam while in that state, ghusl immediately becomes fard upon them.

## Scholarly Opinions

- The distinction between the two types of **shaheed** (battlefield martyr vs. other forms of martyrdom, e.g., drowning, crushed under rubble, killed in self-defense) results in differing rulings regarding:
  - Whether ghusl is performed
  - Whether janazah salah is prayed
  - How the body is buried

## Lessons & Practical Application

1. **Ghusl has a hierarchy** — not every occasion requiring ghusl carries the same legal weight. Knowing the difference between fard, sunnah, and mustahab allows a Muslim to prioritize correctly and avoid sin where there is an obligation.

2. **Cleanliness before worship** — the 18 mustahab occasions of ghusl are largely tied to moments of worship or approaching Allah (tawbah, salah, entering sacred cities). This reinforces the Islamic principle that external purity reflects and supports internal readiness for worship.

3. **Istisqa reminder** — Salat al-Istisqa is performed during **drought** as a communal act of humility before Allah, going out in simple clothing to make du'a for rain. Ghusl before this salah is mustahab.

4. **New Muslims** — if someone accepts Islam while in janabah, ghusl is immediately fard. Da'ees (callers to Islam) and community members should be aware of this ruling to guide new Muslims properly.
