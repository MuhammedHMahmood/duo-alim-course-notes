## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Category Five - Najis Water (الْقِسْمُ الْخَامِس - نَجِس)

**Definition:** Small amount of stagnant water mixed with impurity, whether traces of najasah are apparent or not.

**General Rule for Visible Impurity:** When traces of najasah become apparent in water, the water becomes impure regardless of:
- Amount (little or abundant)
- Movement (stagnant or flowing)

**Legal Ruling:** 
- The water is impure (najis)
- Cannot be used to obtain purity (la tahsulu bihi tahara)
- Makes anything it touches impure
- If mixed with other substances, renders those substances najis as well

### Water from Trees and Fruits
**Ruling:** Wudu is not valid with water extracted from trees or fruits, whether:
- It flows out naturally (bi nafsihi min ghairi 'asr)
- Extracted through squeezing ('asr)

### Water That Has Lost Its Natural Qualities
**Ruling:** Water used in cooking (tabakh) or made into beverages (ashribah) like soup (maraq) cannot be used for purification as it has lost its natural characteristics.

## Conditions & Categories

### Classification of Ma'ul Kathir (Large Body of Water)

**Physical Test:** Water in a large reservoir where one corner/side does not move when the other corner/side is disturbed.

**Measurements:** 
- Length: 10 cubits (ashru dhira')
- Width: 10 cubits 
- Depth: Sufficient that the ground beneath doesn't become exposed when water is taken by hand

### Ma'ul Qalil (Small Body of Water)
**Definition:** Any water less than the ma'ul kathir specifications above.

### Pure Substances Mixed with Water

**Case 1 - Non-Overpowering Mixture:**
- Pure substances (e.g., soap, saffron) mixed with water
- The pure substance does not overpower (ghalib) the water
- **Ruling:** Water remains pure and can be used for purification

**Case 2 - Overpowering Mixture:**
- Pure substance overwhelms water, removing its thinness (riqqa) and fluidity (sayalan)
- **Ruling:** Water remains pure but cannot be used for wudu

### Natural Changes in Water

**Environmental Changes:**
- Color (lawn), taste (ta'am), and smell (ra'iha) changes due to prolonged sitting
- **Ruling:** Water remains pure and usable for purification

**Common Natural Contaminants:**
- Things water is typically exposed to: algae (hashaish), tree leaves (awraq), fallen fruits
- **Ruling:** Water remains pure and purification can be attained with it

## Scholarly Opinions

The text presents the standard Hanafi position on water classification and purification. The detailed measurements for ma'ul kathir (10x10 cubits with adequate depth) represent established fiqh criteria for distinguishing between large and small water bodies, which affects the ruling when impurities are present.

---

## Lessons & Practical Application

1. **Water Assessment:** Muslims must be able to distinguish between different types of water bodies to make correct purification decisions.

2. **Practical Measurements:** The 10x10 cubit rule provides a clear standard for determining when water can be considered "large enough" to potentially remain usable even with some impurity.

3. **Caution with Impurities:** Any known impurity in small water bodies renders the entire amount unusable, emphasizing the importance of maintaining clean water sources.

4. **Natural vs. Artificial Changes:** Water naturally changed by environmental factors remains usable, but water artificially altered (cooking, beverages) loses its purifying quality.

5. **Proportionality Principle:** The ruling depends on whether pure additives overwhelm the water's natural properties, teaching balance in practical applications.
