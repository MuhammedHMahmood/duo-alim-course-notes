**Date:** 2026-04-27

## Session Overview

This session focused on the detailed study of najasa (ritual impurity), covering two main categories: najasa ghaliza (heavy/severe filth) and najasa khafifa (light/mild filth). The lesson included specific examples of each type, their rulings regarding prayer validity, and the conditions under which they are pardoned or require purification. Additionally, the transfer of najasa between objects and surfaces was discussed with practical scenarios.

---

## Key Themes

- Classification system of najasa into ghaliza (heavy) and khafifa (light) categories
- Measurement standards for determining when najasa affects prayer validity
- Conditions for pardoning small amounts of najasa
- Transfer mechanisms of najasa between pure and impure objects
- Practical applications in daily life regarding clothing, bedding, and contact with impurities

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Najasa Ghaliza Examples:
1. **Ad-dammu al-masfuh** - Spilled blood that exits the body
2. **Khamr** - Wine (primarily from dates and grapes)
3. **Lahmul maytati wa jilduha** - Meat and skin of dead animals not slaughtered according to Sharia
4. **Bawlul hayawan** - Urine of animals whose meat cannot be consumed (predators, swine, etc.)
5. **Fadlatull kalb** - Excrement of dogs
6. **Fadlatu saba'i wa luabuha** - Excrement and saliva of predatory/carnivorous animals
7. **Khurru dajajati wa baqtata** - Droppings of chickens and ducks
8. **Everything that breaks wudu** - All substances that exit the human body and invalidate ablution

### Najasa Khafifa Examples:
1. **Bawlul faras** - Urine of horses
2. **Bawl of halal animals** - Urine of animals whose meat can be consumed (camels, cows, goats)
3. **Khurru tayri haram** - Droppings of birds whose meat cannot be consumed

## Conditions & Categories

### Najasa Ghaliza Rulings:
- **Pardoned amount**: Up to the size of a dirham
- **When pardoned**: Prayer is valid but makrooh (disliked)
- **When not pardoned**: If larger than dirham size, must be washed with water or removing substance
- **Prayer validity**: Salah invalid if najasa exceeds dirham size

### Najasa Khafifa Rulings:
- **Pardoned amount**: As long as it's not kathir (abundant)
- **Definition of kathir**: One-quarter of clothing or body
- **Special case**: Droplets of urine pardoned if needle-eye size (ru'us al-ibar)
- **Prayer validity**: Valid if within pardoned limits, but makrooh

### Transfer of Najasa:
- **Wet to dry contact**: Najasa transfers only if traces (athar) become apparent
- **Wringing test**: If wet impure cloth cannot release water when wrung, it won't transfer najasa to pure dry cloth
- **Wind transfer**: Wind carrying najasa particles affects wet garments only if traces are visible

## Scholarly Opinions

### Imam Abu Hanifa's Position on Animal Branding:
- **Initial opinion**: Prohibited due to potential animal harm
- **Later position (rujuu)**: Permitted if no pain is caused to the animal
- **Modern application**: Spray painting preferred over hot branding

### Hanafi School Methodology:
- **Ibadat (worship) matters**: Generally follow Imam Abu Hanifa's opinions as they are timeless
- **Mu'amalat (transactions)**: Often follow students' opinions (Sahibain) due to contemporary changes
- **Reasoning**: Students lived later and witnessed societal changes affecting practical rulings

### Animal Diet Regulations:
- **Detox requirement**: Animals fed haram diet require purification period before slaughter
- **Investigation duty**: For omnivorous animals, inquire about their diet before consumption
- **Herbivorous animals**: No special inquiry needed due to natural grass-based diet

## Lessons & Practical Application

### Daily Life Applications:
1. **Clothing cleanliness**: Check for najasa size using dirham measurement (approximately 2.5cm diameter)
2. **Laundry practices**: Separate washing for items with heavy vs. light najasa
3. **Prayer preparation**: Remove clothing with najasa exceeding permitted limits
4. **Animal products**: Verify halal diet for omnivorous animals before consumption

### Measurement Guidelines:
- **Dirham size**: Reference point for najasa ghaliza (roughly size of a quarter coin)
- **Quarter body/clothing**: Measurement for najasa khafifa abundance
- **Needle-eye size**: Acceptable limit for urine droplets

### Hygiene Principles:
- **Visible traces rule**: If najasa effects are not apparent, object remains pure
- **Wetness factor**: Dry najasa less likely to transfer than wet najasa
- **Estimation method**: Use reasonable judgment for scattered najasa across multiple body parts

### Modern Considerations:
- **Farming practices**: Humane treatment and proper diet essential for halal consumption
- **Animal welfare**: Branding permitted only without causing pain
- **Food industry**: Research animal treatment and diet in commercial farming operations
