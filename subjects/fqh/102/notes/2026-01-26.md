## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Quranic Evidence:**
- "Indeed, Allah loves those who repent and those who keep themselves pure" (2:222)

**Prophetic Evidence:**
- "Cleanliness is half of faith" (Sahih Muslim)
- "The key to paradise is salah, and the key to salah is purification" (Ahmad)

**Core Ruling:**
- Purification is the foundation of worship
- Prayer (salah) is not valid without purification
- Two methods exist for removing ritual impurity: wudu, ghusl, or tayammum (when water unavailable)
- Physical impurities removed through washing with purifying agents

---

## Conditions & Categories

**Two Categories of Purification:**

1. **Tahara min al-Hadath (Purification from Ritual Impurity)**
   - Also called Tahara Hukmiyyah (De jure purification)
   - Intangible state of impurity
   - Examples: Breaking wudu, state of janaba
   - Methods: Wudu, ghusl, or tayammum when water unavailable

2. **Tahara min al-Najasa (Purification from Physical Filth)**
   - Also called Tahara Haqiqiyyah (De facto purification)
   - Tangible physical impurities
   - Examples: Blood, urine, excrement on body/clothes
   - Method: Physical removal using purifying agents

**Types of Absolute Water (Ma'ul Mutlaq):**
- Rainwater (ma'ul samah)
- River water (ma'ul nahar)
- Well water (ma'ul bi'r)
- Spring/fountain water (ma'ul ain)
- Sea water (ma'ul bahr)
- Melted snow water (ma'ul dhaba min al-thalj)
- Melted hail water (ma'ul dhaba min al-barad)

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Educational Approach:**
- Progressive learning: same subjects with increasing complexity each year
- Integration of Arabic language skills with Islamic jurisprudence
- Importance of reading ahead and preparing for classes

**Practical Purification:**
- Both types of purification may be needed simultaneously (e.g., using restroom requires both istinja for physical cleanliness and wudu for ritual purity)
- Sometimes only one type needed (e.g., changing baby's diaper breaks physical purity but not wudu)
- Water must retain original characteristics to be valid for purification
- Alternative methods (tayammum) available when water unavailable or inaccessible

**Study Method:**
- Emphasis on understanding Arabic terminology alongside English meanings
- Application of sentence structure (tarkeeb) skills
- Regular review and preparation expected for effective learning
