## Session Overview

This session completed the discussion of istinja (cleansing after relieving oneself) with specific focus on when impurity exceeds the dirham amount, and introduced the comprehensive classification of najasa (ritual impurities). The class covered practical rulings for cleansing methods, the theological distinctions between fard, wajib, and sunnah, and began exploring the two main categories of impurity: hukmiyya (ritual) and haqiqiyya (physical).

---

## Key Themes

1. **Quantitative thresholds for istinja obligations** - The dirham amount as the decisive factor
2. **Methodological preferences in purification** - Combining dry and wet cleaning methods
3. **Theological categorization of obligations** - Understanding fard vs wajib distinctions
4. **Comprehensive impurity classification** - Ritual vs physical najasa
5. **Prerequisites for valid worship** - Body, clothing, and place requirements

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Quranic Foundation:**
- **وَثِيَابَكَ فَطَهِّرْ** - "Purify your clothes" (Allah's command for purification)

**Prophetic Evidence:**
- **لَا يَقْبَلُ اللَّهُ صَلَاةً مِنْ غَيْرِ طَهُورٍ** - "Allah does not accept salah without purification" (Bukhari & Muslim)

**Istinja Rulings Based on Quantity:**

1. **More than dirham amount**: إِذَا تَجَاوَزَتِ النَّجَاسَةُ الْمَخْرَجَ وَكَانَتْ أَكْثَرَ مِنْ قَدْرِ الدِّرْهَمِ - Washing with water becomes **fard** (obligatory)

2. **Equal to dirham amount**: وَكَانَتْ قَدْرَ الدِّرْهَمِ - Still **wajib** (necessary) to remove with water

3. **Less than dirham amount**: إِذَا لَمْ تَتَجَاوَزِ النَّجَاسَةُ الْمَخْرَجَ - Istinja is **sunnah**, and alternatives to water are permissible

## Conditions & Categories

**Three Essential Elements for Valid Salah:**
1. **البدن** - Clean body
2. **الثياب** - Clean clothing  
3. **المكان** - Clean place

**Najasa Classifications:**

**A. Najasa Hukmiyya (Ritual Impurity):**
- Also called **الحدث** (hadath)
- Subdivided into:
  1. **الحدث الأكبر** (Major hadath) - Requires **ghusl**, prohibits salah, Quran recitation, and masjid entry
  2. **الحدث الأصغر** (Minor hadath) - Requires **wudu**, prohibits salah but permits verbal Quran recitation

## Scholarly Opinions

**Hanafi Madhab Distinctions:**

**Fard vs Wajib:**
- **Fard**: Established by clear-cut evidence with no room for interpretation (e.g., establishing salah)
- **Wajib**: Established evidence but with some interpretive possibilities due to multiple valid interpretations
- Both carry equal practical obligations, but differ in certainty of establishment

**Preferred Cleaning Methods:**
- **Water only**: Permissible for amounts less than dirham
- **Stones/toilet paper only**: Permissible for amounts less than dirham  
- **Combined method**: Most preferable - dry cleaning first, then water washing
- **Three stones/pieces**: Mustahab (recommended) minimum

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Practical Istinja Procedure:**
1. Use dry cleaning method first (stone/toilet paper) to remove bulk filth
2. Wash hands thoroughly before using water
3. Clean the area with water until **تَنْقَطِعَ الرَّائِحَةُ** (odor disappears)
4. After completion, wash hands thoroughly with **دَلَكًا** (vigorous rubbing) until odor is removed

**Worship Prerequisites:**
- Major hadath prevents: salah, Quran recitation, masjid entry
- Minor hadath prevents: salah only
- Physical najasa must be removed from body, clothing, and prayer location

**Flexibility in Application:**
- One or two stones permissible **إِذَا حَصُلَتِ النَّظَافَةُ بِهِ** (if cleanliness is achieved)
- Water washing **أَبْلَغُ فِي النَّظَافَةِ** (most efficient in cleaning) in all cases
