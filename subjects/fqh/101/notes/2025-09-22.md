**Date:** 2025-09-22

## Session Overview

This session focused on chronic excuses (ma'adur) and the categorization of ritual impurities (najasa). The class began with a review of menstruation (hayd), postnatal bleeding (nifas), and dysfunctional uterine bleeding (istihada), then transitioned to detailed rulings for individuals with chronic excuses. The second half covered the classification of filth into heavy (mugallaza/ghalidha) and light (mukhafafa/khafifa) categories, along with purification methods and permissible amounts for prayer.

---

## Key Themes

- Chronic excuses and their three fundamental conditions
- Equivalence between istihada and other chronic conditions
- Classification system for ritual impurities
- Practical purification methods for different types of filth
- Prayer validity with minimal amounts of impurity
- Special circumstances requiring accommodation (labor, incontinence, etc.)

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Chronic Excuse Rulings:**
- Istihada is equivalent to continuous nosebleed in legal ruling
- Does not prevent fasting, praying, intercourse, or tawaf
- Person makes wudu at beginning of each prayer time
- Wudu remains valid despite continuation of the excuse
- Wudu breaks only when new prayer time enters or different impurity occurs

**Heavy Filth (Najasa Ghalidha) includes:**
- Spilled/flowing blood from all animals and humans
- Feces from land animals
- Urine of animals whose meat is impermissible to eat
- Saliva of predatory animals and dogs
- Wine (hummer)
- Sperm/sexual fluid (mani)

**Light Filth (Najasa Khafifa) includes:**
- Urine of animals whose meat is permissible to eat
- Urine of horses
- Droppings of birds whose meat is not permissible to eat

---

## Conditions & Categories

**Three Conditions for Chronic Excuses:**

1. **Establishment of Excuse:** Must continue for entire prayer time, preventing person from making wudu and praying without recurrence

2. **Continuity:** Must occur at least once in every subsequent prayer time, even if intermittent

3. **Termination:** Entire prayer time passes without the excuse occurring

**Blood Classification Exemptions:**
- Blood remaining in meat and vessels of properly slaughtered animals
- Blood in liver, spleen, and heart
- Blood that surfaces but doesn't flow
- Blood of insects, mosquitoes, roaches
- Fish blood

**Purification Methods:**
- **Discernible filth:** Remove the physical body of filth
- **Indiscernible filth:** Wash and squeeze three times, or wash three times with waiting periods

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Permissible Amounts for Prayer:**
- Heavy filth: Size of dirham (inner palm area when water is poured)
- Light filth: Less than one-fourth of garment or body
- Light spray of urine: Dots no larger than pinhead are excused

**Special Circumstances:**
- Women in labor can receive assistance with wudu
- Wet towels acceptable for wudu if water drips off body
- Wudu socks recommended for public situations
- Chronic excuse holders should wait until end of prayer time to establish excuse

**Practical Guidelines:**
- Person with chronic excuse makes fresh wudu for each prayer time
- Can pray multiple obligatory and voluntary prayers within same prayer time
- Different impurity (other than established excuse) breaks wudu immediately
- Difficult-to-remove filth requires reasonable effort with cleaning agents
