**Date:** 2025-12-17

## Session Overview

This session covered the fundamental principles of Zakah (obligatory charity) in Islamic jurisprudence, including its definition, conditions, calculation methods, eligible recipients, and prohibitions. The class also covered Sadaqat al-Fitr (charity at the end of Ramadan) and concluded with a brief overview of the five-day Hajj sequence from the 8th to 13th of Dhul-Hijjah.

---

## Key Themes

- Zakah as an act of worship requiring proper intention (niyyah)
- Transfer of ownership (tamlik) as the core principle of Zakah
- Nisab (threshold amount) and its calculation based on lunar year
- Eight categories of eligible Zakah recipients
- Prohibited recipients of Zakah including family members and wealthy individuals
- Sadaqat al-Fitr as mandatory charity for Eid
- Sequential overview of Hajj rituals and locations

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Zakah Definition & Calculation:**
- Zakah is defined as transfer of ownership (tamlik) of specified portion of wealth to eligible person with proper intention
- Rate: 2.5% of wealth above nisab after one lunar year
- Current nisab thresholds: Gold standard $12,190, Silver standard $1,307
- Liabilities and debts are deducted before calculation
- Gold nisab: 20 mithqal (approximately 86.48 grams)
- Silver nisab: 200 dirhams

**Timing Requirements:**
- Must pass full lunar year (Hijri calendar) from when nisab was first reached
- Zakah calculated on amount possessed at end of year, not beginning
- If below nisab at year-end, no Zakah due even if above during year

**Sadaqat al-Fitr Rulings:**
- Becomes mandatory when Fajr begins on Eid al-Fitr day
- Amount: Half sa' of wheat/flour OR one sa' of dates/raisins/barley
- Monetary equivalent permissible and often superior in times of ease
- Must be paid on behalf of self, minor children, and personal slaves

---

## Conditions & Categories

**Zakah Obligation Conditions:**
- Free Muslim who is legally responsible (adult, sane)
- Possesses nisab amount
- Wealth held for full lunar year
- Above basic personal needs and debts

**Trade Goods & Assets:**
- Value of trade goods combined with gold/silver by value
- Includes stocks, business assets, anything not for personal use
- No Zakah on personal residence, car for personal use, etc.

**Sadaqat al-Fitr Conditions:**
- Mandatory on behalf of: self, minor children, personal slaves
- Not mandatory for: adult children, wife/husband, working slaves, co-owned slaves

## Scholarly Opinions

**Nisab Calculation:**
- Question raised about gold vs. silver standard - requires consultation with qualified scholar
- Imam Tahawi's position: Hashmites are eligible recipients (minority view)
- Disagreement on splitting Sadaqat al-Fitr between multiple recipients
- Hanafi jurisprudence differences on extra amounts above nisab

**Geographic Distribution:**
- Preference for local distribution unless special circumstances
- Acceptable to send elsewhere for relatives, more religious recipients, or greater benefit to Muslim community

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Practical Zakah Guidance:**
- Complex business situations require consultation with qualified Mufti
- Due diligence protects payer if recipient later found ineligible
- Should not make recipient wealthy enough to owe Zakah themselves
- Prefer giving enough so recipient doesn't need to ask for food

**Distribution Priorities:**
1. Eligible relatives (siblings, cousins - not parents/children/spouse)
2. Neighbors  
3. Local district
4. Professional colleagues
5. Fellow citizens

**Modern Considerations:**
- Credit card debt, home financing, car loans counted as liabilities
- Multiple income streams require careful calculation
- Stocks, business assets included in Zakah calculation
- Monetary payment often more practical than goods for Sadaqat al-Fitr

**Eight Eligible Recipients:**
1. Faqir (poor with some wealth below nisab)
2. Miskin (destitute with no wealth)
3. Mukatab (slave buying freedom)
4. Person in debt
5. Soldier cut off from troop
6. Pilgrim separated from group  
7. Wayfarer without sufficient funds
8. Zakah collector

**Prohibited Recipients:**
- Non-Muslims, wealthy individuals (ghani)
- Hashmites and their freed servants
- Direct family: parents, grandparents, children, grandchildren, spouse
- One's servants and slaves
- For funeral expenses or deceased person's debts
