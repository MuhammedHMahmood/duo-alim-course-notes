# Fiqh 101 - Study Notes
**Date:** 2025-11-10

---

## Session Overview

This session covered fundamental aspects of prayer etiquette and rulings, focusing on things disliked (makruhat) and permissible actions during prayer, prayer invalidation, abandoning prayer, prayer while traveling, and the prayer of travelers. The discussion included practical applications for contemporary Muslims regarding prayer conduct, travel distances, and various scenarios encountered in modern life.

---

## Key Themes

- Distinction between makruh tahrimi (prohibitively disliked) and makruh tanzihi (mildly disliked) actions in prayer
- Proper conduct and movement during salah to maintain focus and validity
- Understanding when prayer becomes invalid vs. merely disliked
- Travel prayer (qasr) regulations and distance requirements
- Practical guidance for prayer in vehicles and during journeys
- Emergency situations that permit breaking prayer

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Things Disliked in Prayer (Makruhat):

**Prohibitively Disliked (Makruh Tahrimi):**
1. Deliberately omitting any wajib act (invalidates prayer)
2. Excessive unnecessary movements (fiddling with garments/body)
3. Wiping away pebbles more than once during prayer
4. Cracking knuckles
5. Placing hands on hips
6. Turning neck (turning chest invalidates prayer)

**Actions That Make Prayer Invalid:**
- Deliberately omitting wajib acts
- Excessive movement (amal kathir)
- Turning chest away from qibla
- Uncovering awrah for duration of one complete integral

**Distance for Travel Prayer:**
- Minimum 48 miles or 3 days journey at medium pace
- Must intend to travel this distance
- Prayer shortening begins when leaving city limits, not at 48-mile mark

---

## Conditions & Categories

### Prayer Shortening Rules:
- Four-rakah prayers (Dhuhr, Asr, Isha) become two rakahs
- Fajr remains two rakahs, Maghrib remains three rakahs
- Duration: Less than 15 full days at destination
- If staying 15+ days, pray full prayers as resident

### Emergency Prayer Breaking:
**Permissible to break prayer for:**
- Robbery of items worth one dirham or more
- Helping someone in emergency
- Preventing blind person from falling into danger
- Midwife assisting in delivery

### Prayer in Vehicles:
- Obligatory prayers invalid in moving vehicles except necessity
- Voluntary prayers permitted with head movements only
- Must face qibla when stationary

## Scholarly Opinions

### Hanafi Madhhab Distinctions:
- Concept of wajib unique to Hanafi school
- Fard: proven with concrete evidence without doubt
- Wajib: proven evidence with some level of interpretive possibility
- Both equal in practical application but different in evidential strength

### Travel Prayer Rulings:
- Resident following traveler imam: completes full prayer in current prayer time only
- Traveler following resident imam: must complete full prayer with imam
- Missed prayers made up according to state when missed (traveler = 2, resident = 4)

## Lessons & Practical Application

### Modern Applications:
1. **Vehicle Prayer:** Stop for obligatory prayers when possible; use vehicles only for voluntary prayers while traveling
2. **Clothing Considerations:** Avoid images on garments, ensure proper covering, remove distracting hanging elements
3. **Travel Planning:** Calculate 48-mile distance for determining travel prayer status
4. **Emergency Situations:** Prioritize human safety over prayer completion when necessary

### Contemporary Guidance:
- Pictures in books/magazines should be covered or turned away during prayer
- Nail polish and barriers must be removed for valid wudu
- Concentrate during prayer by closing eyes if needed for focus
- Use sutra (barrier) when praying in public spaces

### Practical Tips:
- Keep prayer area free from distracting images or objects
- Minimize unnecessary movements during prayer
- Plan travel prayers according to departure from city limits
- Maintain proper dress code avoiding transparent or image-bearing clothing
