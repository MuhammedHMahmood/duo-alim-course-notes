## Session Overview

This session covered the concept of النصيحة (nasiha - doing well by others) as a fundamental aspect of religion, as stated by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ: "الدين النصيحة" (Religion is doing well by others). The class explored the five categories of nasiha and began introducing the next hadith on the inviolability of Muslim life and property.

---

## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Main Hadith:**
عَنْ أَبِي رُقَيَّةَ تَمِيمِ بْنِ أَوْسٍ الدَّارِيِّ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ قَالَ: الدِّينُ النَّصِيحَةُ. قُلْنَا: لِمَنْ؟ قَالَ: لِلَّهِ وَلِكِتَابِهِ وَلِرَسُولِهِ وَلِأَئِمَّةِ الْمُسْلِمِينَ وَعَامَّتِهِمْ

**Narrator:** Abu Ruqayya Tameem ibn Aws al-Dari (رضي الله عنه) - the companion who witnessed and met Dajjal, as mentioned in حديث الجساسة

**Translation:** It is narrated by Abu Ruqayya Tameem ibn Aws al-Dari that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Religion is doing well by others." We asked: "To whom?" He said: "By Allah, by His book, by His messenger, by the leaders of the believers, and by the common folk."

---

## Key Themes

1. **Comprehensive Nature of Nasiha**: النصيحة is not merely advice but encompasses sincerity, genuineness, and comprehensive well-wishing
2. **Five Categories of Nasiha**: Directed toward Allah, His book, His messenger, Muslim leaders, and the general public
3. **Religion as Service**: The hadith demonstrates that a significant portion of religious practice involves doing well by others
4. **Heart-Centered Faith**: Connection to previous hadith about the soundness of heart affecting all actions
5. **Prophetic Methodology**: The three responsibilities of messengers - teaching the book, wisdom (حكمة), and purification (تزكية)

## Explanation and Commentary

**The Nature of Nasiha:**
Imam Khattabi's explanation reveals that nasiha is a comprehensive concept encompassing multiple good measures for someone's benefit. Like the word فلاح (success of this world and next), it cannot be captured in a single English translation.

**The Five Categories of Nasiha:**

1. **For Allah (لله):**
   - Having faith in Allah
   - Rejecting shirk (associating partners)
   - Describing Allah with attributes of perfection
   - Loving and hating for Allah's sake
   - Reminding people of Allah's blessings
   - Promoting sincerity in all matters
   - *Reality: Benefits the person doing it, as Allah needs nothing from us*

2. **For His Book (لكتابه):**
   - Believing it is Allah's word and divine revelation
   - Recognizing its inimitability
   - Reciting it correctly with proper tajweed
   - Learning proper makharij (pronunciation points)
   - Defending against false interpretations
   - Following its commandments
   - Acting upon محكم verses and submitting to متشابه verses

3. **For His Messenger (لرسوله):**
   - Accepting the messenger's message
   - Obeying his commands and prohibitions
   - Supporting him against enemies
   - Respecting and venerating him
   - Propagating his Sunnah
   - Defending the Sunnah (even when it becomes difficult like "holding burning coal")

4. **For Muslim Leaders (لأئمة المسلمين):**
   - Helping them establish what is right
   - Obeying them in righteousness
   - Reminding them gently of their responsibilities
   - Avoiding violent rebellion
   - Making dua for their guidance and success

5. **For Common Folk (عامتهم):**
   - Guiding them toward their interests
   - Concealing their private faults
   - Enjoining good and forbidding evil
   - Respecting elders, showing mercy to youth
   - Abstaining from cheating, harmful jealousy
   - Loving for others what you love for yourself
   - Defending their property, honor, and rights

---

## Fiqh Rulings Derived

1. **Communal Obligation**: Nasiha is a فرض كفاية (collective obligation) - if sufficiently fulfilled by some, others are not individually obligated
2. **Proper Methodology**: Correction and guidance must be done with kindness, gentleness, and wisdom
3. **Private vs. Public Correction**: Faults should be addressed privately to preserve dignity
4. **Obedience to Leaders**: Must obey Muslim leaders in righteousness, while gently reminding them of their duties
5. **Tajweed Requirements**: Muslims must learn to recite Quran properly as part of doing well by the Book

---

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **Service Over Selfishness**: The ummah's decline is partly due to reliance on others and unwillingness to serve
2. **Kindness in Da'wah**: Harsh methods drive people away; gentleness attracts hearts (as demonstrated by the Prophet's ﷺ characteristics)
3. **Importance of Sunnah**: Holding to the Sunnah will become increasingly difficult but remains essential
4. **Comprehensive Faith**: Religion encompasses both worship of Allah and service to His creation
5. **Mercy Brings Mercy**: "Those who show mercy, Allah shows mercy to them. Show mercy to those on earth, and the One in the sky will show mercy to you"
6. **Service Leads to Success**: Example of the academically average student who served teachers and achieved worldly and religious success
7. **Taking Initiative**: Muslims must adopt the mindset of "we will do it" rather than waiting for others
