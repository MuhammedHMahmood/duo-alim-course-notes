## Session Overview

This session focused on completing the explanation of a comprehensive Hadith Qudsi about Allah's bounty (*Fadl Allah*) and introduced two additional hadiths on the virtue of dhikr (*Fadl al-Dhikr*) and the abundant paths to goodness (*Sunan al-Khayr*). The class emphasized Allah's infinite mercy, justice, and the various forms of worship and charity available to believers, including the spiritual reward found in permissible daily actions.

---

## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Main Hadith (Hadith Qudsi):**
Narrated by Abu Dharr Al-Ghifari (رضي الله تعالى عنه) from the Prophet (ﷺ) who said that Allah (ﷻ) says:

*"Ya 'ibadi, inni harramtu az-zulma 'ala nafsi wa ja'altuhu baynakum muharraman fala tazalamu..."*

**Second Hadith:**
Narrated by Abu Dharr (رضي الله تعالى عنه): A group of companions said to the Prophet (ﷺ): *"Ya Rasul Allah, dhahaba ahlu ad-duthur bil-ujur..."*

**Third Hadith:**
Narrated by Abu Hurayrah (رضي الله تعالى عنه): The Prophet (ﷺ) said: *"Kullu sulama min an-nas 'alayhi sadaqah..."*

## Key Themes

1. **Divine Justice**: Allah's self-imposed prohibition of oppression and command for human justice
2. **Human Dependence**: Complete reliance on Allah for guidance, sustenance, and clothing
3. **Divine Mercy and Forgiveness**: Allah's unlimited capacity to forgive and provide
4. **Infinite Divine Bounty**: Allah's limitless resources that cannot be diminished
5. **Accessible Worship**: Multiple forms of dhikr and charity available to all believers
6. **Reward in Permissible Actions**: Spiritual benefit in lawful fulfillment of desires
7. **Daily Charity Obligations**: 360 daily acts of charity corresponding to human joints

## Explanation and Commentary

**On Divine Justice:** Allah's self-prohibition of oppression demonstrates His perfect justice. This serves as a model for human conduct - believers must never oppress others, and those wronged have the right to appropriate compensation, though forgiveness is preferred.

**On Human Dependence:** The hadith emphasizes that everything humans possess - guidance, sustenance, clothing - originates from Allah. Even our ability to work and earn comes from Allah's enablement.

**On Divine Forgiveness:** Allah knows human weakness and provides continuous opportunity for forgiveness. The daily mistakes mentioned highlight the importance of regular istighfar (seeking forgiveness).

**On Divine Bounty:** The analogy of the needle in the ocean illustrates Allah's infinite resources. Even if all creation requested simultaneously, Allah's bounty would not diminish - this is merely an analogy for human understanding, as Allah transcends such comparisons.

**On Accessible Worship:** The Prophet (ﷺ) provided practical alternatives to monetary charity through dhikr formulas, making spiritual reward accessible to all economic classes.

**On Rewarded Permissible Actions:** Intimate relations within marriage carry reward when performed with proper intention, demonstrating Islam's balanced approach to human nature.

---

## Fiqh Rulings Derived

1. **Prohibition of Oppression**: Absolute prohibition of wronging others, with right to proportionate recompense
2. **Collective Obligation**: Enjoining good and forbidding evil (*amr bil-ma'ruf wa nahi 'an al-munkar*) is a communal responsibility
3. **Daily Charity Requirements**: 360 acts of charity daily, fulfilled through various means including dhikr
4. **Permissibility of Questioning**: Asking clarifying questions about religion is encouraged when seeking beneficial knowledge
5. **Reward in Lawful Intimacy**: Conjugal relations in marriage carry spiritual reward when performed with proper intention
6. **Multiple Forms of Charity**: Physical assistance, kind words, removing obstacles, and dhikr all constitute valid charity

---

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **Ultimate Dependence on Allah**: Recognition that all blessings, including iman (faith), originate from Allah and require daily gratitude
2. **Importance of Justice**: Following Allah's example of perfect justice in our dealings with others
3. **Accessibility of Worship**: Spiritual reward is available through simple daily actions and remembrance, not just formal worship
4. **Balance in Religion**: Islam provides guidance for all aspects of life, including physical relationships, within a framework of spiritual growth
5. **Daily Spiritual Accounting**: The concept of 360 daily charities encourages constant mindfulness of our actions and their spiritual implications
6. **Power of Intention**: Good intention transforms ordinary permissible actions into acts of worship
7. **Continuous Forgiveness**: Regular seeking of Allah's forgiveness acknowledges human fallibility while maintaining hope in divine mercy
