    Nahw 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-06-16

    Session Overview

    This session covered two main topics:

    1. Completion of Mustasna (Exception) chapter — specifically the I'rab of غَيْر (ghayr) and سِوَى (siwā), followed by a full class exercise on all seven Mustasna scenarios.
    2. Introduction to Majrūrāt (Section 3.9–3.10) — a brief review of the two foundational reasons for jarr, then launching into the five Tawābi' concepts, beginning with Na't Haqīqī (direct description) and its grammar rules, including when a sifah is a complete sentence.

    Na't Sababi (indirect description) was introduced conceptually but deferred to the next class.

    Grammar Rules Covered

    Rule 1: I'rāb of Ghayр (غَيْر)

    • غَيْر and سِوَى, when used as particles of exception, follow Rule 7 of the Mustasna table.
    • For all types of Mustasna (muttasil, munqati', mufarragh, ghayр mufarragh; mujab or ghayр mujab), the noun following غَيْر/سِوَى is always majrūr (because it is mudhāf ilayh of غَيْر).
    • سِوَى is mabnī (indeclinable) — it never changes; always سِوَى.
    • غَيْر is mu'rab (declinable) — it can take any of the three harakāt (ghayra / ghayru / ghayri), so its I'rāb must be determined.

    Two-step method to determine the I'rāb of غَيْر:

    Step 1: Replace غَيْر with إِلَّا. Identify which of the four Mustasna scenarios applies, and determine what I'rāb the Mustasna (the noun after إِلَّا) would receive.

    Step 2: Remove إِلَّا and restore غَيْر. Give غَيْر the I'rāb that the Mustasna would have had. The original noun after غَيْر becomes majrūr as mudhāf ilayh.

    Applied examples:

    Original Sentence Replace غَيْر with إِلَّا Scenario I'rāb of Mustasna I'rāb of غَيْر Final Form
    جَاءَ الْقَوْمُ غَيْرَ زَيْدٍ جَاءَ الْقَوْمُ إِلَّا زَيْدًا #1 (mujab, muttasil) Mansūb Mansūb → غَيْرَ جَاءَ الْقَوْمُ غَيْرَ زَيْدٍ
    مَا جَاءَ غَيْرُ زَيْدٍ مَا جَاءَ إِلَّا زَيْدٌ #4 (ghayр mujab, mufarragh) Marfū' (per 'āmil) Marfū' → غَيْرُ مَا جَاءَ غَيْرُ زَيْدٍ

    Rule 2: Two Foundational Reasons for Jarr (Section 3.9)

    Words are always majrūr for one of two reasons: 1. Preceded by a harf al-jarr — the noun following any harf al-jarr is majrūr (e.g., فِي الْكِتَابِ). 2. Mudhāf ilayh — the second noun in an idāfa construction is always majrūr (e.g., كِتَابُ زَيْدٍ).

    These are foundational and not re-explained in detail (covered extensively in earlier lessons).

    Rule 3: Tawābi' — Introduction (Section 3.10)

    Tābi' (تَابِع): the word that follows another word and matches it in I'rāb. Matbū' (مَتْبُوع): the word that is followed.

    The word that comes second follows the first in one or more of four categories: 1. I'rāb (case ending) 2. Being Ma'rifa or Nakira 3. Gender (masculine/feminine) 4. Number (singular/dual/plural)

    Five concepts fall under Tawābi': 1. Na't (نَعْت) — description ← covered today 2. 'Atf (عَطْف) 3. Tawkīd (تَوْكِيد) 4. Badal (بَدَل) 5. (fifth unnamed in this session)

    Rule 4: Terminology — Mawsūf/Sifah = Man'ūt/Na't

    Term Pair Role
    مَوْصُوف (Mawsūf) = مَنْعُوت (Man'ūt) The noun being described → is the matbū'
    صِفَة (Sifah) = نَعْت (Na't) The adjective/descriptor → is the tābi'

    In Na't Haqīqī, the sifah follows the mawsūf in all four of the above categories.

    Rule 5: Na't Haqīqī (نَعْت حَقِيقِي) — Direct Description

    • The sifah comes after the mawsūf.
    • They agree in four things: I'rāb, being ma'rifa/nakira, gender, and number.
    • Example: اَلْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ — al-kabīru agrees with al-bayt in all four.

    When the Sifah is a complete sentence:

    Two conditions apply: 1. The mawsūf must be nakira (no alif-lam). 2. The sentence functioning as sifah must contain a dhamīr (pronoun) that refers back to the nakira mawsūf.

    Example: جَاءَنِي وَلَدٌ يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ - وَلَدٌ = mawsūf (nakira, fā'il of جَاءَ) - يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ = sifah (complete verbal sentence; hidden dhamīr in يَرْكَبُ refers to وَلَد) - Translation: A boy who was riding the bicycle came to me.

    Contrast — when the noun is ma'rifa:

    جَاءَنِي الْوَلَدُ يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ - الْوَلَدُ is ma'rifa → يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ cannot be sifah - يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ becomes Hāl; الْوَلَدُ becomes Dhul-hāl - Translation: The boy came to me while riding the bicycle.

    When ma'rifa is followed by a sentence — two possibilities: | Preceding noun | Sentence becomes | Example | |---|---|---| | Ma'rifa (with alif-lam) | Khabar | اَلْوَلَدُ يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ (The boy is riding...) | | Ma'rifa (with alif-lam) | Hāl | جَاءَ الْوَلَدُ يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ (The boy came while riding...) | | Nakira | Sifah | جَاءَنِي وَلَدٌ يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ (A boy who was riding came...) |

    Rule 6: Na't with a Jumlah Ismiyyah as Sifah

    جَاءَ رَجُلٌ أَبُوهُ عَالِمٌ - رَجُلٌ = mawsūf (nakira) — fā'il of جَاءَ together with its sifah - أَبُوهُ عَالِمٌ = sifah (complete nominal sentence) - أَبُوهُ: أَبُو = mudhāf, هُ = mudhāf ilayh → together = mubtada' - عَالِمٌ = khabar - هُ in أَبُوهُ is the dhamīr referring back to رَجُل (satisfying the dhamīr rule) - Translation: A man whose father is a scholar came.

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    مُسْتَثْنَى (Mustathna) The excluded noun in an exception construction
    مُسْتَثْنَى مِنْهُ (Mustasna minh) The group from which exclusion is made
    غَيْر (Ghayr) Except / other than (declinable particle of exception)
    سِوَى (Siwā) Except / other than (indeclinable particle of exception)
    مَبْنِي (Mabnī) Indeclinable — form never changes
    مُعْرَب (Mu'rab) Declinable — form changes with I'rāb
    مُضَاف (Mudhāf) First noun in an idāfa (construct)
    مُضَاف إِلَيْهِ (Mudhāf ilayhi) Second noun in an idāfa — always majrūr
    مَجْرُور (Majrūr) Noun in the jarr/genitive case
    مَجْرُورَات (Majrūrāt) Words that are always in the jarr case (Section 3.9)
    تَوَابِع (Tawābi') Followers — words that follow another in I'rāb (umbrella term)
    تَابِع (Tābi') The follower (the word that comes second)
    مَتْبُوع (Matbū') The followed word (the word that comes first)
    نَعْت (Na't) Description / adjective (synonym of Sifah)
    مَنْعُوت (Man'ūt) The described noun (synonym of Mawsūf)
    نَعْت حَقِيقِي (Na't Haqīqī) Direct description — adjective directly describes the noun
    نَعْت سَبَبِي (Na't Sababi) Indirect description — to be covered next class
    مَوْصُوف (Mawsūf) The described noun (matbū' in na't)
    صِفَة (Sifah) The adjective/descriptor (tābi' in na't)
    ضَمِير (Dhamīr) Pronoun
    ضَمِير مُسْتَتِر (Dhamīr Mustatir) Hidden/implied pronoun
    حَال (Hāl) Circumstantial descriptor — describes the state
    ذُو الْحَال (Dhul-Hāl) The noun whose state is being described
    جُمْلَة فِعْلِيَّة (Jumlah Fi'liyya) Verbal sentence
    جُمْلَة اسْمِيَّة (Jumlah Ismiyya) Nominal sentence
    مُفَرَّغ (Mufarragh) Emptied/incomplete — negative sentence with no Mustasna minh stated
    مُجَاب (Mujab) Affirmative/positive sentence
    غَيْر مُجَاب (Ghayр Mujab) Negative sentence
    عَامِل (Āmil) The governing word that determines I'rāb

    I'rāb (Sentence Analysis) Examples

    Example 1: جَاءَ الْقَوْمُ غَيْرَ زَيْدٍ

    (The people came except Zayd)

    Word I'rāb Reason
    جَاءَ Fi'l mādhī Verb
    الْقَوْمُ Fā'il — marfū' Subject of جَاءَ
    غَيْرَ Mustathna — mansūb Replaces إِلَّا in Scenario #1 (mujab, muttasil); gets the nasb the mustathna would have received
    زَيْدٍ Mudhāf ilayhi — majrūr Is the mudhāf ilayhi of غَيْر

    Example 2: مَا جَاءَ غَيْرُ زَيْدٍ

    (No one came except Zayd)

    Word I'rāb Reason
    مَا Harf nafy Negation particle
    جَاءَ Fi'l mādhī Verb
    غَيْرُ Fā'il — marfū' Scenario #4 (ghayр mujab, mufarragh); the 'āmil جَاءَ requires rafa' for its fā'il
    زَيْدٍ Mudhāf ilayhi — majrūr Mudhāf ilayhi of غَيْر

    Example 3: جَاءَنِي وَلَدٌ يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ

    (A boy who was riding the bicycle came to me)

    Word I'rāb Reason
    جَاءَ Fi'l mādhī Verb
    نِ (nī) Maf'ūl bihi — majrūr Object (came to me)
    وَلَدٌ Mawsūf + Fā'il — marfū' Nakira noun; fā'il of جَاءَ together with its sifah
    يَرْكَبُ Fi'l mudhāri' (sifah sentence) Fā'il = dhamīr mustatir (هُو) referring to وَلَد
    الدَّرَّاجَةَ Maf'ūl bihi — mansūb Object of يَرْكَبُ
    يَرْكَبُ الدَّرَّاجَةَ Sifah (jumlah fi'liyya) Sentence describes the nakira وَلَدٌ

    Example 4: جَاءَ رَجُلٌ أَبُوهُ عَالِمٌ

    (A man whose father is a scholar came)

    Word I'rāb Reason
    جَاءَ Fi'l mādhī Verb
    رَجُلٌ Mawsūf + Fā'il — marfū' Nakira; fā'il of جَاءَ (with its sifah sentence)
    أَبُوهُ Mubtada' — marfū' (mudhāf + mudhāf ilayhi) أَبُو = mudhāf; هُ = mudhāf ilayhi referring to رَجُل
    عَالِمٌ Khabar — marfū' Completes the nominal sifah sentence
    أَبُوهُ عَالِمٌ Sifah (jumlah ismiyya) Nominal sentence describing the nakira رَجُلٌ; contains dhamīr هُ referring to مَوْصُوف

    Example 5 (Class Exercise): مَا عَادَ الْمَرِيضَ عَائِدٌ غَيْرَ الطَّبِيبِ

    (No visitor visited the patient except the doctor)

    Word I'rāb Reason
    مَا Harf nafy Negation
    عَادَ Fi'l mādhī Verb
    الْمَرِيضَ Maf'ūl bihi — mansūb Patient = object (comes before fā'il here)
    عَائِدٌ Fā'il — marfū' Visitor = subject; nakira after نَفْي = generalization ("no visitor")
    غَيْرَ Mustathna — mansūb Scenario #7 (ghayр mujab, muttasil with mustasnā minh implied); ghayر takes nasb
    الطَّبِيبِ Mudhāf ilayhi — majrūr Mudhāf ilayhi of غَيْر

    مَا جَاءَ إِلَّا الْأُسْتَاذُ No one came except the teacher #4 (ghayр mujab, mufarragh) Marfū' (per 'āmil)

    Key Takeaways

    1. I'rāb of غَيْر is determined by substitution: Replace غَيْر with إِلَّا, identify the Mustasna scenario, give that I'rāb to غَيْر, and make the following noun majrūr as mudhāf ilayhi.

    2. سِوَى never changes because it is mabnī; غَيْر changes because it is mu'rab — this is why only غَيْر's I'rāb needs to be determined.

    3. Tawābi' is the umbrella for five concepts — tābi' (the follower) matches the matbū' (the followed) in I'rāb and potentially in ma'rifa/nakira, gender, and number.

    4. Mawsūf = Man'ūt; Sifah = Na't — these are synonymous term pairs. The teacher recommends sticking with mawsūf and sifah for clarity.

    5. Na't Haqīqī (direct description): The sifah directly describes the mawsūf and agrees with it in all four categories.

    6. When the sifah is a sentence: Two strict rules apply — (a) the mawsūf must be nakira, and (b) the sentence must contain a dhamīr referring back to the mawsūf.

    7. Three possible roles for a sentence following a noun:

    8. Follows ma'rifa + linked by copula → Khabar
    9. Follows ma'rifa + after a verb → Hāl (with dhul-hāl)

    10. Follows nakira → Sifah (na't)

    11. Next class: Na't Sababi (indirect description) — read the section in advance as it is more technical. Exercise on identifying Na't Haqīqī vs. Na't Sababi.
