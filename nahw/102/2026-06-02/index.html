    Nahw 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-06-02

    Session Overview

    This session focused on the rules of Arabic numbers (Al-Adad wal-Ma'dud) — the grammatical relationship between a number and the noun being counted. The teacher began by reviewing homework (memorizing numbers 1–100 in Arabic) and testing several students. The bulk of the class covered the distinct grammatical rules for numbers 1–2, 3–10, 11–12, and 13–19, with Quranic examples throughout.

    Key terms established at the outset: - Adad (العدد) — the number itself - Ma'dud (المعدود) — the noun/object being counted

    Grammar Rules Covered

    Rule Set 1: Numbers 1 and 2 (Wahid and Ithnan)

    • Adad (1 or 2) acts as Sifah (صفة) — a descriptive adjective
    • Ma'dud acts as Mausuf (موصوف) — the described noun
    • Mausuf comes first, Sifah comes second (same as standard sifah-mausuf structure)
    • They agree in four things: number, gender, ma'rifah/nakirah, and i'rab
    • In most cases, the number is not needed — a singular noun implies "one" and a dual noun implies "two"; use the number only for emphasis
    • When kitabun → al-kitabu, both mausuf and sifah take al: al-kitabu al-wahidu
    Case Muzakkar Mu'annas
    Raf' كِتَابٌ وَاحِدٌ سَيَّارَةٌ وَاحِدَةٌ
    Nasb كِتَاباً وَاحِداً سَيَّارَةً وَاحِدَةً
    Jarr كِتَابٍ وَاحِدٍ سَيَّارَةٍ وَاحِدَةٍ

    For two (tasniyah), the dual form of the noun is sufficient on its own: - كِتَابَانِ (Raf') / كِتَابَيْنِ (Nasb/Jarr) — "two books" (without needing إثنان unless emphasizing)

    Rule Set 2: Numbers 3 to 10

    • Adad acts as Mudaf (مضاف); Ma'dud acts as Mudaf Ilayh (مضاف إليه)
    • Adad comes first (reversed from 1–2)
    • Ma'dud is always plural and majroor (because mudaf ilayh is always majroor)
    • Adad does not accept alif-lam or tanwin (because it is mudaf)
    • Adad can take all three harakaat depending on its position in the sentence
    • CRITICAL RULE: Adad and Ma'dud do NOT agree in gender — they are opposite
    • If ma'dud is muzakkar → adad is mu'annas
    • If ma'dud is mu'annas → adad is muzakkar
    • Gender of ma'dud is determined by looking at its singular form
    Number Muzakkar Ma'dud (books) Mu'annas Ma'dud (cars)
    3 ثَلَاثَةُ كُتُبٍ ثَلَاثُ سَيَّارَاتٍ
    4 أَرْبَعَةُ كُتُبٍ أَرْبَعُ سَيَّارَاتٍ
    5 خَمْسَةُ أَقْلَامٍ خَمْسُ سَيَّارَاتٍ
    6 سِتَّةُ أَقْلَامٍ —
    7 سَبْعَةُ أَقْلَامٍ —
    8 ثَمَانِيَةُ أَقْلَامٍ —
    9 تِسْعَةُ أَقْلَامٍ —
    10 عَشَرَةُ أَقْلَامٍ —

    Quranic references: - أَرْبَعَةُ أَشْهُرٍ — "four months" (Ash-hur = plural of Shahr, muzakkar → adad is mu'annas: أَرْبَعَةُ) - سَبْعَةٌ إِذَا رَجَعْتُمْ — "seven [days of fasting] when you return" (Siyam implied, Surah Al-Baqarah) - عَلَيْهَا تِسْعَةَ عَشَرَ — "over it are nineteen [angels]" (Surah Al-Muddaththir)

    Rule Set 3: Number 11 (Ahada Asharah / Ihda Asharata)

    Forms:

    Gender Form
    Muzakkar أَحَدَ عَشَرَ
    Mu'annas إِحْدَى عَشَرَةَ

    Rules: - Adad comes first, Ma'dud comes second - Ma'dud is singular and mansoob (Nasb case) - Adad and Ma'dud agree in gender - Both parts of the adad are mabni (indeclinable) — they do not change regardless of sentence position - Ma'dud from 11 onwards is always singular (unlike 3–10 where it is plural)

    Examples: - أَحَدَ عَشَرَ كِتَاباً — eleven books - إِحْدَى عَشَرَةَ سَيَّارَةً — eleven cars

    Rule Set 4: Number 12 (Ithna Asharah / Ithnataa Asharata)

    Origin: Originally إثْنَانِ عَشَرَ / إثْنَتَانِ عَشَرَةَ — the noon of tasniyah is dropped.

    Forms:

    Case Muzakkar Mu'annas
    Raf' اثْنَا عَشَرَ اثْنَتَا عَشَرَةَ
    Nasb/Jarr اثْنَيْ عَشَرَ اثْنَتَيْ عَشَرَةَ

    Rules: - Unlike 11, the first part of 12 is NOT mabni — it changes in i'rab (follows dual declension: alif in raf', ya in nasb/jarr) - The sheen of عَشَرَ has fathah for muzakkar; sukoon for mu'annas (عَشْرَة) - Ma'dud is singular and mansoob - Adad and Ma'dud agree in gender

    Examples: - اثْنَا عَشَرَ كِتَاباً (Raf') → اثْنَيْ عَشَرَ كِتَاباً (Nasb/Jarr) - اثْنَتَا عَشَرَةَ سَيَّارَةً (Raf') → اثْنَتَيْ عَشَرَةَ سَيَّارَةً (Nasb/Jarr)

    Rule Set 5: Numbers 13 to 19

    Forms: Numbers are compound — two parts (e.g., ثَلَاثَةَ عَشَرَ = thalatha + ashara)

    Rules: - Adad comes first, Ma'dud comes second - Ma'dud is singular and mansoob - First part of adad disagrees with ma'dud in gender - Second part of adad agrees with ma'dud in gender - Ma'dud muzakkar → first part mu'annas (has ta'), second part (عَشَرَ) muzakkar (sheen with fathah) - Ma'dud mu'annas → first part muzakkar (no ta'), second part (عَشْرَةَ) mu'annas (sheen with sukoon)

    Number Muzakkar Ma'dud Mu'annas Ma'dud
    13 ثَلَاثَةَ عَشَرَ كِتَاباً ثَلَاثَ عَشَرَةَ سَيَّارَةً
    14 أَرْبَعَةَ عَشَرَ قَلَماً أَرْبَعَ عَشَرَةَ بِنْتاً
    15 خَمْسَةَ عَشَرَ وَلَداً خَمْسَ عَشَرَةَ بِنْتاً
    16 سِتَّةَ عَشَرَ وَلَداً —
    17 سَبْعَةَ عَشَرَ غُرْفَةً —
    18 ثَمَانِيَةَ عَشَرَ شُبَّاكاً —
    19 تِسْعَةَ عَشَرَ مَلَكاً —

    Quranic example: عَلَيْهَا تِسْعَةَ عَشَرَ (Surah Al-Muddaththir) — "over it are nineteen [angels]" — Malak (angel) is implied and muzakkar, so تِسْعَةَ (mu'annas/disagrees) and عَشَرَ (muzakkar/agrees).

    I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

    Example 1: كِتَابٌ وَاحِدٌ - كِتَابٌ — Mausuf, mubtada or subject, marfu', muzakkar, nakirah - وَاحِدٌ — Sifah, agrees with mausuf in raf', singular, muzakkar, nakirah

    Example 2: ثَلَاثَةُ كُتُبٍ - ثَلَاثَةُ — Adad, mudaf, marfu' (no tanwin, no alif-lam), mu'annas (ta' marbuta present), opposite of ma'dud - كُتُبٍ — Ma'dud, mudaf ilayh, majroor (tanwin kasra), plural of كِتَاب, muzakkar

    Example 3: أَحَدَ عَشَرَ كِتَاباً - أَحَدَ عَشَرَ — Adad, mabni (indeclinable), muzakkar, agrees with ma'dud - كِتَاباً — Ma'dud, mansoob (fathah tanwin), singular

    Example 4: اثْنَيْ عَشَرَ كِتَاباً (in Nasb/Jarr) - اثْنَيْ — First part of adad, ya replaces alif because nasb/jarr (follows dual declension), muzakkar - عَشَرَ — Second part, mabni, sheen with fathah (muzakkar) - كِتَاباً — Ma'dud, mansoob, singular

    Example 5: ثَلَاثَةَ عَشَرَ كِتَاباً - ثَلَاثَةَ — First part of adad, mu'annas (disagrees with muzakkar ma'dud), mansoob - عَشَرَ — Second part, muzakkar (agrees with muzakkar ma'dud), sheen with fathah - كِتَاباً — Ma'dud, singular, mansoob

    Example 6: ثَلَاثَ عَشَرَةَ سَيَّارَةً - ثَلَاثَ — First part of adad, muzakkar/no ta' (disagrees with mu'annas ma'dud) - عَشَرَةَ — Second part, mu'annas (agrees with mu'annas ma'dud), sheen with sukoon - سَيَّارَةً — Ma'dud, singular, mansoob

    Practice Examples

    Numbers 1–2: - قَلَمٌ وَاحِدٌ — one pen - سَيَّارَةٌ وَاحِدَةٌ — one car - قَلَمَانِ — two pens (dual form sufficient) - قَلَمَانِ اثْنَانِ — two pens (emphatic)

    Numbers 3–10 (opposite gender rule): - ثَلَاثَةُ أَقْلَامٍ — three pens (Qalam = muzakkar → ثَلَاثَة mu'annas) - أَرْبَعَةُ أَقْلَامٍ — four pens - خَمْسَةُ أَقْلَامٍ — five pens - ثَلَاثُ سَيَّارَاتٍ — three cars (Sayyara = mu'annas → ثَلَاث muzakkar)

    Numbers 11–12: - أَحَدَ عَشَرَ كِتَاباً — eleven books - إِحْدَى عَشَرَةَ سَيَّارَةً — eleven cars - اثْنَا عَشَرَ كِتَاباً — twelve books (Raf') - اثْنَتَيْ عَشَرَةَ سَيَّارَةً — twelve cars (Nasb/Jarr)

    Numbers 13–19: - خَمْسَةَ عَشَرَ وَلَداً — fifteen boys (walad = muzakkar) - خَمْسَ عَشَرَةَ بِنْتاً — fifteen girls (bint = mu'annas) - ثَمَانِيَةَ عَشَرَ شُبَّاكاً — eighteen windows (shubak = muzakkar) - تِسْعَةَ عَشَرَ مَلَكاً — nineteen angels (malak = muzakkar; Quranic reference: عَلَيْهَا تِسْعَةَ عَشَرَ)

    Key Takeaways

    1. The two core terms: Adad (number) and Ma'dud (counted noun) — master these before anything else.

    2. Numbers 1–2 follow sifah-mausuf rules: Ma'dud comes first, adad comes second; they agree in all four things (number, gender, ma'rifah/nakirah, i'rab). These numbers are usually omitted unless emphasis is needed.

    3. Numbers 3–10 follow mudaf-mudaf ilayh rules: Adad comes first (as mudaf), ma'dud is plural and majroor (as mudaf ilayh). The most critical rule: adad and ma'dud are opposite in gender.

    4. From 11 onwards, ma'dud is always singular — a key contrast with English where counted nouns are always plural above one.

    5. From 11 onwards, ma'dud is mansoob (Nasb case).

    6. Numbers 11–12: adad and ma'dud agree in gender. Number 11 is fully mabni; number 12 partially declines (follows dual pattern — alif in Raf', ya in Nasb/Jarr).

    7. Numbers 13–19: Compound adad — the first part disagrees with ma'dud in gender; the second part agrees. The sheen of عَشَر has fathah with muzakkar and sukoon with mu'annas.

    8. Practical shortcut: To identify gender, always look at the singular form of the noun, not the plural.

    9. Next class will cover: Rules for 20, 30 ... 90; compound numbers 21–22, 31–32 ... 91–92; 23–29, 33–39 ... 93–99; and 100 and beyond (105, 110, etc.). Continue memorizing the number sequence 1–100 before next class (minimum 100 repetitions of 1–30 to achieve fluency).
