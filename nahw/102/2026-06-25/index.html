    Nahw 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-06-25

    Session Overview

    This session covered the concept of Ta'kid (التأكيد) — emphasis in Arabic grammar. Ta'kid is a type of Tabi' (a word that follows another), falling under the broader umbrella of Tawabi'. The class distinguished between two major types of ta'kid — lafzi (verbal/repetition-based) and ma'nawi (meaning-based) — and explored the specific words used for ma'nawi emphasis in detail. The session concluded with extensive sentence analysis exercises applying all concepts learned.

    Previous topic: Na't Sababi (the final discussion on it was completed last class).

    Next topic: Al-Badl (3.10.3) — to be covered Monday.

    Homework: Exercises on pages 104 and 107 (page 108 is minimal).

    Grammar Rules Covered

    1. Definition of Ta'kid (التأكيد)

    • Ta'kid is a Tabi' (follower) that either:
    • Emphasizes the Matbu' (the word being followed) in the matter related to it, or
    • Emphasizes the inclusion of all members of the Matbu' in the matter related to it.
    • The word doing the emphasizing = Ta'kid (تأكيد) → it is the Tabi'
    • The word being emphasized = Mu'akkad (مؤكد) → it is the Matbu'
    • Ta'kid always follows the Mu'akkad in i'rab (grammatical case).

    2. Types of Ta'kid

    A. Ta'kid Lafzi (التأكيد اللفظي) — Verbal/Repetition Emphasis

    • Emphasis is achieved by repeating the Mu'akkad.
    • The repeated word can be: a noun, a verb, a harf (particle), or a dhamir (pronoun).
    • Rule for repeating a Dhamir: If the Mu'akkad is a Dhamir Muttasil (attached pronoun) or Mustatir (hidden pronoun), you must use a Dhamir Munfasil (detached pronoun) for the Ta'kid — you cannot repeat two attached pronouns back to back.
    Mu'akkad Type Example Notes
    Noun جاء زيدٌ زيدٌ Repeat the noun
    Verb غاب الغائبُ فحضر Repeat the verb
    Harf لا لا أنقضُ العهدَ Repeat the particle
    Dhamir Muttasil افتح أنتَ النافذةَ Use Dhamir Munfasil instead of repeating attached dhamir

    B. Ta'kid Ma'nawi (التأكيد المعنوي) — Semantic/Meaning-Based Emphasis

    Emphasis through specific designated words. Divided into four groups:

    Group 1: نَفْس (Nafs) and عَيْن (Ayn) — "himself / herself / itself"

    Rules: 1. Can be used for singular, dual, or plural Mu'akkad. 2. Must always be used as Mudaf (first term of a genitive construction). 3. The Mudaf Ilayh must be a Dhamir (pronoun). 4. The Dhamir must agree with the Mu'akkad in gender and number. 5. Exception: The plural forms (أنفس / أعيُن) are used for both dual AND plural Mu'akkad — the dual form (نَفْسَانِ / عَيْنَانِ) is not used. The distinction between dual vs. plural Mu'akkad is shown only through the Dhamir.

    Mu'akkad Ta'kid Form Example
    Singular (m) نَفْسُهُ / عَيْنُهُ قام زيدٌ نفسُه
    Singular (f) نَفْسُهَا / عَيْنُهَا جاءت المعلمةُ عينُها
    Dual (m) أنفسُهُمَا / أعيُنُهُمَا قام الزيدان أنفسُهُمَا
    Dual (f) أنفسُهُمَا / أعيُنُهُمَا جاءت المعلمتان أعيُنُهُمَا
    Plural (m) أنفسُهُمْ / أعيُنُهُمْ قام الزيدون أنفسُهُمْ
    Plural (f) أنفسُهُنَّ / أعيُنُهُنَّ جاءت المعلمات أعيُنُهُنَّ
    Group 2: كِلا (Kila) and كِلْتَا (Kilta) — "both"

    Rules: 1. Can only be used for dual Mu'akkad — "both" = exactly two. 2. Originally: كِلانِ (m) and كِلْتَانِ (f) — Tathniya forms. 3. Always used as Mudaf → noon of Tathniya is dropped (standard rule when Tathniya is Mudaf). 4. Therefore: كِلانِ → كِلا and كِلْتَانِ → كِلْتَا. 5. Must be Mudaf to a dual Dhamir (هُمَا for third person, كُمَا for second person, نَا for first person). 6. كِلا = masculine | كِلْتَا = feminine. 7. In Rafa': كِلاهُمَا / كِلْتَاهُمَا; in Nasb/Jarr: كِلَيْهِمَا / كِلْتَيْهِمَا (ya replaces alif).

    Gender Rafa' Nasb/Jarr
    Masculine كِلاهُمَا كِلَيْهِمَا
    Feminine كِلْتَاهُمَا كِلْتَيْهِمَا

    Examples: - قام الزيدان كِلاهُمَا — Both Zaids stood. - قامت المعلمتان كِلْتَاهُمَا — Both female teachers stood.

    Group 3: كُلّ (Kull), جَمِيع (Jami'), and أجْمَع (Ajma') — "all / entire"

    General rule: Can be used for singular and plural (not restricted to dual). Emphasis on completeness/totality.

    Rules for كُلّ and جَمِيع: - Must be Mudaf to a Dhamir that agrees with the Mu'akkad in number and gender. - Singular Mu'akkad → كُلُّهُ / جَمِيعُهُ - Plural Mu'akkad → كُلُّهُمْ / جَمِيعُهُمْ

    Rules for أجْمَع (and its feminine جَمْعَاء): - For singular: follows the same rule as كُلّ/جَمِيع → must be Mudaf to a Dhamir (e.g., قرأتُ الكتابَ أجمعَهُ). - For plural: do not use a Dhamir. Instead, convert أجمع into Jama' Muzakkar Salim by adding واو/نون → أجمعون (m) / جُمَع (f). - Important restriction: كُلّ, جَمِيع, and أجمع can only be used for things that have parts or can be divided — not for indivisible entities (e.g., you cannot say اللهُ كُلُّهُ — incorrect).

    Mu'akkad كُلّ form جَمِيع form أجمع form
    Singular كُلُّهُ جَمِيعُهُ أجمعَهُ
    Plural (m) كُلُّهُمْ جَمِيعُهُمْ أجمعون
    Plural (f) كُلُّهُنَّ جَمِيعُهُنَّ جُمَع

    Examples: - قرأتُ الكتابَ كُلَّهُ — I read the entire book. - جاء الرجالُ كُلُّهُمْ — All the men came. - جاء الناسُ أجمعون — All the people came. - فَسجد الملائكةُ كُلُّهُمْ أجمعون — All the angels prostrated.

    Group 4: أكْتَع / أبْتَع / أبْصَع (and feminines كُتَع / بُتَع / بُصَع) — Additional Emphasis
    • Used after أجمعون / جُمَع for extra/intensified emphasis.
    • Only come after أجمع — they are not used alone.
    • For plural masculine: أكتعون / أبتعون / أبصعون
    • For plural feminine: كُتَع / بُتَع / بُصَع

    Examples: - جاء القومُ أجمعون أكتعون — All the people came (emphatic). - قامت النساءُ جُمَعُ كُتَعُ — All the women stood (emphatic).

    3. Multiple Ta'kid in One Sentence

    It is permissible to use two or more Ta'kid words for a single Mu'akkad:

    جاء القومُ كُلُّهُمْ أجمعون - Al-Qawmu = Mu'akkad - كُلُّهُمْ = Ta'kid #1 - أجمعون = Ta'kid #2 - All three together = Fa'il of جاء

    4. I'rab Position of Ta'kid

    Ta'kid always follows its Mu'akkad in i'rab case: - Mu'akkad in Rafa' → Ta'kid in Rafa' - Mu'akkad in Nasb → Ta'kid in Nasb (e.g., نفسَهُ، كُلَّهُ) - Mu'akkad in Jarr → Ta'kid in Jarr (e.g., نفسِهِ، كُلِّهِ)

    I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

    Example 1: قام زيدٌ نفسُهُ / عينُهُ

    Zayd himself stood. - قام — Fi'l (past tense verb) - زيدٌ — Mu'akkad → Fa'il (subject), marfu', tanwin damm - نفسُهُ — Ta'kid (of Zayd); Nafs = Mudaf, Hu = Mudaf Ilayh (refers to Zayd: masculine singular); marfu' following the Mu'akkad

    Example 2: جاء القومُ كُلُّهُمْ أجمعون

    All the people came. - جاء — Fi'l (Jumlah Fi'liyyah Khabariyyah) - القومُ — Mu'akkad - كُلُّهُمْ — Ta'kid #1; Kull = Mudaf, Hum = Mudaf Ilayh (plural, agreeing with al-Qawm) - أجمعون — Ta'kid #2; Jama' Muzakkar Salim form because Mu'akkad is plural - Al-Qawmu + كُلُّهُمْ + أجمعون together = Fa'il of جاء

    Example 3: فسجد الملائكةُ كُلُّهُمْ أجمعون إلا إبليسَ

    All the angels prostrated except Iblis. - سجد — Fi'l - الملائكةُ — Mu'akkad - كُلُّهُمْ — Ta'kid #1 - أجمعون — Ta'kid #2 - إلا — Harf al-Istithna' - إبليسَ — Mustathna (منصوب) - الملائكةُ + كُلُّهُمْ + أجمعون = Mustathna Minh - The full combination (Mustathna Minh + إلا + Mustathna) = Fa'il of سجد

    Example 4: إيَّاكَ إيَّاكَ والنَّمِيمةَ

    Beware! Beware of gossip. - Hidden Fi'l + Hidden Fa'il (implied "I warn you") - إيَّاكَ (first) — Mu'akkad → Maf'ul Awwal (first object) - إيَّاكَ (second) — Ta'kid Lafzi (repetition) - والنَّمِيمةَ — Second Maf'ul (واو = 'atf or addition) - Both إيَّاكَ (Mu'akkad + Ta'kid) and النميمة are Maf'ul Bih to the hidden verb

    Example 5: غَرَبَتْ غَرَبَتِ الشمسُ

    The sun has indeed set. - غَرَبَتْ (first) — Fi'l → Mu'akkad - غَرَبَتِ (second) — Fi'l → Ta'kid Lafzi (verbal repetition) - الشمسُ — Fa'il for both (for the combined Mu'akkad + Ta'kid)

    Example 6: قَطَعْنَا نَحْنُ أنفسَنَا الطريقَ كُلَّهُ

    We ourselves covered the entire path. - قَطَعْنَا — Fi'l + Dhamir (implied Fa'il "we") - نَحْنُ — Ta'kid Lafzi #1 (Dhamir Munfasil repeating the hidden نا) - أنفسَنَا — Ta'kid Ma'nawi #2 (Anfus = Mudaf, Na = Mudaf Ilayh referring to "we") - نحنُ + أنفسَنا together = Fa'il (Ta'kid of the implied Fa'il in قطعنا) - الطريقَ — Mu'akkad → Maf'ul Bih - كُلَّهُ — Ta'kid #3 of الطريق (Kull = Mudaf, Hu = Mudaf Ilayh) - الطريقَ + كُلَّهُ = Maf'ul Bih of قطعنا

    Example 7: المُلكُ كُلُّهُ لله

    All sovereignty belongs to Allah. - المُلكُ — Mu'akkad → Mubtada' - كُلُّهُ — Ta'kid (Mudaf + Mudaf Ilayh); follows Mubtada' in Rafa' - المُلكُ + كُلُّهُ together = Mubtada' - لِلَّهِ — Khabar (predicate)

    Key Takeaways

    1. Ta'kid is a Tabi' — it always follows the Mu'akkad in grammatical case (i'rab). This is the unifying principle across all types.

    2. Ta'kid Lafzi = repetition. Just repeat the word — noun, verb, harf, or dhamir. If repeating a Dhamir Muttasil/Mustatir, use a Dhamir Munfasil instead.

    3. Ta'kid Ma'nawi uses four groups of special words:

    4. نفس / عين (himself/itself) — Mudaf + Dhamir; use plural form (أنفس/أعيُن) for both dual and plural Mu'akkad; singular form for singular.
    5. كِلا / كِلْتَا (both) — dual only; always Mudaf + dual Dhamir; noon dropped because always Mudaf.
    6. كُلّ / جَمِيع / أجمع (all/entire) — for divisible things only; كُلّ and جَمِيع always take a Dhamir; أجمع takes a Dhamir for singular but becomes Jama' Muzakkar Salim (أجمعون/جُمَع) for plural.

    7. أكتع / أبتع / أبصع (and feminines) — only used after أجمعون/جُمَع for extra emphasis.

    8. The Dhamir in Ma'nawi Ta'kid always refers back to the Mu'akkad and must agree with it in gender and number — this is how you decode what the Mu'akkad is.

    9. كُلّ, جَمِيع, and أجمع cannot be used with indivisible things — do not use them to refer to Allah, a person (as a person), or anything that cannot be divided into parts.

    10. Multiple Ta'kid words can be stacked on a single Mu'akkad (e.g., كُلُّهُمْ أجمعون أكتعون) for increasing levels of emphasis.

    11. حَذَارِ is an Ism al-Fi'l, not a regular noun — it functions like a verb and governs its context accordingly.

    12. When analyzing complex sentences with Ta'kid + Istithna', identify layers: Mu'akkad → Ta'kid(s) → Mustathna Minh (all together) → إلا → Mustathna → then the whole unit fills its syntactic role (Fa'il, Maf'ul, etc.).
