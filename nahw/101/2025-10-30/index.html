    Nahw 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-10-30

    Session Overview

    This session focused on lesson 7: al-fi'l al-mudari (present and future tense verb). The class began with extensive practice of fi'l mudari conjugation patterns, with each student reciting the 14 conjugation forms (sighas). The instructor emphasized the critical importance of memorizing these conjugations perfectly, stating that students should practice them at least 100 times. The lesson covered the formation rules of fi'l mudari, including the four letters of mudari'ah (ya, ta, hamza, noon) and how to convert past tense verbs into present/future forms.

    Grammar Rules Covered

    Formation of Fi'l Mudari (Present/Future Tense)

    1. Four Letters of Mudari'ah (Huruful Mudari'ah):
    2. Ya (ي) - used in 4 positions
    3. Ta (ت) - used in 8 positions
    4. Hamza (أ) - used in 1 position

    5. Noon (ن) - used in 1 position

    6. Conversion from Madhi to Mudari:

    7. Add one of the four letters (ya, ta, hamza, noon) to the beginning
    8. Fa' kalimah becomes sakin (has sukoon)
    9. Ayn kalimah can have fathah, dhamma, or kasrah depending on the verb

    10. Lam kalimah receives dhamma in basic form

    11. Pattern Distribution:

    12. Ya: First 3 sighas (3rd person masculine) + 6th sigha (3rd person plural feminine)
    13. Ta: Sighas 4-5 (3rd person feminine singular/dual) + sighas 7-12 (2nd person)
    14. Hamza: Sigha 13 (1st person singular)

    15. Noon: Sigha 14 (1st person plural)

    16. Dual and Plural Formation:

    17. Dual (tathniyah): Add alif + noon al-wiqayah
    18. Plural masculine (jama' mudhakkar): Add waw + noon al-wiqayah
    19. Plural feminine (jama' mu'annas): Add noon al-niswa only

    Practice Examples

    Conjugation Practice Verbs Used:

    • yaktubu (he writes) - yaktubu, yaktubani, yaktubuna, taktubu, taktubani, yaktubna, taktubu, taktubani, taktubuna, taktubina, taktubani, taktubna, aktubu, naktubu

    • yansuru (he helps) - yansuru, yansurani, yansuruna, tansuru, tansurani, yansurna, tansuru, tansurani, tansuruna, tansurina, tansurani, tansurna, ansuru, nansuru

    • yal'abu (he plays) - yal'abu, yal'abani, yal'abuna, tal'abu, tal'abani, yal'abna, tal'abu, tal'abani, tal'abuna, tal'abina, tal'abani, tal'abna, al'abu, nal'abu

    Key Pattern Examples:

    • Basic form: yaf'alu (he does)
    • Dual: yaf'alani (they two do)
    • Plural: yaf'aluna (they do)
    • Future marker: sa yaf'alu (he will do) - optional

    Key Takeaways

    1. Critical Memorization Requirement: Students must practice conjugations at least 100 times to achieve fluency. Insufficient practice will lead to difficulty in all future lessons.

    2. Present/Future Tense Unity: Arabic fi'l mudari serves as both present and future tense. Context determines the specific meaning:

    3. yakulu = he eats (present)
    4. yakulu = he is eating (continuous present)

    5. yakulu = he will eat (future)

    6. Hidden vs. Visible Pronouns: 5 out of 14 sighas have hidden pronouns (mustatir), while 9 have visible pronouns embedded in the conjugation.

    7. Foundation for Advanced Learning: Mastery of fi'l madhi, fi'l mudari, and dama'ir is essential before proceeding. Students who don't master these fundamentals will likely struggle and eventually quit.

    8. Homework Assignment: Complete word list with two parts - verbs (madhi and mudari forms) and nouns. Six exercises await completion of this chapter.

    9. Future Topics: Next class will cover fi'l mudari majhul (passive voice) and continue with remaining exercises before completing the textbook.
