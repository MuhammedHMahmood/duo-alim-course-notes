    Nahw 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-09-30

    Session Overview

    This session focused on lesson three covering al-fi'l al-madi (past tense verbs) and their conjugation patterns. The class covered the 14 different verb forms (siras) based on number and gender of the doer, practiced conjugation with various verbs, and introduced passive voice formation. Students practiced conjugating verbs following the fa'ala pattern and learned to apply these patterns to other triliteral verbs.

    Grammar Rules Covered

    Al-Fi'l Al-Madi (Past Tense Verb) Conjugation

    • Verbs change based on two factors: number and gender of the doer
    • Total of 14 different forms (siras) possible for each verb
    • Each sira identifies: singular/dual/plural, masculine/feminine, first/second/third person

    Conjugation Pattern (using fa'ala as the scale):

    Third Person (ghaib): - fa'ala (wahid mutakkar ghaib) - he did (singular masculine) - fa'ala (tathniya mutakkar ghaib) - they two did (dual masculine)
    - fa'alu (jama' mutakkar ghaib) - they did (plural masculine) - fa'alat (wahid mu'annas ghaib) - she did (singular feminine) - fa'alata (tathniya mu'annas ghaib) - they two did (dual feminine) - fa'alna (jama' mu'annas ghaib) - they did (plural feminine)

    Second Person (hadir): - fa'alta (wahid mutakkar hadir) - you did (singular masculine) - fa'altuma (tathniya mutakkar hadir) - you two did (dual masculine) - fa'altum (jama' mutakkar hadir) - you did (plural masculine) - fa'alti (wahid mu'annas hadir) - you did (singular feminine) - fa'altuma (tathniya mu'annas hadir) - you two did (dual feminine) - fa'altunna (jama' mu'annas hadir) - you did (plural feminine)

    First Person (mutakallim): - fa'altu (wahid mutakallim) - I did - fa'alna (jama' mutakallim) - we did

    Passive Voice Formation (Fi'l Majhool)

    • Change fa'ala → fu'ila
    • Rule: Give dhamma to the fa' kalima (first letter) and kasra to the ayn kalima (second letter)
    • The lam kalima (third letter) stays the same
    • Used when the doer is unknown or not mentioned

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    al-fi'l al-madi past tense verb
    sira (plural: siras) verb form/conjugation
    tasreef conjugation (Arabic term)
    gardan conjugation (Persian term)
    wahid singular
    tathniya dual
    jama' plural
    mutakkar/muzakkar masculine
    mu'annas feminine
    ghaib third person (absent)
    hadir second person (present)
    mutakallim first person (speaker)
    fi'l majhool passive voice
    fa' kalima first letter of the root
    ayn kalima second letter of the root
    lam kalima third letter of the root

    Practice Examples

    Active Voice Conjugations Practiced:

    • nasara (he helped) - nasara, nasara, nasaru, nasarat, nasarata, nasarna...
    • dharaba (he hit) - dharaba, dharaba, dharabu, dharabat, dharabata, dharabna...
    • sami'a (he heard) - sami'a, sami'a, sami'u, sami'at, sami'ata, sami'na...
    • karuma (he was noble) - karuma, karuma, karumu, karumat, karumata, karumna...
    • kharaja (he went out) - kharaja, kharaja, kharaju, kharajat, kharajata, kharajna...
    • ja'ala (he made/put) - ja'ala, ja'ala, ja'alu, ja'alat, ja'alata, ja'alna...
    • fataha (he opened) - fataha, fataha, fatahu, fatahat, fatahata, fatahna...
    • hasuna (he was good/beautiful) - hasuna, hasuna, hasunu, hasunat, hasunata, hasunna...
    • qara'a (he read) - qara'a, qara'a, qara'u, qara'at, qara'ata, qara'na...

    Passive Voice Examples:

    • nasara → nusira (he was helped)
    • ja'ala → ju'ila (it was made/put)
    • sami'a → sumi'a (it was heard)
    • karuma → kurima (he was honored)

    Key Takeaways

    1. Practice is essential - Students must practice conjugations 100 times to achieve fluency
    2. fa'ala is the universal scale - All triliteral verbs follow this pattern
    3. 14 siras total - Based on number (singular/dual/plural) and gender (masculine/feminine) combinations
    4. First person has no gender distinction - wahid mutakallim and jama' mutakallim are used for both genders
    5. Passive voice formation is systematic - Dhamma on first letter, kasra on second letter
    6. Root letter identification - fa', ayn, lam represent the three root letters of any triliteral verb
