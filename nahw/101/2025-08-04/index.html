    Nahw 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-08-04

    Session Overview

    This introductory session focused on establishing the foundational understanding of Arabic grammar (Nahw) and its importance in understanding the Quran. The instructor emphasized that Nahw is essential for comprehending the linguistic miracle of the Quran and developing the ability to understand 50-60% of Quranic recitation during prayers. The class included student introductions, revealing approximately 50% of students are hafiz/hafizah, and covered the basic distinction between Nahw (syntax) and Sarf (morphology).

    Grammar Rules Covered

    Definition and Pronunciation of Nahw

    • Nahw (نحو): Arabic grammar/syntax - NOT "nahaw" or "nahwa"
    • Pronunciation challenge: both 'ha' and 'waw' have sukoon (no vowel marks)
    • Full term: Ilmun Nahwi (علم النحو) - the science of Arabic grammar

    Two Major Branches of Arabic Language

    1. Nahw (نحو) - Syntax
    2. Sarf (صرف) - Morphology

    Sarf (Morphology) Overview

    • Study of verb patterns (verbalization) for tense and voice
    • Determines gender, number (singular/dual/plural) of verb subjects
    • Arabic has three numbers: singular, dual, plural (unlike English which only has singular/plural)
    • Only acknowledges two genders: male and female

    Nahw (Syntax) Overview

    • Study of word arrangement to form meaningful sentences
    • Determines grammatical structure through word endings
    • Focuses on proper word order and short vowel endings (i'rab)

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    نحو (Nahw) Arabic grammar/syntax
    صرف (Sarf) Morphology
    علم النحو (Ilmun Nahwi) The science of Arabic grammar
    اللغة العربية (Al-Lughat al-Arabiyya) Arabic language
    إعراب (I'rab) Grammatical analysis/declension
    ضمة (Dhamma) U-sound vowel mark
    فتحة (Fatha) A-sound vowel mark
    كسرة (Kasra) I-sound vowel mark
    سكون (Sukoon) No vowel mark/silence
    مفرد (Mufrad) Singular
    مثنى (Muthanna) Dual
    جمع (Jam') Plural
    بلاغة (Balagha) Rhetoric

    I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

    Example from Surah Al-Fatiha:

    • الحمدُ لله (Alhamdu lillahi)
    • الحمد ends with Dhamma (u-sound) - not Fatha or Kasra
    • لله ends with Kasra (i-sound) - not Dhamma or Fatha
    • رب العالمين (Rabbi al-alameen)
    • العالمين ends with Fatha (a-sound)

    Key Questions Addressed by Nahw:

    • Why does الحمد end with Dhamma and not Fatha or Kasra?
    • Why does لله end with Kasra?
    • What determines these specific vowel endings?

    Practice Examples

    Word Order Importance:

    English example demonstrating proper syntax: - Correct: "I am talking to you in English" - Incorrect: "In English am I to you talking" - Incorrect: "Am I to you talking English in"

    The same principle applies to Arabic - words must be arranged in proper order to create meaningful sentences.

    Short Vowel Recognition:

    Students should practice identifying the three short vowels in Quranic recitation: - Dhamma (u-sound): ـُ - Fatha (a-sound): ـَ
    - Kasra (i-sound): ـِ

    Key Takeaways

    1. Nahw is essential for understanding the Quran's linguistic miracle and rhetoric (balagha)
    2. Goal: Understand 50-60% of Quranic recitation during Tarawih prayers by course completion
    3. Learning process: Terms and concepts will be repeated throughout the year for gradual mastery
    4. Two main components of Nahw:
    5. Proper word arrangement in sentences
    6. Correct vowel endings (i'rab) for each word
    7. Universal Grammar Theory: All languages share basic grammatical structures (verbs, nouns, adjectives), suggesting divine origin of language
    8. Student composition: Approximately 50% of the class are hafiz/hafizah, creating a strong foundation for Arabic study
    9. Study methodology: Information may seem overwhelming initially, but consistent repetition will lead to understanding
    10. Practical application: Students who complete two years can understand 60-70% of Quranic recitation during prayers
