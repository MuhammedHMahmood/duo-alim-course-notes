    Fiqh 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-05-11

    Session Overview

    This session covered the ruling of wudu (Hukm al-Wudu), its linguistic and technical definitions, its integrals (Arkan), the conditions for its validity (Shurut al-Sihah), and the conditions that make wudu obligatory (Shurut al-Wujub). Students read aloud from the text in Arabic and translated passages into English, with the teacher providing explanations and answering questions throughout.

    Key Themes

    1. Ruling of Wudu — established from Quranic verse and hadith; wudu is obligatory for Salah
    2. Definition of Wudu — linguistic vs. shari'i (technical) meanings
    3. Virtue of Maintaining Wudu — reward and elevation of rank for one who remains in a state of wudu
    4. Integrals (Arkan) of Wudu — the four obligatory acts that constitute wudu
    5. Conditions of Validity (Shurut al-Sihah) — three conditions required for wudu to count
    6. Conditions of Obligation (Shurut al-Wujub) — seven conditions that make wudu binding upon a person

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    Ruling of Wudu

    Wudu is obligatory (fard) before performing Salah. It is also required before touching the Mus'haf (physical copy of the Quran).

    Evidence 1 — Quranic Verse (Surah al-Ma'idah):

    يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ فَاغْسِلُوا وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى الْمَرَافِقِ وَامْسَحُوا بِرُؤُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى الْكَعْبَيْنِ "O you who believe, when you stand for Salah, wash your faces and your hands up to and including the elbows, and wipe your heads, and wash your feet up to and including the ankles."

    • فَاغْسِلُوا is a command form (Amr), indicating obligation
    • وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ grammatically connects back to فَاغْسِلُوا (not to وَامْسَحُوا), confirming feet are to be washed, not wiped
    • إِلَى in إِلَى الْمَرَافِقِ and إِلَى الْكَعْبَيْنِ is inclusive — the elbows and ankles must be included in the wash

    Evidence 2 — Hadith of the Prophet ﷺ:

    لَا يَقْبَلُ اللَّهُ صَلَاةَ أَحَدِكُمْ إِذَا أَحْدَثَ حَتَّى يَتَوَضَّأَ "Allah does not accept the Salah of any one of you when he enters into a state of hadath, until he performs wudu."

    • This confirms that the acceptance of Salah is conditioned upon being in a state of wudu
    • If one breaks wudu, they must renew it before praying

    Additional Rulings Derived

    Ruling Detail
    Salah is not permissible without wudu A person in a state of hadath may not pray
    Touching the Mus'haf is not permissible without wudu One must be in a state of wudu to handle the physical Quran
    A barrier on the skin invalidates wudu If water cannot reach the skin, wudu does not count
    Wudu performed while a nullifier occurs is invalid If a nullifier of wudu takes place during the act of wudu, wudu must be restarted
    Wudu is not sufficient when ghusl is required One in a state of Hadath al-Akbar must perform ghusl, not merely wudu

    Conditions & Categories

    Definition of Wudu

    Perspective Definition
    Linguistic (Lughawi) Al-Husn (beauty) and al-Nazafa (cleanliness)
    Shari'i (Technical) A ceremonial purification involving water (Taharatun Ma'iyah) that includes washing the face, hands, and feet, and wiping the head

    Arkan al-Wudu — The Four Integrals (All Obligatory)

    These are the minimum obligatory acts of wudu. Each must be performed at least once:

    # Integral Arabic Notes
    1 Washing the face once غَسْل الوَجْه مَرَّة Boundaries defined below
    2 Washing both arms up to and including the elbows once غَسْل الأَيْدِي إِلَى الْمَرَافِق المَرَافِق = elbows, included in the wash
    3 Wiping one-fourth of the head مَسْح رُبُع الرَّأْس Note: wiping, not washing
    4 Washing both feet up to and including the ankles once غَسْل الأَرْجُل إِلَى الكَعْبَيْن الكَعْبَيْن = ankles, included in the wash

    Boundaries of the face: - In length (al-Tul): From the highest point of the forehead (أَعْلَى سَطْح الجَبْهَة) to the bottom of the chin (أَسْفَل الذَّقَن) - In width (al-'Ard): From earlobe to earlobe (مَا بَيْن شَحْمَتَي الأُذُنَيْن)

    Shurut al-Sihah — Three Conditions for Validity of Wudu

    Wudu is not valid unless these three conditions are met. The sought-out benefit of wudu (reward and elevated rank) is also not attained without them:

    # Condition Detail
    1 Water must reach all body parts whose washing is obligatory Every required part must have water flow over it completely
    2 Nothing must be present that prevents water from reaching the skin E.g. wax (شَمْع), flour/dough (عَجِين), superglue, caulking, oil-based paint, nail polish — any physical barrier invalidates wudu
    3 No nullifier of wudu must occur during the wudu If one passes wind, bleeds, etc. while performing wudu, the wudu is invalidated and must be restarted

    Note on barriers: Nail polish forms a complete layer over the nails and is considered a barrier — it must be removed for wudu to be valid. Flour is particularly noted because it creates a barrier both wet and dry, but especially when wet.

    Shurut al-Wujub — Seven Conditions for Wudu to be Obligatory

    Wudu is only obligatory upon a person in whom all of the following conditions are found:

    # Condition Arabic Detail
    1 Puberty (al-Bulugh) البُلُوغ Wudu is not legally obligatory upon a child; however, children should be taught early for proper upbringing (tarbiyah)
    2 Sound intellect (al-'Aql) العَقْل Wudu is not obligatory upon the insane (al-Majnun); this applies to those who cannot differentiate right from wrong or cannot perform basic functions. Conditions like severe autism, extreme developmental disorders, or severe dissociative disorders may fall under this category. Bipolar disorder alone, however, generally does not classify a person as majnun
    3 Islam الإِسْلَام Wudu is not legally obligatory upon a non-Muslim (al-Kafir)
    4 Ability to use water (al-Qudrah) القُدْرَة عَلَى اسْتِعْمَال المَاء One must be able to access and use sufficient water for all wudu limbs. If a person only has enough water for survival (drinking), they are not required to use it for wudu and may perform tayammum instead
    5 Presence of Hadath al-Asghar وُجُود الحَدَث الأَصْغَر Wudu is not necessary upon a person already in the state of wudu (al-Mutawaddi)
    6 Freedom from Hadath al-Akbar خُلُوُّه مِن الحَدَث الأَكْبَر If a person is in a state of major impurity (Hadath al-Akbar), wudu alone is insufficient — ghusl is required instead
    7 Tightness of time (Diyq al-Waqt) ضِيق الوَقْت If there is ample time before the Salah's time expires, one is not obligated to perform wudu immediately (not 'ala al-fawr) — delay is permissible. However, if time is tight (the Salah is about to expire), wudu and Salah become immediately obligatory

    Scholarly Opinions

    • The Hanafi position (consistent with this text) holds that إِلَى in the wudu verse is inclusive — the elbows and ankles are washed as part of the obligatory limbs.
    • The discussion of what constitutes a "majnun" for legal purposes acknowledges that modern psychiatric conditions must be assessed based on whether the person can reason and differentiate right from wrong — not merely based on a diagnosis.
    • The question of nail polish as a barrier is noted, with the teacher indicating it does constitute a barrier that must be removed for wudu to be valid.

    Lessons & Practical Application

    1. Maintain wudu at all times. The one who is consistent (wadhaba) upon wudu — sleeping in wudu, renewing immediately after breaking it — earns great reward and elevation of rank in the Akhirah. This is a Prophetic practice to cultivate.

    2. Check for physical barriers before wudu. Before making wudu, ensure the skin is free of wax, heavy lotion residue, nail polish, superglue, paint, caulking, or any other substance that forms a physical layer over the skin.

    3. Do not interrupt wudu. If a nullifier of wudu (passing wind, bleeding, etc.) occurs mid-wudu, restart from the beginning.

    4. Teach children early. Even though wudu is not legally obligatory on pre-pubescent children, good tarbiyah requires instilling these habits early so they are second nature upon reaching puberty.

    5. Know when tayammum applies. If water is insufficient for all wudu limbs — or only enough for survival — tayammum is performed instead.

    6. Wudu vs. Ghusl. Understand the distinction between Hadath al-Asghar (minor impurity, remedied by wudu) and Hadath al-Akbar (major impurity, requiring full ghusl). Wudu in a state of Hadath al-Akbar does not suffice.

    7. Time awareness for Salah. If a Salah's time is running out and one has not yet prayed, it becomes obligatory to immediately perform wudu and pray. When ample time remains, one has flexibility in when to perform wudu.
