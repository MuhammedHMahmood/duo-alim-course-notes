    Fiqh 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-06-22

    Session Overview

    This was the final Fiqh class of the academic year. The session covered three major topics: (1) the legal basis (mashru'iyyah) and conditions (shurut) of Tayammum, its integrals (arkan), sunnahs, method of performance, and its invalidators; (2) wiping over leather/thick socks (masah 'ala al-khuffayn) — its conditions, time periods, and invalidators; and (3) wiping over wound dressings and casts (masah 'ala al-'isabah wal-jabirah). The session also included miscellaneous rulings connecting intention to validity of Tayammum for various acts of worship.

    Key Themes

    • Tayammum is a divinely granted concession enabling worship when water is unavailable or harmful to use
    • The validity of Tayammum depends strictly on eight conditions, particularly the correctness of intention (niyyah)
    • Intention must be made for an ibadah maqsudah (a purposive act of worship) for Tayammum to be valid for Salah
    • Masah 'ala al-khuffayn is a concession granted by Shari'ah to ease the practice of Wudu — not an independent act of worship
    • Islamic law consistently removes hardship (haraj) from worship, as evidenced by rulings on tayammum, masah, and wound dressings
    • The same invalidators that break Wudu break Tayammum and Masah al-khuffayn

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    Qur'anic Evidence for Tayammum

    Surah al-Nisa (4:43) / al-Ma'idah (5:6):

    "...wa in kuntum marda aw 'ala safarin aw ja'a ahadun minkum min al-gha'it aw lamastumu al-nisa'a falam tajidu ma'an fatayyamamu sa'idan tayyiban famsahu biwujuhikum wa aydikum. Inna Allaha kana 'afuwwan ghafura."

    "If you are sick, or on a journey, or any of you has used the restroom, or has had intimate relations and you find no water, then perform Tayammum with clean earth — wipe your faces and hands. Indeed Allah is Most Pardoning, Most Forgiving."

    Hadith Evidence for Tayammum

    The Prophet ﷺ said:

    "Fuddiina 'ala al-naas bi-thalathin: ju'ilat sufufuna kasufuf al-mala'ikah, wa ju'ilat lana al-ardu kulluha masjidan, wa ju'ilat turbataha lana tahuran idha lam najid al-ma'."

    "We have been given virtue over people in three things: our rows have been made like the rows of the angels; the entire earth has been made a place of prayer for us; and its soil has been made purifying for us when we do not find water."

    Reason Tayammum Was Legislated

    Tayammum was prescribed because human beings sometimes cannot use water — either due to water being unavailable (mafqud) or due to illness — so that people are not deprived of performing acts of worship (like Salah) that require purification.

    Hadith Evidence for Masah al-Khuffayn

    The Prophet ﷺ said:

    "Al-mashu 'ala al-khuffayn lil-musafir thalathu ayyamin wa layaliyha, wa lil-muqim yawm wa laylah."

    "Wiping over leather socks is permissible for a traveler for three days and nights, and for a resident for one day and one night."

    Qur'anic Evidence for Concessions (Masah over Wound Dressings)

    "Wa ma ja'ala 'alaykum fi al-dini min haraj." "He has not placed any hardship upon you in the religion." (Surah al-Hajj 22:78)

    Conditions & Categories

    Conditions of Valid Tayammum (8 Conditions)

    1. Niyyah (Intention) Tayammum is invalid without intention. The intention must be for one of three things: - Purification from hadath - The permissibility of Salah (istibahatu al-salah) - An ibadah maqsudah — a purposive act of worship that cannot be validly performed without purification (e.g., Salah, Sajdatu al-Tilawah)

    Key rulings on intention: - It is not necessary to specify which hadath in the intention - If a person makes Tayammum with the intention of touching the Mus'haf: Salah will not be valid, because merely touching the Qur'an is not an ibadah in itself — the actual ibadah is tilawah (recitation) - If a person makes Tayammum with the intention of calling the Adhan or Iqamah: Salah will not be valid, as Adhan and Iqamah are not stand-alone ibadah maqsudah - If a person makes Tayammum with the intention of recitation of Qur'an while in a state of hadath asghar: Salah will not be valid, because recitation of Qur'an — though it is an ibadah maqsudah — can be performed without Wudu - If a person makes Tayammum with the intention of Salah: all other acts (touching the Qur'an, reciting, Sajdatu al-Tilawah) become permissible under that same Tayammum

    2. Presence of a Valid Excuse One of the following excusable circumstances must exist: 1. Water is more than a mile away 2. A skilled Muslim doctor informs the person that using water will cause illness, worsen existing illness, or delay recovery 3. The person overwhelmingly fears that using cold water (the only water available) will cause death or severe harm 4. The person fears thirst for himself or a companion if water is used for Wudu (water is insufficient for both drinking and purification) 5. No instrument (bucket, rope) is available to extract water from a well 6. An enemy — human or carnivorous animal (hayawan muftaris) — stands between the person and the water 7. The person overwhelmingly fears that performing Wudu will cause him to miss 'Eid Salah or Salatu al-Janazah (prayers with no qada)

    Important distinction: If a person fears missing a regular Fard Salah or Jumu'ah due to performing Wudu, Tayammum is not permissible. He must perform Wudu, pray, and make qada of the missed obligatory prayer. For Jumu'ah specifically, he will pray Zuhr as a substitute ('iwadan 'an al-Jumu'ah).

    3. Use of a Pure Earthy Substance Tayammum must be performed with something from the earth (jins al-ard): dirt (turab), stone (hajar), or thick dust (raml). Tayammum is not valid with wood, silver, or gold.

    4. Wiping the Entire Face and Both Arms Including Elbows The entirety (or majority) of the full hand must be used. If a person uses only two fingers to wipe — even if he covers the entire arm — the Tayammum is not valid because the majority of the hand was not used.

    5. [Implicit from text] The wiping must be with both hands on both body parts.

    6. Two Strikes with the Inner Palms (Darbatayn bi-batin al-kaffayn) A person must perform two strikes — one for the face, one for the arms. If a person strikes twice in the same place, or dirt falls onto his body and he wipes it without striking, the Tayammum remains valid.

    7. No Barrier Between Wiping and Skin Nothing must be present between the hand and the skin: wax (sham'), grease (shahm), thick paint, super glue, nail polish (for sisters), etc. These must be removed before wiping. If present and not removed, Tayammum is invalid.

    8. No Ongoing Invalidator Nothing that invalidates Tayammum should occur during the performance of Tayammum, such as: - Menstrual bleeding (hayd) - Postnatal bleeding (nifas) - Hadath occurring mid-performance (passing wind, vomiting, bleeding — unless the person is ma'dhur)

    Integrals (Arkan) of Tayammum

    There are only two physical integrals (plus intention as the internal integral): 1. Wiping the entirety of the face (mashu jami' al-wajh) 2. Wiping both arms including the elbows (mashu al-yadayn ma'a al-mirfaqayn)

    Sunnahs of Tayammum

    1. Reciting Bismillah al-Rahman al-Rahim at the beginning
    2. Following the order: face first, then right arm, then left arm
    3. Not separating the wiping of the face and hands with a foreign action (fi'l ajnabi)
    4. Moving the hands forwards and backwards (iqbal wa idbar) in the dirt to get a good amount on the hands
    5. Dusting off the hands (nafd) after lifting them from the dirt, before wiping
    6. Keeping the fingers spread apart when placing the hands in the dirt

    Method of Performing Tayammum (Kayfiyyah)

    1. Pull sleeves up above the elbows (shammara 'an sa'idayhi)
    2. Recite Bismillah al-Rahman al-Rahim
    3. Make intention (niyyah) — e.g., for permissibility of Salah (istibahatu al-salah)
    4. Place the inner palms on pure earth with fingers spread apart, moving hands forward and backward
    5. Raise hands and dust them off
    6. Wipe the entire face with both hands
    7. Place hands on earth a second time in the same manner
    8. Wipe the right arm entirely (including elbow) with the entire left hand
    9. Wipe the left arm entirely (including elbow) with the right hand
    10. Tayammum is now complete — one may now perform as many Fard and Nafl prayers as desired

    Invalidators (Nawaqid) of Tayammum

    1. Everything that invalidates Wudu also invalidates Tayammum
    2. Finding and gaining the ability to use water (al-qudrah 'ala isti'mal al-ma')
    3. The disappearance of the excuse that originally made Tayammum permissible (e.g., enemy is gone, illness resolved, water found)

    Miscellaneous Rulings on Tayammum

    Situation Ruling
    Tayammum made for entering a Masjid Cannot perform Salah with it (not an ibadah maqsudah; also Wudu not required to enter Masjid)
    Tayammum made for I'tikaf I'tikaf itself is not the issue — but Ghusl (if in janabah) still does not permit Salah without intention for Salah
    Tayammum made with intention of visiting graves or burying the deceased Salah is not permissible with this Tayammum
    Person hopes to find water before Salah time exits It is mustahab to delay Tayammum and wait
    Someone has promised to bring the person water It is wajib (yajib) to wait for the water
    Person has little water needed for kneading flour (making bread) He should make Tayammum and use the water for food
    Person has little water needed for making gravy/curry He must use the water for Wudu — gravy is liquid and not a substitute survival need in the same category
    Seeking water from a companion Obligatory to ask if in a place where people are generally not stingy with water; not obligatory if people in the area are known to be stingy
    Performing Tayammum before Salah time enters Permissible if the person is not in the ma'dhur category and has no expectation of finding water
    Person whose hands and feet are amputated and whose face is injured Prays without any purification
    Majority of limbs are injured Performs Tayammum, then wipes over the injured parts
    Majority of limbs are uninjured Performs Wudu and wipes over the injured parts

    Conditions for Masah 'ala al-Khuffayn (6 Conditions)

    1. The socks must have been put on while in a state of Taharah (Wudu) — specifically, the feet must have been washed before putting them on. If a person washes his feet, puts on the socks, then completes Wudu — this is valid, as long as Wudu was not broken between washing the feet and completing Wudu.
    2. The socks must cover the ankles (ka'bayn) — ankle socks do not qualify.
    3. The socks must be free of holes equal to or larger than the size of the three smallest toes (thalathu asabi' min asghar asabi' al-qadam). If the holes are that large or larger, the socks cannot be used for masah.
    4. The socks must stay on the feet without needing to be tied (yastamsika 'ala al-rijlayn biduni shadd) — they must not fall off when the foot is lifted.
    5. The socks must prevent water from reaching the feet — they must be waterproof.
    6. A person must be able to walk consistently while wearing them.

    The Obligatory (Fard) and Sunnah Amount of Masah al-Khuffayn

    Ruling
    Fard (minimum): Equivalent of the three smallest fingers of the hand (min asghar asabi' al-yad) wiped on the top (zahir) of each foot
    Sunnah: Keep fingers spread wide and wipe from the tips of the toes up to the shin (saq), starting from the bottom upward on the top of the foot

    Time Periods for Masah al-Khuffayn

    Person Duration
    Resident (Muqim) 1 day and 1 night = 24 hours
    Traveler (Musafir) 3 days and 3 nights = 72 hours

    When does the clock start? The time period begins from the first time Wudu breaks after putting on the socks while in a state of Taharah — not from when the socks were put on or when Wudu was completed.

    Transitioning Between Resident and Traveler Status

    Situation Ruling
    Resident begins traveling before his 24 hours expire He completes the remaining time up to 72 hours (the traveler's period)
    Traveler returns home (becomes resident) after 48 hours of wiping He must remove socks and restart; his time period ends when he becomes a resident
    Traveler returns home within 24 hours (still within resident period) He completes the 24-hour resident period and then is done
    Musafir wiped for less than 1 day then becomes a resident He completes 1 full day and night (yawm wa laylah) as the resident period

    Invalidators of Masah al-Khuffayn

    1. Everything that invalidates Wudu
    2. Removing either or both socks (naz' al-khuff) — even one sock requires restarting
    3. Majority of the foot slipping out of the top of the sock (saq al-khuff)
    4. Expiry of the time period (intiha' muddatihi)
    5. Water reaching the majority of one foot inside the sock (sock is no longer waterproof)

    What Cannot Be Used for Masah

    Item Ruling
    Turban ('imamah) Masah over it is not permissible in place of wiping the head
    Topi / head cap (qalansuwah) Not permissible for masah of the head
    Burqa / face veil (burqu') Not permissible for masah of the head
    Gloves (quffazayn) Not permissible for masah in place of washing the hands

    The only concession for wiping over a covering is for the feet, using socks that meet the specified conditions.

    Masah over Wound Dressings ('Isabah) and Casts (Jabirah)

    Conditions and Rulings:

    • If a person cannot wash or wipe a body part due to injury, he may wipe the majority of the wound dressing or cast from on top
    • This continues until the wound heals (ila an yalta'ima al-jurh)
    • It is not a condition that the dressing be tied upon a state of Taharah (impurity such as blood under the bandage does not affect validity)
    • The same applies to a cast (jabirah): wipe over it until healed; no condition of Taharah for putting it on
    Situation Ruling
    One foot has a cast, the other is healthy Wipe over the cast on the injured foot; wash the other foot normally
    Cast falls off before wound heals Masah remains valid; no need to rewipe (if already wiped once)
    Cast is changed to a new one Not obligatory to wipe the new cast, but it is better (afadal) to wipe it
    Person has sore eyes and skilled Muslim doctor forbids washing eyes Wiping over the eyes is permissible

    Niyyah in Masah

    Act Niyyah Required?
    Masah 'ala al-Khuffayn Not required
    Masah 'ala al-Jabirah (cast) Not required
    Masah of the head (in Wudu) Not required
    Tayammum Required — the only act among these where Niyyah is a condition

    Scholarly Opinions

    • The Hanafi position (implied throughout) is that Tayammum is a full substitute for Wudu and Ghusl when valid excuses exist, and a single Tayammum made for Salah covers all subsidiary acts of worship
    • The ruling that adhan and iqamah are not ibadah maqsudah is consistent with the position that Wudu is not obligatory for them (though it is recommended), and missing them does not require qada
    • The ruling distinguishing between fear of missing 'Eid/'Janazah (permits Tayammum) versus fear of missing Fard/Jumu'ah (does not permit Tayammum) reflects the principle that prayers with qada can be made up, while those without cannot

    Lessons & Practical Application

    1. Intention determines validity: Before performing Tayammum, be clear about what ibadah you are performing it for. Intending Salah covers the broadest scope of subsequent worship acts.

    2. Know your excuses: Tayammum is not freely done — a specific, valid excuse from the list must be present. The most common in daily life are unavailability of water, illness, or fear of harm from water.

    3. The earth is your purifier: The Prophet ﷺ reminded us that the entire earth is a Masjid and its soil is tahur. This is a unique blessing of this ummah.

    4. Masah al-Khuffayn is a real concession: In cold weather or travel, wiping over thick socks that meet the six conditions is a fully valid and legitimate alternative to washing the feet. Learn the conditions and time periods carefully.

    5. Injured individuals are not excused from worship: Rather, they are given accommodation. A person with a cast does not skip Wudu — they simply wipe over the cast. A person with amputated limbs still performs Salah.

    6. The deen removes hardship: All of these concessions — Tayammum, masah, wiping over casts — reflect the Qur'anic principle: "Allah intends ease for you and does not intend hardship." Fiqh is not a set of obstacles but a framework of facilitation.

    7. Practical Tayammum sequence to memorize: Roll up sleeves → Bismillah → Niyyah → Strike earth (fingers spread) → Move hands forward and back → Lift and dust off → Wipe face → Strike earth again → Wipe right arm with left hand → Wipe left arm with right hand.

    8. Seeking water is itself obligatory: If a companion has water, asking for it is wajib (in places where people do not withhold water). If promised water, waiting is wajib. Tayammum is not a shortcut — it is a last resort.
