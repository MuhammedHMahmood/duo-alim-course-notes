    Fiqh 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-04-06

    Session Overview

    This session covers the rulings of well water (أَحْكَامُ مِيَاهِ الْآبَارِ), focusing on what happens when various types of impurities or animals fall into wells. The discussion establishes clear guidelines for determining when well water becomes impure and the specific purification procedures required for different scenarios.

    Key Themes

    • Classification of animals by their inherent purity status (نَجِسُ الْعَيْنِ vs. pure animals)
    • Connection to previous chapter on leftover water (سُؤْر) rulings
    • Practical purification procedures using bucket counts when complete water removal is impossible
    • Timeline considerations when contamination discovery date is unknown
    • Distinction between animals with flowing blood vs. insects/small creatures

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    When Pure Impurity Falls in Wells

    Ruling: If any amount of impurity (نَجَاسَة) falls into a well, even a drop of blood or wine, all water must be removed from the well.

    Animals that are Najis al-Ayn (Inherently Impure)

    Ruling: If an animal that is نَجِسُ الْعَيْنِ (like pig) falls into a well, all water must be removed regardless of: - Whether the animal died or came out alive - Whether its mouth touched the water or not

    Animals with Impure Saliva but Pure Bodies

    Ruling: If an animal falls in that is not inherently impure but whose leftover water (سُؤْر) is impure (like dogs, predatory animals), all water must be removed.

    Pure Animals and Humans

    Ruling: If a human or pure animal (mule, donkey, falcon, eagle) falls in and: - Comes out alive - Has no impurity on their body - Their mouth/beak doesn't touch the water Then the water remains pure.

    The Principle of Leftover Water Connection

    Key Principle: "وَإِذَا وَصَلَ لُعَابُ الْوَاقِعِ فِي الْمَاءِ فَهُوَ فِي حُكْمِ سُؤْرِهِ" If the saliva of a fallen creature reaches the water, then the water follows the ruling of that animal's leftover water.

    Conditions & Categories

    Animals Without Flowing Blood

    Examples: Bugs, flies, wasps, scorpions, honeybees, spiders, mosquitoes Ruling: Do not render well water impure when they die in it

    Aquatic Animals

    Examples: Fish, frogs, crabs - animals born and living in water Ruling: Do not render well water impure when they die in it

    Large Animals That Die in Wells

    Examples: Dogs, goats, humans Ruling: Water becomes impure and all water must be removed, but only if removed immediately before bloating occurs

    Practical Bucket Guidelines (When Complete Removal Impossible)

    • General cases requiring full water removal: 200 average-sized buckets
    • Medium animals (cat, chicken) that died: 40 buckets
    • Small animals (sparrow, mouse) that died: 20 buckets

    Animal Dung Categories

    Pure dung (doesn't render well impure): Horse, camel, cow dung - unless amount is excessive Small bird droppings: Pigeon, sparrow droppings don't render well impure

    Scholarly Opinions

    The text presents the Hanafi position consistently without mentioning alternative scholarly opinions in this session.

    Lessons & Practical Application

    Timeline Rulings for Unknown Contamination

    1. If animal found bloated with unknown entry time: Well considered impure for last 3 days and 3 nights
    2. If dead animal found before bloating with unknown entry time: Well considered impure for last 1 day and 1 night
    3. Consequence: Any prayers performed with wudu from that water must be repeated, clothes washed, body cleaned

    Purification of Equipment and Person

    After removing contaminated water, the rope, bucket, and hands of the person doing the removal automatically become pure - no separate washing required.

    Connection to Modern Applications

    • Farmers dealing with harmful animals (boars, coyotes) may hunt them for crop protection
    • Payment for such services is permissible as it removes harm
    • Animal hides may be used if properly slaughtered and tanned
    • Forgetting to say "Bismillah" when hunting is excused if unintentional

    Practical Decision Framework

    When any creature falls in well water: 1. Identify the creature type and purity status 2. Determine if mouth/saliva contacted water
    3. Check if creature came out alive or died 4. Apply appropriate purification procedure 5. Consider timeline factors if discovery timing unknown
