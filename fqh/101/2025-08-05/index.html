    Fiqh 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-08-05

    Session Overview

    This introductory session emphasized the importance and scope of Islamic jurisprudence (fiqh) as a comprehensive way of life. The instructor discussed the generational nature of Islamic knowledge, the significance of sincere intention in seeking knowledge, and introduced the fundamental structure of fiqh. The session covered the two main categories of Islamic law and outlined the five pillars of Islam that will form the core curriculum for the semester.

    Key Themes

    • Generational Impact of Islamic Knowledge: How learning and teaching Islamic knowledge benefits entire family lineages
    • Sincerity in Seeking Knowledge: The importance of studying for Allah's sake alone, distinguishing between fame (shohra) and divine acceptance (kabuliyah)
    • Comprehensive Nature of Fiqh: Islam as a complete way of life encompassing all aspects of human behavior
    • Priority of Divine Obligations: Allah's rights take precedence over worldly matters
    • Foundation for Spiritual Success: Knowledge of fiqh as preparation for the hereafter

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    Fiqh Islamic jurisprudence; understanding of Islamic law
    Ibadat Acts of worship
    Mu'amalat Social transactions and interpersonal relations
    Shohra Fame (bottom-up system)
    Kabuliyah Divine acceptance (top-down from Allah)
    Tahara Purification/cleanliness
    Badani Physical worship requiring bodily effort
    Mali Worship requiring financial expenditure
    Hafidh One who has memorized the Quran
    Istiqamah Steadfastness and consistency

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    Definition and Scope of Fiqh

    • Fiqh encompasses both ibadat (worship) and mu'amalat (social dealings)
    • Books of fiqh are expansive because Islam governs all aspects of life
    • Both categories are essential for a complete Islamic lifestyle

    Hadith of Five Pillars

    Evidence: Abdullah ibn Umar (ra) narrated that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Islam has been built on five pillars: testifying that there is no deity worthy of worship except Allah and that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah, establishing salah, paying zakah, making hajj to the house of Allah, and fasting in the month of Ramadan." [Bukhari & Muslim]

    Quranic Evidence for Priority System

    Surah Al-Jumu'ah (62:9-10): - When called for Jumu'ah prayer: "Run towards the remembrance of Allah and leave your businesses" - After prayer completion: "Spread out in the earth" (resume worldly activities) - Ruling: Divine obligations always take priority over worldly matters

    Conditions & Categories

    Categories of Worship (Ibadat)

    1. Badani - Physical worship requiring bodily effort
    2. Mali - Worship requiring financial expenditure
    3. Mixed - Combining both physical and financial elements (e.g., Hajj)

    Tahara as Prerequisite

    • Hadith: "Al-tahuru shatru al-iman" (Cleanliness is half of faith)
    • Required for: Salah, Tawaf, touching/reciting Quran
    • Certain states prevent mosque entry and Quran recitation

    Scholarly Opinions

    Hajj Classification

    • Considered both Badani and Mali worship
    • Requires physical presence and financial capability
    • Historical requirement: ensuring family's financial security before departure
    • Modern costs range from $6,000-$25,000 per person

    Nikah (Marriage) Classification

    • Contains elements of both ibadat and mu'amalat
    • Prophetic practice (Sunnah) aspect makes it worship
    • Daily marital relations and rights fall under social dealings

    Lessons & Practical Application

    For Students of Knowledge

    1. Maintain Sincere Intention: Seek knowledge purely for Allah's sake
    2. Understand Generational Impact: Your learning benefits your entire family lineage
    3. Embrace Challenges: Hard work is necessary to overcome initial difficulties
    4. Seek Divine Acceptance: Aim for kabuliyah (divine acceptance) rather than shohra (fame)

    Semester Curriculum Plan

    The course will cover the five pillars in order: 1. Tahara (Purification) - Prerequisite for worship 2. Salah (Prayer) 3. Sawm (Fasting) 4. Zakah (Alms tax)
    5. Hajj (Pilgrimage)

    Study Approach

    • Each academic year builds upon the previous one's foundation
    • Consistent effort and dedication are essential for progression
    • Arabic proficiency will develop throughout the program
    • Students should actively seek help when facing difficulties
