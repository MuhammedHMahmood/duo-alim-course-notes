    Fiqh 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-08-25

    Session Overview

    This session covered the disliked actions (makruh) during wudu, the legal reasons for performing wudu, conditions for validity, and categories of wudu (obligatory, mandatory, recommended). The class also included a review of the emphasized sunnah of wudu from the previous session and concluded with a discussion on the differences between fard and wajib rulings.

    Key Themes

    • Classification of disliked actions in Islamic law (makruh tahrimi vs makruh tanzihi)
    • Proper etiquette and conditions for valid wudu
    • Legal reasoning (shar'i) for performing wudu
    • Categories of when wudu is required: obligatory, mandatory, and recommended
    • Understanding the hierarchy of Islamic legal rulings (fard, wajib, sunnah)

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    Makruh tahrimi Prohibitively disliked - mandatory to abstain from
    Makruh tanzihi Mildly disliked - better to avoid but no sin
    Isa'ah Doing something wrong (between the two types of makruh)
    Israf Wastage, using excessive resources
    Shar'i Legal reasoning from Islamic perspective
    Rukun (pl. arkan) Integral/pillar of an act
    Fard Obligatory act - if omitted, entire action becomes invalid
    Wajib Mandatory act - sin if omitted intentionally, but action can be remedied
    Mandub/Mustahab Recommended/preferred action
    Ma'dhur Person with chronic excuse that persists
    Sajdah al-sahu Prostration of forgetfulness to remedy missed wajib acts

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    Disliked Actions During Wudu

    Five main disliked actions:

    1. Wasting water (israf) - Using more than necessary, including washing more than three times
    2. Using too little water - Insufficient to reach all required body parts
    3. Striking/slapping face with water - Causes excessive water on clothing (makruh tanzihi)
    4. Speaking during wudu - Except for dhikr, du'a, or necessary matters (responding to parents, salam)
    5. Having assistance without valid excuse - When capable of performing wudu independently

    Evidence cited: "Indeed, the wasteful are brothers of the devils" (Qur'anic reference to prohibition of waste)

    Legal Reasons for Performing Wudu

    Two primary shar'i reasons: 1. Desire to perform acts impermissible without wudu (prayer, touching Qur'an) 2. Divine command due to near expiration of prayer time

    Conditions & Categories

    Conditions for Legal Responsibility (Taklif)

    1. Being Muslim
    2. Puberty (bulugh)
    3. Sanity (aql)
    4. Termination of menstruation, postnatal bleeding, or ritual impurity
    5. Access to sufficient purifying water
    6. Physical ability to perform prayer

    Conditions for Validity of Wudu

    1. Complete water contact - Water must reach skin of all obligatory limbs
    2. Barrier removal - No substances (paint, glue) blocking water from skin
    3. No contradictory states - Nothing breaking wudu during its performance

    Categories of Wudu

    1. Obligatory (Fard) - When in minor ritual impurity and intending: - Ritual prayer (salah) - Prostration of recital (sajdah al-tilawah)
    - Touching Qur'anic verses (unless with non-attached barrier)

    2. Mandatory (Wajib) - When in minor ritual impurity and intending: - Circumambulation (tawaf) - Touching books of tafsir

    3. Recommended (Mandub) - When in minor ritual impurity for: - Touching fiqh books (out of respect) - Renewing wudu for another prayer - Avoiding scholarly disagreement (after touching opposite gender/genitalia) - Before sleeping - After waking from sleep - After committing sins - After laughing loudly outside prayer

    Scholarly Opinions

    Barriers and Substances

    • Body lotions: Permissible as oils don't create barriers
    • Vaseline: Questionable if it forms barrier layer
    • Phone apps: Difference of opinion regarding touching digital Qur'an

    Attached vs Non-attached Barriers

    • Attached barriers (impermissible): Gloves, book covers
    • Non-attached barriers (permissible): Cloth held in hand, head covering used to grab Qur'an

    Lessons & Practical Application

    Key Differences: Fard vs Wajib

    • Fard: If omitted, entire act becomes invalid; denying established fard can remove one from Islam
    • Wajib: If omitted intentionally, incurs sin but act can be remedied (e.g., through sajdah al-sahu); denying doesn't remove from Islam

    Practical Guidance

    • Clothing length: Men's garments must not extend below ankles (except Abu Bakr's specific exception)
    • Speaking during wudu: Permissible for religious matters (salam responses, dhikr) but avoid worldly conversation
    • Assistance: Acceptable for holding water sources, not for performing actual washing without need
    • Supplications in restroom: Can be made silently in heart when necessary

    Etiquette Reminders

    • Maintain proper order of limbs during wudu
    • Ensure continuity between washing actions
    • Start with right limbs when applicable
    • Use appropriate amount of water (neither wasteful nor insufficient)
