    Fiqh 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-11-03

    Session Overview

    This session covered the mandatory requisites (wajibat) and recommended acts (sunnah) of prayer, followed by the etiquette (adab/mustahabbat) of prayer. The class then discussed various supplications recited during prayer and concluded with actions that invalidate the prayer. The instructor emphasized the difference between fard (obligatory), wajib (mandatory requisites), and sunnah (recommended) acts, using the analogy of a house structure versus its beautification.

    Key Themes

    • Classification of prayer acts: Fard vs Wajib vs Sunnah
    • The beautification concept: Sunnah acts enhance the basic structure of prayer
    • Proper gaze direction throughout different prayer positions
    • Hanafi madhab rulings on follower's silence during congregational prayer
    • Actions and behaviors that invalidate prayer
    • Flexibility in making up missed components through sajdat sahw

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    Wajibat Mandatory requisites of prayer
    Sunnah Recommended acts
    Adab/Mustahabbat Etiquette/preferred acts
    Tahrima Opening takbir of prayer
    Thana Opening supplication
    Ta'awud Seeking refuge (A'udhu billahi min ash-shaytani'r-rajim)
    Tasmiyyah Bismillahi'r-rahmani'r-rahim
    Takbirat al-intiqaliyya Takbirs of movement
    Qira'ah Recitation
    Durood Ibrahimi Blessings upon Prophet Ibrahim
    Tahiyyat Tashahhud/sitting recitation
    Qunut Special supplication in Witr
    Sajdat Sahw Prostration of forgetfulness

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    Sunnah Acts of Prayer (16 total):

    1. Raising hands during tahrima: Men raise to ears, women to shoulders
    2. Hand placement: Men place right over left under navel, women on chest under breasts
    3. Reciting thana (opening supplication)
    4. Reciting ta'awud in first rakah only
    5. Reciting basmala before Fatiha in every rakah (except followers remain silent)
    6. Saying Ameen after Fatiha and "Rabbana lakal hamd"
    7. Silent recitation of ta'awud, basmala, ameen, tahmeed
    8. Imam's audible takbir
    9. Takbirat al-intiqaliyya (movement takbirs)
    10. Tasbih three times in ruku and sujud
    11. Reciting Fatiha in 3rd-4th rakah of four-rakah prayer and 3rd of Maghrib
    12. Durood Ibrahimi in final sitting
    13. Final du'a using words that resemble divine speech
    14. Turning head right and left during salam
    15. Intention for greetings when making salam
    16. Various other recommended acts

    Wajib vs Fard Distinction:

    • Qira'ah (recitation) is fard in any two rakahs of obligatory prayer
    • Reciting Fatiha specifically is wajib in first two rakahs
    • Reciting surah after Fatiha is wajib in first two rakahs
    • Missing wajib acts can be remedied with sajdat sahw

    Conditions & Categories

    Follower's Rules in Congregation:

    • Remains silent during recitation (Hanafi position)
    • Recites thana, ta'awud, tahiyyat, and du'as silently
    • Does not recite Fatiha or surahs when following imam
    • Makes intention for imam's position when making salam

    Prayer Invalidators (16 categories):

    1. Human speech - even if forgetful
    2. Excessive movement - if observer cannot tell person is praying
    3. Eating - any external amount; internal residue up to chickpea size permitted
    4. Drinking - any amount
    5. Throat clearing with intentional letter utterance
    6. Crying/moaning from pain (not from remembrance of Allah)
    7. Greeting others with intention of responding
    8. Handshaking
    9. Responding to statements with dhikr
    10. Turning torso away from qibla
    11. Du'as resembling normal conversation
    12. Elongating hamza in takbir (saying "Allahu" instead of "Allahu")
    13. Uncovering nakedness (1/4 of limb for duration of one integral)
    14. Carrying unexcused filth
    15. Follower preceding imam without makeup
    16. Missing prayer integral without later makeup

    Scholarly Opinions

    Hanafi Madhab Positions:

    • Follower's silence: Complete silence during imam's recitation
    • Basmala: Recited silently before Fatiha in every rakah
    • Ameen: Said silently after Fatiha
    • Takbir: Imam says audibly, followers silently

    Areas of Scholarly Difference:

    • Whether followers should recite Fatiha behind imam
    • Audible vs silent recitation of various elements
    • Specific wording and practices of prayer components

    Lessons & Practical Application

    Key Practical Points:

    1. House analogy: Fard acts are the structure, sunnah acts beautify the prayer
    2. Gaze direction: Always maintain low gaze throughout prayer positions
    3. Movement guidelines: Avoid excessive movement that makes prayer unclear to observers
    4. Makeup prayers: When late to congregation, complete missed rakahs after imam's salam
    5. Error correction: Many mistakes can be remedied through sajdat sahw rather than invalidating prayer
    6. Intention matters: Actions done intentionally vs accidentally have different rulings

    Etiquette Guidelines:

    • Remove coverings from hands during opening takbir
    • Maintain proper gaze in each position (standing: place of sajda, ruku: feet, sajda: nose tip, sitting: lap, salam: shoulders)
    • Refrain from unnecessary coughing, cover mouth when yawning
    • Focus on meaning of recitations when not actively reciting (for followers)

    Common Scenarios:

    • Late arrival: Join imam's position, complete missed portions after his salam
    • Forgotten components: Use sajdat sahw for missed wajib acts
    • Congregational movement: Limited movement acceptable to close gaps in rows
    • Language considerations: Du'as must avoid resembling casual conversation
