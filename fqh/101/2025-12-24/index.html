    Fiqh 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-12-24

    Session Overview

    This session introduced the chapter on Hajj, covering the fundamental definition, requirements, and detailed procedures of the pilgrimage. The class focused on the three types of Hajj (Qiran, Tamattu', and Ifrad), the specific conditions required for obligation, and the step-by-step process of performing the Hajj rites from entering Ihram through the farewell Tawaf.

    Key Themes

    • Hajj as a journey requiring specific place (Makkah), state (Ihram), and time (days of Dhul Hijjah)
    • The urgency of performing Hajj when able - it is Fard and equally obligatory as Salah
    • Three distinct types of Hajj with different procedures
    • Detailed prohibitions and requirements while in the state of Ihram
    • The importance of avoiding harm to others during pilgrimage rites
    • Gender-specific differences in Hajj procedures

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    Ihram State of pilgrim sanctity
    Miqat Designated boundaries for entering Ihram
    Qiran Hajj type: Umrah and Hajj performed in one Ihram
    Tamattu' Hajj type: Umrah and Hajj performed in two separate Ihrams
    Ifrad Hajj type: Hajj only, no Umrah (for local residents)
    Talbiya "Labbayka Allahumma Labbayk..." - pilgrim's response to Allah
    I'tiba Placing upper garment under right armpit, exposing right shoulder
    Ramal Walking briskly with gentle shoulder movement (first 3 rounds of Tawaf)
    Hatim Semicircular enclosure that is part of the Kaaba
    Sa'i Walking/running between Safa and Marwa (7 rounds)
    Milayn Akhdarayn The two green lines where pilgrims rush during Sa'i
    Wuquf Standing at Arafah (most significant pillar of Hajj)
    Jamarat Stone pillars for stoning ritual
    Tawaf al-Qudum Welcome Tawaf upon arrival
    Tawaf al-Ziyarah Obligatory Tawaf of visitation
    Tawaf al-Wida Farewell Tawaf

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    Obligation of Hajj: - Fard once in a lifetime when able - Equal in obligation to daily Salah - neglecting it when able is like neglecting Fajr or Dhuhr - Must be performed "al-fawr" (immediately/as soon as possible) when conditions are met

    Essential Elements (Arkan): 1. Standing at Arafah (Wuquf) - most crucial pillar 2. Tawaf al-Ziyarah - second pillar

    Prayer Combinations During Hajj: - In Arafah: Dhuhr and Asr combined in Dhuhr time (one Adhan, two Iqamahs) - In Muzdalifah: Maghrib and Isha combined in Isha time (one Adhan, one Iqamah) - Only valid when praying with the main Imam according to Imam Abu Hanifa - Imam Abu Yusuf and Muhammad allow combination even when praying alone

    Conditions & Categories

    Conditions for Hajj Obligation: 1. Free (not enslaved) 2. Legally responsible (Mukallaf) 3. Able-bodied and healthy 4. Financial ability for provisions and transportation 5. Excess wealth beyond basic necessities and family support 6. Safe passage to Makkah 7. For women: not in Iddah, accompanied by husband or trustworthy Mahram

    Prohibitions in Ihram: 1. Intercourse or speaking about it inappropriately (Rafath) 2. Vulgar speech (Fusuq) 3. Argumentation (Jidal) 4. Acts of disobedience 5. Killing or indicating land game to hunters 6. Wearing stitched garments (men) 7. Covering head (men) or face (both genders) 8. Applying scent to body or clothes 9. Shaving or plucking hair

    Types of Hajj: 1. Qiran: Umrah and Hajj in one continuous Ihram 2. Tamattu': Umrah first, exit Ihram, then re-enter for Hajj 3. Ifrad: Hajj only (for Makkah residents)

    Scholarly Opinions

    Prayer Combination Differences: - Imam Abu Hanifa: Combination only valid with main Imam and in Ihram state - Imam Abu Yusuf & Muhammad: Combination allowed even when praying alone (stronger opinion)

    Touching the Black Stone: - Permissible only if done without harm to others - Caution needed due to fragrance (Itr) applied to it - touching would violate Ihram prohibitions - Can perform Istilam (greeting) from distance if crowded

    Lessons & Practical Application

    Daily Schedule of Hajj: - 8th Dhul Hijjah: Depart to Mina after Fajr, stay entire day - 9th Dhul Hijjah: To Arafah, perform Wuquf until sunset, then to Muzdalifah - 10th Dhul Hijjah: Fajr in Muzdalifah, collect 70 pebbles, return to Mina, stone largest Jamara (7 stones), sacrifice animal, shave head, perform Tawaf al-Ziyarah - 11th-12th Dhul Hijjah: Stone all three Jamarat (21 stones each day) after midday - 13th Dhul Hijjah (optional): Stone all three Jamarat, can be done after true dawn

    Practical Guidance: - Collect 70 pebbles total in Muzdalifah - Stop reciting Talbiya after throwing first stone on 10th - Everything becomes halal after head shaving except spousal relations - Spousal relations become permissible after Tawaf al-Ziyarah - Can leave after 12th stoning, but if staying past sunset of 12th until Fajr of 13th, must complete 13th day stoning

    Gender Differences: - Women don't uncover heads, use face covering with distance from face - Women recite Talbiya quietly to themselves - Women don't perform Ramal or rush between green lines - Women trim hair instead of shaving - Women may wear stitched garments - Women should avoid crowded areas around Black Stone
