    Fiqh 101 - Study Notes

    Date: 2025-10-06

    Session Overview

    This class continued the purification chapter, focusing on wells and water sources that may become impure, saliva categories affecting water purity, and concluded with the chapter on cleansing after relieving oneself (istinja). The session covered detailed rulings on when wells become impure based on what falls into them, how to purify wells based on animal sizes, and four categories of animal saliva affecting water purity. The class concluded with istibra (for males) and istinja procedures.

    Key Themes

    • Wells as water sources for purification and their contamination rules
    • Size-based categorization for well purification procedures
    • Saliva categories determining water usability for wudu and drinking
    • Proper etiquette and procedures for istinja (post-toilet cleansing)
    • Distinction between istibra (males) and general cleansing requirements
    • Practical applications of purity laws in daily life

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    Najasa Impurity, filth
    Istinja Cleansing after relieving oneself
    Istibra Removing remaining urine from male private parts (obligatory for men)
    Makruh Tahrimi Strongly disliked, bordering on forbidden
    Makruh Tanzihi Mildly disliked
    Janabah State of major ritual impurity
    Qibla Direction of prayer (toward Mecca)
    Wajib Obligatory
    Mustahab Recommended
    Yaqeen Certainty

    Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

    Small Wells

    • Ruling: Small wells become impure if any filth falls in, even without visible traces
    • Examples: Urine, blood, chicken droppings
    • Exception: Very small amounts of animal dung that observers don't consider "large"

    Things That DO NOT Make Wells Impure

    1. Pigeon or sparrow droppings
    2. Dead creatures without flowing blood (large mosquitoes, flies, wasps, scorpions)
    3. Dead sea creatures (born and residing in water - fish, crabs, frogs)
    4. Living creatures that fall in and come out alive (if no filth on body)

    Purification by Bucket Removal

    Based on animal size that died in well:

    1. Small animals (mouse-sized): Remove 20 buckets
    2. Medium animals (pigeon-sized): Remove 40 buckets
    3. Large animals: Remove ALL water
    4. Any bloated/fragmented animal (regardless of size): Remove ALL water
    5. If removing all water is difficult: Remove 200 buckets

    Note: Bucket size = average/customary size recognized in that locality

    Conditions & Categories

    Four Categories of Animal Saliva

    1. Pure and Purifying (Not Disliked)

    • Human saliva (clean mouth, even if in janabah or non-Muslim)
    • Horses
    • Animals whose meat is halal to eat

    2. Impure (Cannot Use)

    • Dogs, pigs
    • Predatory land animals (lions, wolves, hyenas, apes)
    • Exception: Can drink only in dire need (life-threatening thirst)

    3. Disliked (Makruh Tanzihi if alternatives available)

    • House cats
    • Released chickens (not enclosed, roaming freely)
    • Predatory birds (hawks, falcons)
    • Creatures roaming houses (mice, snakes)

    4. Doubtful Purifying Nature

    • Mules and donkeys
    • Ruling: If no other water available, make wudu then perform tayammum

    Scholarly Opinions

    Chickens Distinction

    • Released chickens: Disliked (roam and may consume filth)
    • Enclosed chickens: Not disliked (fed pure food, avoid filth except their own)

    Well Water vs. Large Bodies of Water

    • Small wells follow bucket removal rules
    • Large bodies of water: Can use different area if contamination is localized
    • Flowing water: Different area usage generally permissible

    Lessons & Practical Application

    Istibra (Males Only)

    • Obligation: Must ensure no urine drops remain in urethra
    • Methods: Walking, clearing throat, lying on side, gentle squeezing
    • Purpose: Prevent wudu invalidation from subsequent urine drops
    • When: Must be done before istinja

    Istinja Guidelines

    • Sunnah to clean after using restroom
    • Not required after passing gas (gas is not impure)
    • Procedure: Use toilet paper first, then wash with water
    • Direction: Wipe forward and backwards until clean
    • Goal: Remove bad smell and convince oneself area is clean

    Restroom Etiquette

    • Enter: Left foot first, seek Allah's protection
    • During: Don't face qibla, sun, or moon; avoid unnecessary speech
    • Exit: Right foot first, say "Alhamdulillahi alladhi adhaba anni al-adha..."
    • Makruh Tahrimi: Using right hand, bones, dry dung, food, valuable items (silk/cotton)

    Practical Notes

    • Next class begins with comprehensive test on entire purification chapter
    • Principle: When in doubt about purity but no original certainty existed, err on side of caution
    • Custom and locality determine "average bucket size" for well purification
