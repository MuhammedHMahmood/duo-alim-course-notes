    TFS 102 - Study Notes¶

    Date: 2024-02-22 Surah Covered: Surah Al-Asr (Chapter 103)

    Session Overview¶

    This session covers Surah Al-Asr, one of the earliest, shortest, and most comprehensive surahs of the Quran. The teacher explains the surah's structure, its roadmap for salvation, and the distinction between two levels of engaging with the Quran. A significant portion of the session also addresses theological questions from students regarding tawassul, istigatha, and related matters.

    Key Themes and Topics¶

    • The comprehensiveness of Surah Al-Asr despite its brevity
    • Two levels of engaging with the Quran: Tadhakkur vs. Tadabbur
    • The four-part formula for success and salvation
    • The significance of time (Al-Asr) as a witness
    • The universal reality of human loss
    • Theological discussion on Tawassul vs. Istigatha

    Main Tafseer Points and Explanations¶

    Structure of Surah Al-Asr¶

    • The entire surah is essentially two sentences divided into three ayat
    • Sentence 1: "Wal-Asr" (By time)
    • Sentence 2: "Inna al-insan la fi khusrin illa al-ladhina amanu wa amilu al-salihati wa tawasaw bil-haqqi wa tawasaw bil-sabr" (Indeed, mankind is in loss, except those who believe, do righteous deeds, enjoin truth, and enjoin patience)
    • The division of sentences into ayat is determined solely by Allah; there is no human-made rule for it

    The Surah Presents Three Elements¶

    1. An oath - "Wal-Asr" (By time)
    2. A universal rule (Qa'ida Kulliyya) - All of mankind is in loss
    3. One exception - Those who possess the four qualities

    The Oath: Why Time?¶

    • Time is the most essential element of existence; nothing other than Allah is timeless
    • Time never stops - it is "serial time" (one moment following another continuously)
    • Allah is the only being who is both spaceless and timeless, as He is the Creator of time
    • Different creations may experience time differently (e.g., time in the heavens vs. on earth)
    • Time serves as a witness testifying that mankind is in loss
    • The loss reaches its climax on the Day of Judgment

    The Universal Loss¶

    • Every human being is in a state of loss
    • This is evident in the constant struggle of life: education, career, family, emotional hardships
    • On one's deathbed, people realize they did not achieve what they could have
    • Hadith reference: On the Day of Judgment, people will wish they had endured more hardship in the dunya in exchange for reward

    The Four Components of Success (The Exception)¶

    These are the minimum requirements for salvation, not the maximum:

    1. Iman (Right belief/ideology/aqeedah) - Having the correct faith and understanding
    2. Amal Salih (Right practice) - Righteous deeds based on that correct belief
    3. Tawasaw bil-Haq (Enjoining truth) - Having the courage to speak the truth

    4. Tawasaw bil-Sabr (Enjoining patience) - Having patience when facing challenges that arise from speaking the truth

    5. These four are compared to a medical prescription: all four medicines must be taken together for them to work; dropping even one renders the treatment ineffective

    6. They are also compared to a passing grade: 60% is the minimum; scoring 59% means failure regardless of how close it is

    Two Levels of Engaging with the Quran¶

    Level 1: Tadhakkur (Seeking Guidance)¶

    • Based on the ayah from Surah Al-Qamar: "Wa laqad yassarna al-Qur'ana lil-dhikri fa hal min muddakir" (We have made the Quran easy for remembrance, so is there anyone who will take heed?)
    • This is the basic level accessible to everyone, even those with no scholarly background
    • Simply reading the Quran (even in translation) and drawing basic guidance from it
    • Many people, including non-Muslims, have accepted Islam through this level
    • The Quran is made easy for this purpose
    • Important clarification: Allah says the Quran is easy for dhikr (remembrance/advice), NOT easy to "understand" in the scholarly sense

    Level 2: Tadabbur (Contemplation)¶

    • The highest level of learning from the Quran
    • Requires a solid academic foundation:
    • Knowledge of the Arabic language
    • Knowledge of ahadith related to Quranic interpretation
    • Knowledge of the statements (aqwal) of the Sahaba
    • Knowledge of the traditions (athar) of the Salaf
    • The serious problem arises when unqualified people attempt tadabbur instead of remaining at tadhakkur, potentially misguiding themselves and others

    Addressing the Atheist's Objection¶

    • Objection: The Quran contradicts itself because it claims to be "easy" (Surah Al-Qamar) yet also says some ayat are known only to Allah (Surah Al-Imran regarding mutashabihat)
    • Response: Allah never said the Quran is easy to understand; He said it is easy for dhikr (remembrance). Even reciting Alif Lam Meem without understanding its meaning constitutes dhikr of Allah. Understanding (tadabbur) and seeking guidance (tadhakkur) are two different things.

    Historical Context and Anecdotes¶

    The Practice of the Sahaba¶

    • The Companions used to recite Surah Al-Asr to each other after greeting, as a reminder to practice its teachings

    Imam Shafi'i's Statement¶

    • Imam Shafi'i (rahimahullah) said that if Allah had revealed nothing in the Quran except Surah Al-Asr, it would have been sufficient for guidance

    State of Tafseer in American Masajid (1990s)¶

    • In the 1990s, many masajid in America had unqualified individuals (often wealthy board members or doctors with no Islamic scholarly training) conducting tafseer sessions
    • The teacher recalls attending tarawih in St. Louis (circa 1997-1999) where a man in his late 60s was making major errors in Quranic recitation while conducting tafseer
    • This situation has improved significantly with the presence of many qualified ulema in America today

    Theological Discussion: Tawassul and Istigatha¶

    Definitions¶

    • Tawassul: Asking Allah by the virtue/status of a righteous person (e.g., "O Allah, accept my dua for the sake of Rasulullah"). This is considered ja'iz (permissible).
    • Istigatha: Directly asking someone other than Allah for something. This is considered shirk.

    Key Evidence and Arguments¶

    • Hadith from Sahih al-Bukhari: Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA) sought the wasilah (intercession) of Abbas ibn Abdul Muttalib (RA) during salat al-istisqa (prayer for rain) after the Prophet's death
    • The Salafi position holds that wasilah is only permissible through a living person (since Abbas was alive)
    • The Ash'ari and Maturidi position argues there is no difference between living and dead in this matter, since both are "other than Allah" (ghayrullah); if wasilah were shirk, it would be shirk in both cases

    Are the Prophets Alive After Death?¶

    • This connects to the question of whether Anbiya are alive in their graves (in Barzakh)
    • Both sides have hadith evidence; this is a matter of legitimate scholarly disagreement
    • One cannot excommunicate another Muslim from Ahl al-Sunnah based on their position on this issue
    • If the Prophets are alive (in Barzakh), then seeking their wasilah is analogous to seeking wasilah of a living person

    Practical Guidelines¶

    • At the grave of the Prophet: One may greet him and ask him to make dua to Allah (since the hadith states that the Prophet hears the salam of visitors)
    • Away from the grave: One should address Allah directly, saying "O Allah, for the sake of Rasulullah, accept my dua." Calling upon the Prophet from a distance would be bid'ah.
    • Asking a non-Prophet in his grave to make dua: The teacher advises against this practice

    Regarding Salihin (Pious People) in Graves¶

    • Asking a deceased pious person (non-prophet) to directly do something for you is shirk
    • Every believer is alive in Barzakh after death, but the application of wasilah to non-prophets is more restricted

    Other Fiqh Questions Addressed¶

    Repentance for Major Sins (Gunah Kabira)¶

    • If a person repents sincerely with a firm determination not to repeat the sin, Allah accepts tawbah
    • It is NOT necessary that a person who committed a major sin must go to Jahannam even after repentance
    • Evidence: Allah says He forgives all sins (except shirk, if He wills) - Surah Al-Zumar

    Breaking a Nafl (Voluntary) Fast Early by Mistake¶

    • If someone misreads the time and breaks a voluntary fast before Maghrib (e.g., 15 minutes early), they must make up the fast

    Lessons and Practical Takeaways¶

    1. Surah Al-Asr is a complete life program - Despite being one of the shortest surahs, it provides the essential roadmap for success in this life and the hereafter

    2. The four pillars of success are inseparable - Iman, righteous deeds, enjoining truth, and patience must all be present together; dropping any one renders the others insufficient

    3. These are minimum requirements, not maximum - One should strive to exceed these four baseline qualities, but at minimum all four must be present

    4. Know your level when approaching the Quran - Ordinary Muslims should engage in tadhakkur (seeking guidance); tadabbur (deep contemplation and deriving rulings) requires extensive scholarly training

    5. Unqualified tafseer is dangerous - Speaking about the Quran without proper knowledge can lead to misguidance of self and others

    6. Time is humanity's most precious and unstoppable resource - It testifies against those who waste it; use it to build the four qualities of success

    7. Theological differences require knowledge and nuance - Issues like tawassul, the life of prophets in Barzakh, and similar topics have legitimate scholarly disagreement; one should not rush to label others based on superficial understanding

    8. Sincere repentance is accepted - No sin (other than shirk) is beyond Allah's forgiveness when repentance is sincere and accompanied by firm resolve not to repeat the sin
