    TFS 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2024-02-08 Surah Covered: Surah Al-Qari'ah (Chapter 101)

    Session Overview

    This session covers the complete tafseer of Surah Al-Qari'ah, which describes the events of the Day of Judgment -- the striking calamity, the state of people and mountains on that day, and the weighing of deeds that determines one's eternal fate.

    Key Themes and Topics

    • The Day of Judgment as a sudden, terrifying, and inescapable event
    • The imagery of cosmic destruction: people scattered like moths, mountains like wool
    • The connection between Surah Al-Qari'ah, Surah Al-Zilzal, and Surah Al-Adiyat
    • The weighing of deeds on the Day of Judgment (Mizan)
    • The two possible outcomes: pleasant life (Jannah) or the Pit (Hawiyah)
    • The concept that death itself initiates one's personal Qiyamah

    Important Vocabulary

    Main Tafseer Points and Explanations

    The Meaning of "Al-Qari'ah" (Ayat 1-3)

    • Al-Qari'ah is an adjective (sifah); the described noun is hidden. The full meaning is "Al-Musibatul Qari'ah" (the striking calamity) or "As-Sa'atul Qari'ah" (the striking hour).
    • Allah repeats the word Al-Qari'ah three times in the opening ayat to draw attention to the extreme seriousness of this event.
    • The phrase "wa ma adraaka" (and what will make you know) was previously explained in Surah Al-Qadr. When Allah uses this phrase in the past tense (adraaka), He follows it with an answer. When He uses the present tense (yudreeka), He does not answer -- the reader must reflect on the answer himself.

    The Four Implications of "Qari'ah" -- Why This Word Was Chosen

    The word Qari'ah (derived from knocking a door hard at night) captures four things that happen simultaneously:

    1. Unexpected arrival -- The visitor was not anticipated; similarly, the Day of Judgment will come when people are not expecting it.
    2. Disturbance -- Just as a loud knock in the middle of the night disrupts you, Qiyamah will disrupt everyone's daily routine (hadith: people will be in the middle of milking cows, painting, breastfeeding, etc.).
    3. Fear -- The knock causes fright; Qiyamah will bring an unprecedented level of terror.
    4. Disoriented movement -- You run around trying to see who is at the door; people will run around aimlessly trying to save their lives, but no one will be saved.

    Connection to Previous Surahs

    • Connection to Surah Al-Zilzal: Both surahs describe events of the Day of Judgment.
    • Connection to Surah Al-Adiyat: The end of Al-Adiyat says secrets concealed in hearts will be exposed. This naturally raises the question: what will that day be like? Surah Al-Qari'ah answers this.

    People Like Scattered Moths (Ayah 4)

    • "Kal-farashi al-mabthuth" -- People will be like scattered moths.
    • The resemblance is precise: moths near a fire fly in all directions with no order, no coordination, and they fall into the fire, which kills them.
    • Similarly, people will be scattered with no order, unable to plan or coordinate, running in all directions, and ultimately perishing.
    • This is describing the moment of destruction on earth, not the Hellfire (which comes later).

    Grammar Note on "Yawma" (Ayah 4)

    • When yawma is mudaf (annexed) to a full sentence, there are two grammatical options:
    • Mabni 'ala al-fath (built on fathah) -- yawma
    • Given a haraka based on the governing agent ('amil) -- yawmu
    • Both readings are valid according to the rules covered in Tasheel al-Nahw under Zuruf.

    Mountains Like Fluffed Wool (Ayah 5)

    • "Kal-'ihni al-manfush" -- Mountains will become like fluffed-up wool.
    • Allah mentions mountains because they are the largest things on Earth. If even mountains cannot survive, nothing can.
    • The mountains will be reduced to dust particles scattered in space, just like asteroids -- remnants of destroyed celestial bodies.
    • This event happens after all people have already died; no one will be alive to witness it.

    The Weighing of Deeds (Ayat 6-11)

    • Heavy scales (good deeds outweigh bad): The person will have a pleasant life ('ishah radiyah).
    • Light scales (good deeds are light): The person's abode (ummuhu) will be Hawiyah -- a valley/pit of Hellfire.
    • Hawiyah is further described: "wa ma adraaka ma hiyah? Narun hamiyah" -- an intensely hot fire.
    • The word "umm" here means abode/destination (not "mother"), though both meanings exist in Arabic.
    • The grammatical note: "ma hiyah" -- originally "ma hiya" (hiya is a detached pronoun), but "ha" is added at the end for rhyme, which is common in Arabic.

    Spiritual Gravity

    • On a day when physical gravitational pull ceases (leading to cosmic destruction), the only "gravity" that matters is the weight of one's deeds -- a spiritual gravity.
    • Good deeds create weight on the scale; this is the ruhani (spiritual) gravity.

    Lessons and Practical Takeaways

    1. Death is your personal Qiyamah. The Prophet (SAW) said: "One who dies, his Qiyamah has already been established." No one should assume the Day of Judgment is far off -- it arrives for each person at death.

    2. Signs of the Day of Judgment have already appeared. Examples mentioned: people who were once shepherds now compete in building high-rises. Yet most people do not change their behavior despite seeing these signs fulfilled.

    3. Prepare your scales now. The only thing that will matter on that day is the weight of one's good deeds versus bad deeds. There will be no second chance to add to one's record after death.

    4. The Quran's precision in word choice. A single word (Al-Qari'ah) encapsulates an entire scenario -- unexpected arrival, disturbance, fear, and disoriented flight. This demonstrates the miraculous nature of Quranic language.

    5. No escape from Allah's decree. People will try to save themselves, but nothing -- not houses, caves, or bunkers -- will provide shelter from the destruction.

    6. The destruction is universal. It will not be limited to Earth; the heavens and the earth will all be folded up (reference to the ayah: "yawma natwi as-samawati wal-ard").

    Historical Context and Additional Discussions

    The Location of Mahshar

    • The location of the gathering place (Mahshar) is unknown and is from the knowledge of the unseen ('ilm al-ghayb). Allah has not disclosed this, and knowing the "address" of Mahshar serves no practical purpose.

    Isra and Mi'raj -- Unique Blessing of the Prophet (SAW)

    • No prophet was granted the closeness to Allah that the Prophet Muhammad (SAW) received during Mi'raj.
    • Adam (AS) was in Jannah, but Jannah is not the same as being near the Arsh.
    • Musa (AS) was Kalim Allah (spoke to Allah), but he spoke to Allah on Earth, not on Mi'raj.
    • This blessing is exclusive (khas) to the Prophet Muhammad (SAW).

    Adam (AS) as a Prophet

    • A hadith explicitly states that Adam (AS) was the first prophet. A Sahabi asked the Prophet (SAW) and was told: "Yes, he was the first prophet."
    • Further proof: In the hadith of intercession on the Day of Judgment, people first approach Adam (AS) to intercede -- they would not approach him if he were not a prophet.

    Dinosaurs and Islam

    • The existence of dinosaurs does not contradict any ayah of the Quran or hadith of the Prophet (SAW).
    • Time before Adam (AS) is irrelevant to the discussion because Allah is beyond time; there is no "before and after" for Allah.
    • What is rejected: evolutionary claims about human origins (homo sapiens evolving from earlier species) -- this contradicts Islamic texts about Adam's creation.
    • Dinosaurs themselves? No conflict with Islamic sources.

    Jinn Possession

    • Confirmed: Jinn can possess humans.

    Ruqyah (Spiritual Healing)

    • Blowing (nafth) after reciting Quranic ayat or prescribed du'as is established in the Quran (Surah Al-Falaq) and in the Hadith.
    • Placing one's hand on the head during recitation: no specific evidence (daleel) was found for this practice.

    Jannah and Jahannam During Qiyamah

    • There is scholarly discussion on whether Jannah and Jahannam will be temporarily "given death" during the destruction.
    • Some scholars say they will be destroyed temporarily; others say they will not, since they are not located on Earth.

    Prayer Direction in Space

    • An astronaut in space should pray facing the Earth.
    • If humans were on Mars, they would face Earth, and the qiblah direction would change as Earth moves.

    2 Mal-Qari'ah What is the Striking Calamity?
    3 Wa ma adraaka mal-Qari'ah And what will make you know what it is?
    4 Yawma yakunun-nasu kal-farashi al-mabthuth The day people will be like scattered moths
    5 Wa takunul-jibalu kal-'ihni al-manfush And mountains will be like fluffed-up wool
