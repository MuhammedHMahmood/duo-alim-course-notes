    Sarf 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-06-12

    Session Overview

    This session focused on the governing particles (حروف عاملة) that affect the فعل مضارع (present/future tense verb) — specifically the حروف جازمة (jazm-giving particles) and their effects on the 14 verb patterns. The class covered:

    • Review of non-human plural treated as feminine singular (a Nahw rule)
    • How جازم particles render the مضارع مجزوم (with sukoon or dropped letter)
    • The نون الإعراب (noon of i'rab) and when it drops
    • لام الأمر (lam of command) for 3rd and 1st person
    • لا النهي (la of prohibition) as a negative command
    • لَمْ and لَمَّا and their specific meanings
    • Weak-letter (حروف العلة) verbs and how jazm affects them
    • The distinction between حرف جر and لام العامل

    Word Patterns (Awzan) Covered

    The Five Patterns Affected by حروف ناصبة / حروف جازمة

    From the 14 entries of the صرف table, five patterns are directly affected by the vowel change (the remaining ones are governed through نون الإعراب):

    Pattern Example (base) After جازم (e.g., لَمْ)
    يَفْعَلُ (3rd m. sg.) يَفْعَلُ لَمْ يَفْعَلْ
    تَفْعَلُ (3rd f. sg.) تَفْعَلُ لَمْ تَفْعَلْ
    تَفْعَلُ (2nd m. sg.) تَفْعَلُ لَمْ تَفْعَلْ
    أَفْعَلُ (1st sg.) أَفْعَلُ لَمْ أَفْعَلْ
    نَفْعَلُ (1st pl.) نَفْعَلُ لَمْ نَفْعَلْ

    The last letter receives سكون (sukoon) / jazm in all five cases.

    The Seven Patterns Where نون الإعراب Drops

    For the remaining seven patterns (those whose مرفوع form ends in نون as the sign of رفع), the نون drops when preceded by a جازم or ناصب particle:

    • يَفْعَلُونَ → لَمْ يَفْعَلُوا (with protective الف)
    • تَفْعَلُونَ → لَمْ تَفْعَلُوا
    • يَفْعَلَانِ → لَمْ يَفْعَلَا
    • تَفْعَلَانِ → لَمْ تَفْعَلَا
    • تَفْعَلِينَ → لَمْ تَفْعَلِي
    • And the corresponding dual/plural patterns

    The Two Patterns Where نون Does NOT Drop

    These two patterns retain their noon even when governed by a جازم:

    Pattern Description Example
    يَفْعَلْنَ 3rd person feminine plural لَمْ يَفْعَلْنَ
    تَفْعَلْنَ 2nd person feminine plural لَمْ تَفْعَلْنَ

    Reason: The نون here is نون النسوة (noon of femininity), not نون الإعراب. Dropping it would cause confusion with other patterns (it would merge with يَفْعَلْ). Although the noon is retained, these verbs are still grammatically مجزوم — the jazm is present but not visibly marked (مجزوم في محل / مجزوم بسبب حرف الجازمة). This is analogous to مُوسى, which never changes its written form but is still considered مرفوع / منصوب / مجرور depending on its grammatical role.

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    حروف Plural of حرف (particle/letter)
    حرف Singular: a particle or letter
    حروف جازمة Jazm-giving particles (render the مضارع مجزوم)
    حروف ناصبة Nasb-giving particles (render the مضارع منصوب with فتحة)
    حروف عاملة / عوامل Governing particles — those that act upon (exert grammatical influence over) what follows
    مجزوم In the state of jazm (sukoon on final letter, or dropped نون / حرف علة)
    منصوب In the state of nasb (فتحة on final letter, or dropped نون)
    مرفوع In the state of raf' (ضمة on final letter, or presence of نون)
    نون الإعراب The noon that signals the grammatical state (رفع) in certain مضارع patterns
    نون النسوة The noon of femininity (never dropped)
    لام الأمر The lam of command — used to issue commands to 3rd and 1st persons
    لا النهي The prohibiting lا — used for negative commands (prohibition)
    لَمْ Negates the مضارع and pushes its meaning into the past ("did not")
    لَمَّا "Not yet" — negates with implication that the action is still expected
    حروف العلة Weak letters: و، ي، ا (Alif, Waw, Ya)
    ألف مقصورة The standing alif written as ى (without dots)
    مجهول Passive voice
    معروف Active voice
    مصدر Verbal noun / infinitive-like noun
    مصدر مشتق A derived verbal noun
    تقييد / تأكيد Emphasis/affirmation
    نون التوكيد الثقيلة Heavy noon of emphasis (نّ)
    نون التوكيد الخفيفة Light noon of emphasis (نْ)
    مُرَكَّب Compound phrase (can stand in place of an اسم)
    مُضاف إليه The second noun in an إضافة (genitive) phrase
    مركب توصيفي Adjectival/descriptive phrase (adjective-noun phrase)

    لام الأمر + يَرْكَعُ (to bow/do rukoo')

    Person Base مضارع With لام الأمر
    3rd m. sg. يَرْكَعُ لِيَرْكَعْ
    3rd f. sg. تَرْكَعُ لِتَرْكَعْ
    3rd m. dual يَرْكَعَانِ لِيَرْكَعَا
    3rd f. dual تَرْكَعَانِ لِتَرْكَعَا
    3rd m. pl. يَرْكَعُونَ لِيَرْكَعُوا
    3rd f. pl. يَرْكَعْنَ لِيَرْكَعْنَ
    1st sg. أَرْكَعُ لِأَرْكَعْ
    1st pl. نَرْكَعُ لِنَرْكَعْ

    (The عين receives sukoon in all cases where the ending is not a weak letter or نون نسوة)

    Exercise Conjugations from Class (using لام الأمر)

    Root Verb Conjugated Form Note
    يَفْجُرُ (to transgress) لِيَفْجُرْ ضمة on ر drops → سكون
    يَرْكَعُ (to bow) لِيَرْكَعْ فتحة on ع drops → سكون
    يَعْبُدُ (to worship) لِيَعْبُدْ ضمة on د drops → سكون
    يَمْلِكُ (to own/possess) لِيَمْلِكْ كسرة on ك drops → سكون

    Weak-Letter Verb Examples (حروف العلة dropped by جازم)

    Original مضارع Root letters Effect of جازم Result
    يَخْشَى خ-ش-ي Alif maqsoorah dropped لَمْ يَخْشَ
    يَرْمِي ر-م-ي ياء dropped لَمْ يَرْمِ (kasra on م retained to mark dropped ي)
    يَدْعُو د-ع-و واو dropped لَمْ يَدْعُ (damma on ع retained to mark dropped و)

    Key principle: When a jazm-giving particle precedes a مضارع whose final letter is a حرف علة (و، ي، or ألف مقصورة), that weak letter is completely deleted. The remaining vowel on the letter before it signals what letter was originally there.

    Root Analysis

    The 14 Entries and How Commands Are Distributed

    The 14 entries of the صرف table break down as: - 6 patterns for 2nd person (المخاطب) — these use the direct أمر form (command conjugation); no لام needed - 6 patterns for 3rd person (الغائب) — command issued via لام الأمر - 2 patterns for 1st person (المتكلم) — command issued via لام الأمر

    This is why لام الأمر is essential: the direct أمر form only exists for the 2nd person. To issue a command to an absent (3rd) person or to include oneself (1st person), لام الأمر + مضارع is the grammatical tool.

    لَمْ vs. لَمَّا

    Particle Meaning Effect Example
    لَمْ "did not" (past negation) Gives jazm; pushes مضارع meaning into past لَمْ يَفْعَلْ = "he did not do"
    لَمَّا "not yet" (negation with expectation) Gives jazm; implies action is still pending لَمَّا يَفْعَلْ = "he has not yet done (but is expected to)"

    لام الأمر vs. لا النهي

    Tool Arabic Meaning Example
    لام الأمر لِ + مضارع Positive command (3rd/1st person) لِيَفْعَلْ = "let him do / he should do"
    لا النهي لَا + مضارع Prohibition / negative command لَا يَفْعَلْ = "let him not do / he must not do"

    Note on لام الأمر voweling: لام الأمر always carries a كسرة (kasra). The لام with فتحة (لَ) is the لام of affirmation/emphasis (e.g., لَأَذْهَبَنَّ = "I will surely go"), a completely different particle.

    The حرف جر Rule — A Critical Distinction

    حرف الجر can ONLY precede an اسم (or a phrase acting in place of an اسم). It can NEVER precede a فعل directly.

    The لام in لِيَفْعَلَ is not حرف الجر — it is لام العامل (a governing particle). The distinction: - If you claim something is حرف جر, you must be able to identify the اسم following it - No اسم follows لِ in لِيَفْعَلَ — the فعل follows directly - Therefore it is لام الأمر, a governing particle from the حروف جازمة category - What can follow حرف جر after a verb: convert the verb into مصدر form (verbal noun), then حرف جر applies to that مصدر

    Practice Examples

    Identifying Jazm in Weak-Letter Verbs

    Q: In لَمْ يَخْشَ — the verb appears to end in only two letters (يَخْشَ). Where is the third root letter?

    A: The third root letter is ي (from خ-ش-ي). The مضارع was originally يَخْشَى (with ألف مقصورة). When لَمْ preceded it, the ألف مقصورة was deleted as the sign of jazm. The student must consult the dictionary to confirm the original pattern.

    Q: In لَمْ يَرْمِ — why does the مeem have a kasra if the verb is مجزوم?

    A: The original form was يَرْمِي. The ياء was deleted by jazm. The kasra on the مeem is not the i'rab vowel — it is a remnant marker indicating the deleted ياء. Reading it as sukoon on the مeem would be wrong because one cannot drop two letters simultaneously.

    Applying لام الأمر

    Given يَعْبُدُ (he worships):

    • لِيَعْبُدْ — let him worship (3rd m. sg.)
    • لِيَعْبُدُوا — let them worship (3rd m. pl.) — نون dropped, protective الف added
    • لِأَعْبُدْ — let me worship (1st sg.)
    • لِنَعْبُدْ — let us worship (1st pl.)

    Non-Human Plural + Feminine Singular Agreement (Nahw connection)

    Example phrase: حروف جازمة

    • حروف = plural (non-human)
    • جازمة = feminine singular adjective

    Why? Non-human plurals are grammatically treated as feminine singular in Arabic. Therefore the adjective/attribute agrees as feminine singular (مفرد مؤنث), not as a masculine plural.

    Key Takeaways

    1. حروف جازمة render the مضارع مجزوم — the sign of jazm is: sukoon on the final letter (for the 5 base patterns), or dropping of نون الإعراب (for the 7 noon-bearing patterns).

    2. نون النسوة is never dropped — even when governed by a جازم. These verbs are still مجزوم grammatically, but the jazm is "hidden" (مجزوم محلاً), just as مُوسى is always written the same but changes grammatical role depending on context.

    3. لام الأمر vs. لا النهي: لام الأمر (with kasra) = positive command for 3rd and 1st persons. لا النهي = prohibition/negative command for all persons. Both are حروف جازمة.

    4. لَمْ vs. لَمَّا: لَمْ simply negates (past meaning). لَمَّا means "not yet" — the action is negated but still anticipated.

    5. Weak-letter verbs lose their حرف علة under jazm: ألف مقصورة, ياء, or واو at the end of a مضارع is completely deleted when a جازم governs it. The remaining vowel on the preceding letter is a trace indicating which letter was dropped.

    6. حرف جر ONLY governs اسم — never فعل. Whenever you identify something as حرف جر, you must be able to point to the اسم it governs. The لام in لِيَفْعَلَ is لام العامل (governing particle), not حرف جر.

    7. The 14 patterns and command distribution: Direct أمر form exists only for 2nd person (6 patterns). For 3rd person (6 patterns) and 1st person (2 patterns), لام الأمر + مضارع is the mechanism.

    8. Dropping نون with ناصب vs. جازم: The نون of إعراب drops with both حروف ناصبة AND حروف جازمة, but the resulting state is different — منصوب (nasb, فتحة meaning) vs. مجزوم (jazm, sukoon meaning). The visual form may look the same; the grammatical ruling differs based on which governing particle precedes it.

    9. The grammarians' methodology: The rules of Nahw and Sarf were derived by the early Muslim grammarians (مصرّفون/نحاة) through data analysis of the language — scanning the Quran and Arabic literature, identifying patterns, and codifying them. These rules are descriptive of how the language works, not prescriptive inventions.
