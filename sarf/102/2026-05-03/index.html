    Sarf 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-05-03

    Session Overview

    This session began the study of "Min Kunuz as-Sarf" (From the Treasures of Arabic Morphology). The class focused on analyzing the book's title to demonstrate proper Arabic grammatical analysis, covering fundamental morphological concepts including mudaf constructions, plural patterns, verb classifications, and the distinction between different types of negation and commands. The instructor emphasized the importance of understanding grammatical relationships between words rather than just memorizing vocabulary.

    Word Patterns (Awzan) Covered

    Plural Patterns: - Kanz → Kunuz (treasure → treasures) - Qalb → Qulub (heart → hearts)
    - Asl → Usul (principle → principles)

    Morphological Analysis Patterns: - Fa'ala pattern structure: fa kalima, ain kalima, lam kalima (first letter, middle letter, last letter) - Mudaf-Mudaf Ilayhi constructions demonstrated in "Kunuz as-Sarf"

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    Min From
    Man Who (relative pronoun)
    Kanz Treasure
    Kunuz Treasures (plural)
    Sarf Morphology/patterns
    Mudaf First part of possessive construction
    Mudaf Ilayhi Second part of possessive construction
    Mutaharrik Letter with haraka (vowel mark)
    Maskun Letter without haraka
    Haraka Vowel mark (fatha, kasra, dhamma)
    Fa'il Subject/doer
    Maf'ul bihi Direct object
    Mutaadi Transitive verb (needs object)
    Lazim Intransitive verb (doesn't need object)
    Ma'ruf Active voice
    Majhul Passive voice
    Amr Positive command
    Nahi Negative command/prohibition
    Nafi Simple negation

    Verb Conjugation Tables

    Voice Classifications: - Ma'ruf (Active Voice): Subject explicitly mentioned - Majhul (Passive Voice): Subject omitted or unknown

    Verb Types by Object Requirements: - Mutaadi (Transitive): Requires direct object - Example: Kataba (wrote) - "wrote what?" - Lazim (Intransitive): Does not require direct object
    - Example: Dhahaba (went) - complete meaning without object

    Root Analysis

    Book Title Analysis: "Min Kunuz as-Sarf" - Min: Preposition meaning "from" - Kunuz: Plural of "kanz" (treasure), mudaf (first part of possessive) - as-Sarf: "The morphology," mudaf ilayhi (second part of possessive) - Translation: "From the Treasures of Arabic Morphology"

    Key Grammatical Points: - Mudaf cannot have "al" prefix - Mudaf ilayhi cannot have tanwin - Context determines whether مِن is "min" (from) or "man" (who)

    Practice Examples

    Mudaf Constructions: - Kunuz as-Sarf (treasures of morphology) - Usul al-Fiqh (principles of jurisprudence) - Usul at-Tafsir (principles of exegesis)

    Command vs Negation Examples: - Amr (Positive Command): If'al (do it) - Nahi (Prohibition): La taf'al (don't do it) - Nafi (Simple Negation): La ilaha illa Allah (there is no god but Allah)

    Typography Notes: - ar-Rahman (الرحمن) - no alif written after ra - ar-Rahim (الرحيم) - alif written after ra due to different vowel pattern

    Key Takeaways

    1. Analytical Approach: Every word in Arabic must connect meaningfully within phrases and sentences
    2. Context is Crucial: Grammar analysis requires understanding word relationships, not just individual meanings
    3. Pattern Recognition: Understanding morphological patterns (like plural formations) is more important than memorizing individual vocabulary
    4. Three Types of Words: Ism (noun), Fi'l (verb), and Harf (particle)
    5. Question-Based Learning: Students should actively ask questions to deepen understanding
    6. Structural Understanding: Focus on grammatical relationships rather than just translation
    7. Command Classifications: Distinguish between amr (positive command), nahi (negative command), and nafi (simple negation)
