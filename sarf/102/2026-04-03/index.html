    Sarf 102 - Study Notes

    Date: 2026-04-03

    Session Overview

    Post-Ramadan review session focusing on fundamental three-letter verb patterns in Arabic morphology. The class emphasized understanding why Sarf is studied separately from Nahw, and how three-letter verb combinations transform from past tense (madhi) to present tense (mudari). Key focus on the six valid combinations out of nine theoretical possibilities, and proper dictionary usage for determining harakat (vowel markings).

    Word Patterns (Awzan) Covered

    Three-Letter Verb Patterns (Past Tense - Madhi):

    1. فَعَلَ (fa'ala) - middle letter has fatha
    2. فَعِلَ (fa'ila) - middle letter has kasra
    3. فَعُلَ (fa'ula) - middle letter has dhamma

    Present Tense (Mudari) Possibilities:

    • From فَعَلَ: يَفْعُلُ (yaf'ulu), يَفْعَلُ (yaf'alu), يَفْعِلُ (yaf'ilu) - all three harakat possible
    • From فَعِلَ: يَفْعَلُ (yaf'alu), يَفْعِلُ (yaf'ilu) only - dhamma not allowed
    • From فَعُلَ: يَفْعُلُ (yaf'ulu) only - same haraka maintained

    Important Vocabulary

    Arabic Term Meaning
    صرف (Sarf) Morphology/word patterns
    نحو (Nahw) Grammar/sentence structure
    ماضي (Madhi) Past tense
    مضارع (Mudari) Present/future tense
    حركات (Harakat) Vowel markings
    فتحة (Fatha) Short 'a' vowel
    كسرة (Kasra) Short 'i' vowel
    ضمة (Dhamma) Short 'u' vowel
    فعل (Fi'l) Verb
    اسم (Ism) Noun
    حرف (Harf) Particle
    مذكر (Mudhakkar) Masculine
    مؤنث (Mu'annath) Feminine
    مصدر (Masdar) Verbal noun

    Verb Conjugation Tables

    Six Valid Combinations (Active Voice, Third Person Masculine Singular):

    Past Tense Present Tense Example Dictionary Form
    فَعَلَ يَفْعُلُ كَتَبَ → يَكْتُبُ kataba/yaktubu
    فَعَلَ يَفْعَلُ فَتَحَ → يَفْتَحُ fataha/yaftahu
    فَعَلَ يَفْعِلُ ضَرَبَ → يَضْرِبُ daraba/yadribu
    فَعِلَ يَفْعَلُ سَمِعَ → يَسْمَعُ sami'a/yasma'u
    فَعِلَ يَفْعِلُ حَسِبَ → يَحْسِبُ hasiba/yahsibu
    فَعُلَ يَفْعُلُ رَحُبَ → يَرْحُبُ rahuba/yarhubu

    Rule Summary:

    • Past tense: First and last letters always have fatha
    • Middle letter variations: Determines present tense possibilities
    • Nine theoretical combinations reduced to six: Language doesn't allow فَعِلَ → يَفْعُلُ, فَعُلَ → يَفْعَلُ, or فَعُلَ → يَفْعِلُ

    Root Analysis

    Three-Letter Root System:

    • Most Arabic verbs built on three-letter roots (approximately 70-80%)
    • فعل (fa'l) as reference pattern: ف (fa) = 1st letter, ع (ayn) = 2nd letter, ل (lam) = 3rd letter
    • Root letters can be any Arabic letters - فعل is just a placeholder system
    • Each root carries core meaning that extends through all derived forms

    Examples Analyzed:

    • ك-ت-ب (k-t-b): Writing/recording
    • ن-ص-ر (n-s-r): Helping/supporting
    • ع-ل-م ('a-l-m): Knowing/learning

    Practice Examples

    Dictionary Usage Examples:

    كتب (kataba) - Past: كَتَبَ (kataba) - he wrote - Present: يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu) - he writes/will write - Masdar: كِتَابَة (kitaba) - writing

    نصر (nasara) - Past: نَصَرَ (nasara) - he helped - Present: يَنْصُرُ (yansuru) - he helps/will help - Masdar: نَصْر (nasr) - help

    علم ('alima) - Past: عَلِمَ ('alima) - he knew - Present: يَعْلَمُ (ya'lamu) - he knows/will know - Masdar: عِلْم ('ilm) - knowledge

    Advanced Patterns:

    رحب (rahiba/rahuba) - رَحِبَ (rahiba) → يَرْحَبُ (yarhabu) - to be spacious - رَحُبَ (rahuba) → يَرْحُبُ (yarhubu) - to be wide

    Key Takeaways

    1. Foundation First: Sarf deals with word patterns (building blocks), while Nahw governs how these blocks combine into sentences

    2. Six Sacred Combinations: Memorize the six valid past-to-present transformations - this is fundamental to reading classical texts and Quran

    3. Dictionary Skills Essential: Learn to read Arabic dictionaries to determine correct harakat, especially for middle letters in verbs

    4. Gender and Number Awareness: Everything in Arabic has gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/dual/plural)

    5. Practice Reading: Regular exposure to correctly vowelized texts builds intuitive understanding of patterns

    6. Root Recognition: Understanding three-letter roots unlocks vocabulary expansion and meaning connections

    7. No Guesswork: Harakat placement follows rules - if unknown, consult dictionary rather than guess

    8. Active Learning Required: Weekly meetings demand consistent review of recordings and materials between classes

    9. Practical Application: Focus on understanding methodology rather than memorizing isolated examples

    10. Long-term Goal: Solid Sarf foundation enables independent reading of classical Islamic texts and Quranic comprehension
