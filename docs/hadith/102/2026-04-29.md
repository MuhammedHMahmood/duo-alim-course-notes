**Date:** 2026-04-29

## Session Overview

This session covered Hadith number 29, narrated by Mu'ad ibn Jabal (رضي الله عنه), in which he asks the Prophet (ﷺ) about actions that lead to paradise and keep one away from hellfire. The Prophet's comprehensive response covers the five pillars of Islam, the "doors of good," and concludes with a powerful lesson about controlling one's tongue. The hadith demonstrates the hierarchical structure of Islamic practice and emphasizes that while the path to paradise involves great matters, Allah makes it easy for those He wills.

---

## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Narrator:** Mu'ad ibn Jabal (رضي الله عنه)

**Hadith Text:**
Mu'ad ibn Jabal said: "I said, 'O Messenger of Allah, inform me about an action that will enter me into paradise and keep me far away from the hellfire.'"

The Prophet (ﷺ) replied: "Indeed you have asked about something great, and verily it is easy upon that individual whom Allah has made it easy for. You worship Allah without associating any partners with Him, establish prayer, give zakat, fast during Ramadan, and perform hajj to the House."

Then he said: "Shall I inform you about the doors of good? Fasting is a shield, charity extinguishes sin just like water extinguishes fire, and the prayer of an individual in the middle of the night." Then he recited: "Their sides separate from their beds..." until he reached "...what they used to do."

Then he said: "Shall I inform you about the root of the matter, its pillar, and its apex?" I said, "Of course, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "The root of the matter is Islam, its pillar is prayer, and its apex is jihad."

Then he said: "Shall I inform you about the essential prerequisite of all of that?" I said, "Of course, O Messenger of Allah." So he grabbed his tongue and said: "Hold this back." I said, "O Prophet of Allah, will we be held to account for what we speak?" He said: "May your mother be bereft of you! Do not many people get thrown into the hellfire on their faces (or on their nostrils) except due to what their tongues have reaped?"

## Key Themes

1. **Hierarchy of Islamic Practice** - The hadith presents a structured approach from basic obligations to higher spiritual pursuits
2. **Divine Facilitation** - Great acts become easy for those whom Allah assists
3. **The Five Pillars** - Fundamental obligations for every Muslim
4. **Doors of Good** - Recommended acts that provide spiritual benefits and protection
5. **Control of Speech** - The tongue as a key determinant of spiritual success or failure
6. **Comprehensive Faith** - Integration of belief, worship, charity, and moral conduct

## Explanation and Commentary

The Prophet's response follows a logical progression from fundamental to advanced spiritual practices:

**Foundation Level - The Five Pillars:**
1. **Tawhid** - Pure monotheism without shirk
2. **Salah** - Establishing regular prayer
3. **Zakat** - Giving obligatory charity
4. **Sawm** - Fasting during Ramadan
5. **Hajj** - Pilgrimage to the Ka'bah

**Intermediate Level - Doors of Good:**
1. **Fasting as Protection** - Beyond Ramadan, voluntary fasting serves as a shield against sins
2. **Charity's Purifying Power** - Like water extinguishing fire, charity removes sins
3. **Night Prayer** - Tahajjud represents the highest form of voluntary worship

**Advanced Level - Structural Understanding:**
- **Islam** as the foundation (رَأْسُ الْأَمْرِ)
- **Prayer** as the supporting pillar (عَمُودُ)  
- **Jihad** as the apex (ذِرْوَةُ سَنَامِهِ)

**Master Key - Tongue Control:**
The Prophet emphasizes that all previous acts can be undermined by uncontrolled speech, making tongue restraint the essential prerequisite for spiritual success.

---

## Fiqh Rulings Derived

1. **Obligation of the Five Pillars** - These are mandatory for every capable Muslim
2. **Recommendation of Additional Acts** - Voluntary fasting, extra charity, and night prayers are highly recommended
3. **Priority of Speech Control** - Guarding one's tongue is essential for spiritual development
4. **Gradual Spiritual Development** - Islam accommodates different levels of spiritual capacity
5. **Accountability for Speech** - Muslims will be questioned about their words on the Day of Judgment
