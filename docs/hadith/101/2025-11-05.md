## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Hadith #9:**
عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَانِ بْنِ صَخْرٍ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى عَنْهُ قَالَ سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ يَقُولُ مَا نَهَيْتُكُمْ عَنْهُ فَاجْتَنِبُوهُ وَمَا أَمَرْتُكُمْ بِهِ فَأْتُوا مِنْهُ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُمْ فَإِنَّمَا أَهْلَكَ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ كَثْرَةُ مَسَائِلِهِمْ وَاخْتِلَافُهُمْ عَلَى أَنْبِيَائِهِمْ رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَمُسْلِمٌ

**Translation:** From Abu Huraira Abdul Rahman ibn Sakhr (may Allah be pleased with him), he said: "I heard the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) saying: 'That which I have warned you against, avoid it. And that which I have commanded you to do, do it to the best of your ability. Indeed, the people before you were destroyed by their abundant questioning and their opposition to their prophets.'" [Bukhari and Muslim]

**Hadith #10:**
عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى عَنْهُ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِنَّ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى طَيِّبٌ لَا يَقْبَلُ إِلَّا طَيِّبًا وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ أَمَرَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ بِمَا أَمَرَ بِهِ الْمُرْسَلِينَ فَقَالَ تَعَالَى يَا أَيُّهَا الرُّسُلُ كُلُوا مِنَ الطَّيِّبَاتِ وَاعْمَلُوا صَالِحًا وَقَالَ تَعَالَى يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُلُوا مِنْ طَيِّبَاتِ مَا رَزَقْنَاكُمْ ثُمَّ ذَكَرَ الرَّجُلَ يُطِيلُ السَّفَرَ أَشْعَثَ أَغْبَرَ يَمُدُّ يَدَيْهِ إِلَى السَّمَاءِ يَا رَبِّ يَا رَبِّ وَمَطْعَمُهُ حَرَامٌ وَمَشْرَبُهUhuُ حَرَامٌ وَمَلْبَسُهُ حَرَامٌ وَغُذِّيَ بِالْحَرَامِ فَأَنَّى يُسْتَجَابُ لَهُ

**Translation:** From Abu Huraira (may Allah be pleased with him), he said: "The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: 'Indeed, Allah is pure and only accepts that which is pure. And verily Allah commands the believers with that which He has commanded the messengers. Allah says: "O messengers, eat from that which is pure and perform good deeds." And Allah says: "O you who believe, eat from those pure things which We have provided to you." Then he mentioned a person who prolongs his travel, dusty and disheveled, raising his hands to the sky saying "O Lord, O Lord," while his food is unlawful, his drink is unlawful, his clothing is unlawful, and he has been nourished with that which is unlawful - how can this person's call be answered?'"

---

## Key Themes

1. **Absolute Obedience to Prohibitions**: What Allah and His Messenger have forbidden must be completely avoided
2. **Capability-Based Obedience to Commands**: Religious obligations should be fulfilled according to one's ability
3. **Avoiding Excessive Questioning**: Unnecessary questions that complicate religion should be avoided
4. **Purity in Earnings and Consumption**: All aspects of life (food, drink, clothing, income) must be lawfully obtained
5. **Connection Between Purity and Spiritual Acceptance**: Unlawful consumption affects the acceptance of worship and supplications

## Explanation and Commentary

**Context of Hadith #9:** This hadith was revealed when a companion named Aqra' bin Habis asked the Prophet (ﷺ) three times whether Hajj should be performed every year after the Prophet announced that Allah had made Hajj obligatory. The Prophet remained silent initially but after the third repetition, gave this response to discourage excessive questioning that could make religion more burdensome.

**The Two-Part Command Structure:**
1. **Prohibitions are Absolute**: When something is forbidden, it must be completely avoided without exception (except in dire necessity like consuming haram food to save one's life)
2. **Commands are According to Ability**: Religious obligations should be fulfilled to the best of one's capacity, referencing the Quranic principle "Allah does not burden a soul beyond its capacity" (Quran 2:286)

**Examples of Capability-Based Worship:**
- A person with chronic ablution issues (ma'dhur) performs wudu and prays to their best ability
- Someone unable to stand during prayer sits instead
- A person with a cast wipes over it during ablution
- If unable to wash certain body parts due to illness, they are excused

**The Danger of Excessive Questioning:** The hadith references the story of Bani Israel and the cow (Surah Al-Baqarah), where excessive questioning made a simple command increasingly difficult to fulfill. The Prophet (ﷺ) warned against this pattern to protect his Ummah from similar difficulties.

**Hadith #10 - The Parable:** The Prophet uses a powerful analogy of a traveler who appears outwardly pious (prolonged journey, disheveled from worship, raising hands in supplication) but whose entire lifestyle is built on unlawful earnings. This creates a barrier between the person and Allah's acceptance of their worship.

---

## Fiqh Rulings Derived

1. **Jurisprudential Flexibility**: The principle "do according to your ability" forms the basis for various legal concessions (rukhsah) in Islamic law
2. **Prohibition of Excessive Inquiry**: Unnecessary questions that could complicate religious obligations should be avoided
3. **Earning Standards**: All income, food, clothing, and sustenance must be obtained through lawful means
4. **Impact of Haram Consumption**: According to other narrations, consuming haram food can cause deeds to be rejected for 40 days
5. **Prerequisites for Accepted Prayer**: Purity in earnings and consumption is essential for spiritual acceptance

---

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **Balanced Approach to Religion**: Islam requires strict adherence to prohibitions while showing mercy in the application of commands based on individual capacity
2. **Avoid Religious Extremism**: Excessive questioning and making religion unnecessarily difficult goes against prophetic guidance
3. **Holistic Purity**: True spirituality requires purity not just in ritual worship but in all aspects of daily life - earnings, food, clothing
4. **Economic Ethics**: The connection between economic activity and spiritual life is fundamental in Islam
5. **Prophetic Teaching Methods**: The Prophet (ﷺ) used practical analogies to make complex spiritual concepts easily understandable
6. **Learning from Past Nations**: The fate of previous communities serves as a warning against similar mistakes
7. **Integration of Spiritual and Material**: Islamic teachings seamlessly connect worldly actions with spiritual consequences
