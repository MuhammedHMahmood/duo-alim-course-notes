# Hadith 101 - Study Notes
**Date:** 2025-09-10

---

## Session Overview

This session continued the study of Hadith Jibreel (Gabriel's Hadith), focusing on the detailed explanation of Islam, Iman, and Ihsan as taught by Angel Jibreel in human form to the Prophet ﷺ and his companions. The hadith covers the foundational pillars of faith and includes signs of the Day of Judgment, with particular emphasis on parent-child relationships and the construction of tall buildings as indicators of the end times.

---

## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Narrator:** Umar ibn al-Khattab ﷺ

**Key Arabic Text:**
- بَيْنَمَا نَحْنُ جُلُوسٌ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صَلَى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِذْ طَلَعَ عَلَيْنَا رَجُلٌ
- شَدِيدُ بَيَاضِ الثِيَابِ شَدِيدُ سَوَادِ الشَّعَرِ
- يَا مُحَمَّدُ أَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الْإِسْلَامِ
- قَالَ صَدَقْتَ - فَعَجِبْنَا لَهُ يَسْأَلُهُ وَيُصَدِّقُهُ

---

## Key Themes

1. **The Three Pillars of Faith:** Islam (external practice), Iman (internal belief), and Ihsan (excellence in worship)
2. **Signs of the Day of Judgment:** Focus on moral decay and material excess
3. **Knowledge of the Unseen:** Only Allah knows the timing of the final hour
4. **Proper Etiquette in Learning:** Dress appropriately and show respect when seeking knowledge
5. **Parent-Child Relationships:** The importance of respecting and serving parents

---

## Important Vocabulary

| Arabic Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| الْإِسْلَامُ (al-Islam) | Submission to Allah through external practices |
| الْإِمَانُ (al-Iman) | Faith and belief in the heart |
| الْإِحْسَانُ (al-Ihsan) | Excellence in worship as if seeing Allah |
| تُقِيمَ الصَّلَاةَ (tuqim as-salah) | To establish prayer (not just pray) |
| أَمَارَتِهَا (amaratiha) | Signs/indicators of the Day of Judgment |
| أَنْ تَلِدَ الْأَمَةُ رَبَّتَهَا | That a slave girl gives birth to her mistress |
| يَتَطَاوَلُونَ فِي الْبُنْيَانِ | Competing in constructing tall buildings |
| رِيَاءٌ (riya) | Showing off/ostentation |

## Explanation and Commentary

**Islam consists of five pillars:**
1. **Shahada:** Testifying that there is no god but Allah and Muhammad ﷺ is His messenger
2. **Salah:** Establishing prayer (using "iqamah" - making it part of one's life)
3. **Zakah:** Giving obligatory charity
4. **Sawm:** Fasting during Ramadan
5. **Hajj:** Pilgrimage to Makkah if able

**Iman consists of six articles:**
1. Belief in Allah (His oneness, attributes, and 99 beautiful names)
2. Belief in angels (as honored servants who only act by Allah's command)
3. Belief in revealed books (Zabur, Tawrah, Injil, Quran, and other scriptures)
4. Belief in messengers (their truthfulness and divine mission)
5. Belief in the Last Day (resurrection, judgment, paradise, hell)
6. Belief in predestination (qadar), both good and bad

**Ihsan:** Worshipping Allah as if you can see Him; if you cannot see Him, then know that He sees you. This represents the highest level of consciousness and excellence in worship.

---

## Fiqh Rulings Derived

1. **Distinction between Islam and Iman:** While sometimes used interchangeably, they represent different realities - Islam focuses on external actions, Iman on internal beliefs
2. **Etiquette of Learning:** Students should dress appropriately and show proper respect when seeking knowledge
3. **Knowledge of the Unseen:** Only Allah knows the exact timing of the Day of Judgment
4. **Making Dua during Study:** It is permissible and encouraged to make dua when learning about matters of faith

---

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **Respect for Parents:** The greatest emphasized lesson was honoring and serving parents. The hadith warns that disrespect of parents will be a major sign of the end times.

2. **Value Time with Parents:** Spend quality time with parents while they are alive, as this time cannot be recovered once lost.

3. **Excellence in All Actions:** Ihsan should be applied to everything we do, from worship to daily activities.

4. **Simplicity in Lifestyle:** The Prophet ﷺ lived simply despite having the means for luxury, teaching us not to compete in material excess.

5. **The Reality of Predestination:** Everything happens according to Allah's decree - "The pens have been lifted and the pages have dried."

6. **Proper Student Etiquette:** Come clean, dressed well, give undivided attention, and respond with humility ("Allah and His Messenger know best").

7. **Integration of Faith:** Islam encompasses both external practices and internal beliefs, unified through the excellence of ihsan.
