## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Hadith #12:**
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه قال، قال رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم: من حسن إسلام المرء تركه ما لا يعني

*'An Abi Hurayrata radiyallahu anhu qal, qal Rasulullah sallallahu alayhi wasallam: Min husni islamil mar'i tarkuhu ma la ya'ni*

"From the beauty of a person's Islam is that he leaves that which does not concern him."

**Hadith #13:**
عن أبي حمزة أنس بن مالك رضي الله عنه خادم رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم عن النبي صلى الله عليه وسلم قال: لا يؤمن أحدكم حتى يحب لأخيه ما يحب لنفسه

*'An Abi Hamzata Anas ibn Malikin radiyallahu anhu khadimi Rasulillahi sallallahu alayhi wasallam 'an an-Nabi sallallahu alayhi wasallam qal: La yu'minu ahadukum hatta yuhibba li'akhihi ma yuhibbu li'nafsi*

"A person cannot have true iman until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself."

---

## Key Themes

1. **Focusing on beneficial matters** - The importance of occupying oneself with what is useful and avoiding what doesn't concern one
2. **Brotherhood in faith** - The requirement to love for fellow believers what one loves for oneself
3. **Complete vs. incomplete iman** - Understanding levels of faith and spiritual perfection
4. **Prophetic eloquence** - How the Prophet (ﷺ) conveyed profound meanings in few words

## Explanation and Commentary

**Hadith #12 - On Beneficial Occupation:**
- This hadith demonstrates the Prophet's (ﷺ) eloquent speech that "packs many precious meanings into few words"
- Similar guidance found in the advice of Luqman al-Hakim: being truthful in speech, returning trusts to their owners, and leaving what doesn't concern one
- One scholar noted that Allah's abandonment of a servant is evidenced by placing his preoccupation in matters that don't concern him
- This hadith forms one of four foundational prophetic sayings that serve as the basis for all Sunan (prophetic practices) according to Imam Abu Dawud

**Hadith #13 - On Brotherhood:**
- This doesn't mean a person lacks iman entirely if they don't possess this quality, but rather that their iman is incomplete
- The Nasa'i version clarifies: "until he loves for his brother of good what he loves for himself"
- Sheikh Abu Amr explains this is achievable by loving good for others without infringing on one's own benefits
- The reality is preference, not mere equality - wanting better for one's brother than for oneself
- Believers are like one soul, as evidenced by the hadith comparing believers to one body where if one limb suffers, the whole body feels it

---

## Fiqh Rulings Derived

1. **Principle of non-interference** - Muslims should avoid meddling in affairs that don't concern them
2. **Standards of complete faith** - True iman requires loving good for fellow believers
3. **Community responsibility** - Believers should actively prefer good for others and help those in lesser positions
4. **Spiritual priorities** - Focus should be on beneficial activities that advance one's Islam

---

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **Mind your own business** - Avoiding what doesn't concern you is a sign of spiritual maturity and beautiful Islam
2. **Prophetic wisdom** - The Prophet (ﷺ) could convey profound guidance in remarkably few words
3. **Brotherhood requires sacrifice** - True Islamic brotherhood means preferring others over yourself, not just treating them equally
4. **Faith has levels** - Basic iman and complete iman are different spiritual states
5. **Sound hearts find it easy** - Those with pure hearts naturally want good for others; it's only difficult for corrupted hearts
6. **Unity of the ummah** - Believers function as one spiritual body, sharing each other's joys and sufferings
