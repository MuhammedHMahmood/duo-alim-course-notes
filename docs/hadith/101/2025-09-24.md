## Session Overview

This session covered Hadith #4 from Abdullah Ibn Mas'ud (RA) about the stages of human creation and divine predestination (qadr). The class began with a review of the previous hadith about the five pillars of Islam, then proceeded to examine this profound hadith about embryonic development and Allah's predetermined knowledge of each person's sustenance, lifespan, deeds, and ultimate fate.

## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Narrator:** Abu Abd al-Rahman Abdullah Ibn Mas'ud (RA) - known as the most intuitive and intellectual among the companions (afqah al-sahabah)

**Text:**
عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَانِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَىٰ عَنْهُ قَالَ حَدَّثَنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ وَهُوَ الصَّادِقُ الْمَصْدُوقُ إِنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ يُجْمَعُ خَلْقُهُ فِي بَطْنِ أُمِّهِ أَرْبَعِينَ يَوْمًا نُطْفَةً ثُمَّ يَكُونُ عَلَقَةً مِثْلَ ذَٰلِكَ ثُمَّ يَكُونُ مُضْغَةً مِثْلَ ذَٰلِكَ ثُمَّ يُرْسَلُ إِلَيْهِ الْمَلَكُ فَيَنْفُخُ فِيهِ الرُّوحَ وَيُؤْمَرُ بِأَرْبَعِ كَلِمَاتٍ بِكَتْبِ رِزْقِهِ وَأَجَلِهِ وَعَمَلِهِ وَشَقِيٌّ أَوْ سَعِيدٌ فَوَاللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا إِلَٰهَ غَيْرُهُ إِنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ لَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ حَتَّىٰ مَا يَكُونُ بَيْنَهُ وَبَيْنَهَا إِلَّا ذِرَاعٌ فَيَسْبِقُ عَلَيْهِ الْكِتَابُ فَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ النَّارِ فَيَدْخُلُهَا وَإِنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ لَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ النَّارِ حَتَّىٰ مَا يَكُونُ بَيْنَهُ وَبَيْنَهَا إِلَّا ذِرَاعٌ فَيَسْبِقُ عَلَيْهِ الْكِتَابُ فَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ فَيَدْخُلُهَا

## Key Themes

1. **Divine Predestination (Qadr)** - Allah's predetermined knowledge of all events
2. **Stages of Human Creation** - The embryonic development process
3. **Divine Recording** - The angel's recording of four crucial matters
4. **Final Actions** - The significance of one's final deeds
5. **Trust in Allah's Wisdom** - Understanding divine decree while maintaining personal responsibility

## Explanation and Commentary

### Stages of Creation (120 days total):
1. **First 40 days**: Nutfah (sperm/fluid stage)
2. **Second 40 days**: Alaqah (blood clot stage)  
3. **Third 40 days**: Mudghah (lump of flesh stage)
4. **After 120 days**: Angel blows the soul (ruh) into the fetus

### The Angel's Four Responsibilities:
The angel is commanded to record:
1. **Rizq** - The person's sustenance/provision
2. **Ajal** - The appointed time of death
3. **Amal** - The person's deeds/actions
4. **Shaqi or Sa'id** - Whether the person will be wretched or blessed

### The Emphasis on Truthfulness:
The narrator emphasizes "as-sadiq al-masdooq" (the truthful and credible one) because this hadith reveals unseen knowledge about embryonic development that was unknown to people 1400+ years ago, now confirmed by modern science.

---

## Fiqh Rulings Derived

1. **Ensoulment occurs after 120 days** of pregnancy
2. **Divine knowledge is absolute** - Allah knows all outcomes before they occur
3. **Human responsibility remains** despite divine predestination
4. **Final actions are crucial** - one's ending determines their fate
5. **No one knows their predetermined fate** - this removes any excuse for inaction

---

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **Trust in Divine Provision**: Allah has already designated each person's sustenance. Financial stress can only be removed by trusting that Allah is Ar-Raziq (The Provider).

2. **Importance of Perseverance**: Since no one knows their predetermined fate, everyone must continue striving in righteousness until death.

3. **Significance of Final Actions**: The hadith "Actions are judged by their endings" (innama al-a'mal bil-khawatim) reminds us that final deeds determine one's fate.

4. **Avoiding Spiritual Complacency**: Those doing good should not become arrogant, remembering that their ability to do good is Allah's blessing.

5. **Never Despair of Allah's Mercy**: Those who have sinned should never postpone repentance, as death's timing is unknown.

6. **Balance of Predestination and Free Will**: While Allah knows all outcomes, humans don't know their fate, so they must continue choosing righteousness.

7. **Modern Scientific Validation**: This hadith's description of embryonic development demonstrates the Prophet's ﷺ truthfulness and the Quran's divine origin.
