## Session Overview

This session covered the famous Hadith Jibreel (Gabriel), also known as Maratib ad-Din (The Levels of Religion), narrated by Umar ibn al-Khattab (رضي الله عنه). The hadith explains the three fundamental levels of Islamic faith: Islam, Iman, and Ihsan. The session focused on reading the complete Arabic text and providing detailed translation, with commentary reserved for the following week.

---

## Hadith Text and Chain of Narration

**Arabic Text:**
عَنْ عُمَرَ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ أَيْضًا قَالَ بَيْنَمَا نَحْنُ جُلُوسٌ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ ذَاتَ يَوْمٍ إِذْ طَلَعَ عَلَيْنَا رَجُلٌ شَدِيدُ بَيَاضِ الثِّيَابِ شَدِيدُ سَوَادِ الشَّعَرِ لَا يُرَى عَلَيْهِ أَثَرُ السَّفَرِ وَلَا يَعْرِفُهُ مِنَّا أَحَدٌ حَتَّى جَلَسَ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ فَأَسْنَدَ رُكْبَتَيْهِ إِلَى رُكْبَتَيْهِ وَوَضَعَ كَفَّيْهِ عَلَى فَخِذَيْهِ وَقَالَ يَا مُحَمَّدُ أَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الْإِسْلَامِ قَالَ الْإِسْلَامُ أَنْ تَشْهَدَ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ وَتُقِيمَ الصَّلَاةَ وَتُؤْتِيَ الزَّكَاةَ وَتَصُومَ رَمَضَانَ وَتَحُجَّ الْبَيْتَ إِنِ اسْتَطَعْتَ إِلَيْهِ سَبِيلًا قَالَ صَدَقْتَ فَعَجِبْنَا لَهُ يَسْأَلُهُ وَيُصَدِّقُهُ قَالَ فَأَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الْإِيمَانِ قَالَ أَنْ تُؤْمِنَ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ وَتُؤْمِنَ بِالْقَدَرِ خَيْرِهِ وَشَرِّهِ قَالَ صَدَقْتَ قَالَ فَأَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الْإِحْسَانِ قَالَ أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللَّهَ كَأَنَّكَ تَرَاهُ فَإِنْ لَمْ تَكُنْ تَرَاهُ فَإِنَّهُ يَرَاكَ قَالَ فَأَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ السَّاعَةِ قَالَ مَا الْمَسْؤُولُ عَنْهَا بِأَعْلَمَ مِنَ السَّائِلِ قَالَ فَأَخْبِرْنِي عَنْ أَمَارَتِهَا قَالَ أَنْ تَلِدَ الْأَمَةُ رَبَّتَهَا وَأَنْ تَرَى الْحُفَاتَ الْعُرَاتَ الْعَالَةَ رِعَاءَ الشَّاءِ يَتَطَاوَلُونَ فِي الْبُنْيَانِ ثُمَّ انْطَلَقَ فَلَبِثْتُ مَلِيًّا ثُمَّ قَالَ يَا عُمَرُ أَتَدْرِي مَنِ السَّائِلُ قُلْتُ اللَّهُ وَرَسُولُهُ أَعْلَمُ قَالَ فَإِنَّهُ جِبْرِيلُ أَتَاكُمْ يُعَلِّمُكُمْ دِينَكُمْ

**Chain of Narration:** Umar ibn al-Khattab (رضي الله عنه)

## Key Themes

1. **The Three Levels of Religion:** Islam, Iman (Faith), and Ihsan (Excellence/Perfection)
2. **Divine Teaching Method:** Angel Jibreel's pedagogical approach through questioning
3. **The Mystery of the Final Hour:** Knowledge belongs only to Allah
4. **Signs of the End Times:** Social and economic transformation
5. **Scholarly Etiquette:** Protecting the dignity of companions who made mistakes

## Explanation and Commentary

**The Five Pillars of Islam:**
1. **Shahada** - Bearing witness that there is no deity except Allah and Muhammad is His messenger
2. **Salah** - Establishing prayer
3. **Zakah** - Giving obligatory charity
4. **Sawm** - Fasting during Ramadan
5. **Hajj** - Pilgrimage to Mecca (if able)

**The Six Articles of Faith (Iman):**
1. Belief in Allah
2. Belief in His angels
3. Belief in His books
4. Belief in His messengers
5. Belief in the Last Day
6. Belief in divine decree (qadar), both good and bad

**Ihsan Definition:**
Worshipping Allah as if you see Him; if you cannot achieve this level, then remember that He sees you.

**Knowledge of the Hour:**
The Prophet (ﷺ) emphasized that knowledge of when the Day of Judgment will occur belongs only to Allah - neither the questioner nor the questioned has this knowledge.

**Scholarly Etiquette:**
The instructor noted that scholars traditionally do not mention the names of companions who made mistakes out of respect for their status, referring instead to "Muhajir Umm Qais" (the migrant of Umm Qais).

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **The Importance of Learning Religion:** Jibreel came specifically to teach the companions their religion through this questioning method

2. **Progressive Learning:** The hadith presents religion in three ascending levels - outward practice (Islam), inner belief (Iman), and spiritual excellence (Ihsan)

3. **Humility in Knowledge:** Even the Prophet (ﷺ) acknowledged the limits of human knowledge regarding the Final Hour

4. **Respect for Companions:** Scholars maintain adab (etiquette) when discussing mistakes made by the Prophet's companions

5. **Signs of End Times:** The hadith provides specific signs including social upheaval (slave girls giving birth to masters) and economic transformation (poor shepherds building tall buildings)

6. **Divine Pedagogy:** Allah uses various means, including angels in human form, to teach important religious concepts
