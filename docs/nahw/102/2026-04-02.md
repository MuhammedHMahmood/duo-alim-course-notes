## Session Overview

This session covered adverbs of time (zarf al-zaman) and place (zarf al-makaan) in Arabic grammar. The class began with a clarification on sifa (adjectives) in mudaf constructions, then proceeded through various temporal and spatial adverbs, focusing on their i'rab rules and dual natures. Key emphasis was placed on understanding when certain adverbs are mu'rab (declinable) versus mabni (indeclinable) based on whether their mudaf ilaih is explicitly mentioned or merely intended.

---

## Grammar Rules Covered

### Sifa in Mudaf Constructions
- If sifa describes the mudaf: comes after mudaf ilaih with alif lam
- If sifa describes the mudaf ilaih: matches properties of mudaf ilaih and is in jar case

### Temporal Adverbs (Zarf al-Zaman)
1. **Mata (متى)** - dual usage:
   - Interrogative: "when" (questions)
   - Conditional: causes both shart and jaza to be majzoom (like in)

2. **Munzu/Mud (منذ/مذ)** - dual nature:
   - As harf jar: following word is majroor
   - As ism (mubtada): following word is khabar (marfu)

3. **Qabl/Ba'd (قبل/بعد)** - dual nature:
   - Mu'rab when mudaf ilaih is mentioned in words
   - Mabni ala al-dham when mudaf ilaih is intended but not mentioned

### Spatial Adverbs (Zarf al-Makaan)
1. **Haythu (حيث)** - generally mudaf to entire sentences
2. **Quddam/Khalf, Tahta/Fawq (قدام/خلف، تحت/فوق)** - follow same rules as qabl/ba'd
3. **Inda (عند)** - standard mudaf behavior

### General Zarf Rules
- Zarf is mafu'ul fihi (adverbial object)
- By default in nasab case unless governed by harf jar
- Ismuz-zarf accepts other i'rab if there's reason, otherwise defaults to nasab

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

**Mata tasum asum (متى تصم أصم)**
- متى: conditional noun causing jazm
- تصم: fi'l mudari majzoom (shart)
- أصم: fi'l mudari majzoom (jaza)

**Ma ra'aytuhu munzu yawmil jumu'ati (ما رأيته منذ يوم الجمعة)**
- منذ: harf jar
- يوم الجمعة: majroor bi munzu

**Min qablu wa min ba'du (من قبل ومن بعد)**
- قبل/بعد: majroor bi min, but mabni ala al-dham (mudaf ilaih intended)

**Ijlis haythu Zaydun jalisun (اجلس حيث زيد جالس)**
- اجلس: fi'l amr
- حيث: zarf makaan mudaf
- زيد جالس: jumla ismiyya (mubtada khabar) acting as mudaf ilaih

## Practice Examples

1. **Temporal negation emphasis:**
   - ما ضربته قط (I never hit him - past)
   - لن أضربه عود (I will never hit him - future)

2. **Qabl/Ba'd variations:**
   - من قبل الفتح (min qabli al-fath) - mudaf ilaih mentioned, majroor
   - من قبل (min qablu) - mudaf ilaih intended, mabni ala al-dham

3. **Spatial positioning:**
   - جلس زيد تحت وعمرو فوق (Zayd sat under and Amr above)
   - المال عند زيد (The money is with Zayd)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Dual Nature Concept**: Many adverbs (munzu/mud, qabl/ba'd, quddam/khalf, tahta/fawq) can be either mu'rab or mabni depending on whether their mudaf ilaih is explicitly mentioned or merely intended.

2. **Default Zarf I'rab**: All adverbs default to nasab case as mafu'ul fihi unless governed by a harf jar.

3. **Conditional vs Interrogative**: Mata can function both ways - as interrogative (questions) and conditional (causing jazm like 'in').

4. **Mabni Consistency**: When adverbs are mabni (indeclinable), they maintain their form regardless of grammatical context, though their i'rab function may change.

5. **Sentence-level Mudaf**: Haythu uniquely takes entire sentences as its mudaf ilaih, demonstrating Arabic's structural flexibility.

6. **Emphasis Particles**: Qattu (past) and audu (present/future) require prior negation to function as emphasis for "never."
