This session focused on **Zuruf** (adverbs), specifically section 2.4.6 covering **Zuruf al-Zaman** (adverbs of time). ## Grammar Rules Covered

### Basic Principles of Zarf
- **Definition**: Zarf is a noun that gives an idea of when or where work is done
- **Alternative name**: Zarf is also called **Maf'ul Fi**
- **Default case**: Zarf is always in **Nasab case** by default (since Maf'ul is always Nasab)
- **Exception**: Only changes case when there is an 'amil (governing agent) causing a different case

### Types of Zuruf
1. **Zuruf al-Zaman** (adverbs of time) - can be specific or unspecified
2. **Zuruf al-Makan** (adverbs of place)

### Rules for إذ (Idh)
1. **Meaning**: "When" (in statements, not questions)
2. **Tense rule**: Gives meaning of **Madi** even when appearing before **Fi'l Mudareh**
3. **Sentence types**: Can be entered upon **Jumla Ismiya** or **Jumla Fi'liya**
4. **Three meanings**:
   - **When** (temporal statement)
   - **Suddenly** (إذ الفجائية - Idh al-Fuja'iya)
   - **Because** (إذ التعليلية - Idh al-Ta'liliya)

### Rules for إذا (Idha)
1. **Meanings**: "When" or "If"
2. **Tense rule**: Gives meaning of **Mudareh** even when appearing before **Fi'l Madhi** (opposite of إذ)
3. **Condition rule**: Gives meaning of **Shart and Jaza** but does NOT give **Jazm** (because verbs are Madhi, and Madhi cannot accept Jazm)
4. **Sentence preference**: Can be entered upon Jumla Ismiya or Jumla Fi'liya, but **Jumla Fi'liya is preferred**
5. **Suddenness**: Can give meaning of suddenness, but only when entered upon **Jumla Ismiya**

### Comparison: إذ vs إذا
- **إذ**: Madi meaning even with Mudareh verbs
- **إذا**: Mudareh meaning even with Madhi verbs
- **Both**: Can express suddenness and can be entered upon both sentence types

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### إذ Examples:
1. **واذكروا إذ أنتم قليلون** (Quran)
   - *Wazkuru idh antum qalilun*
   - "Remember when you were few"
   - إذ entered upon Jumla Ismiya, gives Madi meaning

2. **وإذ يرفع إبراهيم القواعد من البيت** (Quran)
   - *Wa idh yarfa'u Ibrahim al-qawa'id min al-bayt*
   - "And when Ibrahim was raising the foundations of the house"
   - إذ gives Madi meaning despite يرفع being Mudareh

### إذا Examples:
1. **إذا طلعت الشمس آتيك**
   - *Idha tala'at ash-shamsu atayk*
   - "When the sun rises, I will come to you"
   - إذا entered upon Jumla Ismiya

2. **إذا نزل المطر فارجع إلى البيت**
   - *Idha nazal al-matar farji' ila al-bayt*
   - "If it rains, return home"
   - نزل is Madhi but gives Mudareh meaning due to إذا

3. **إذا جاء نصر الله** (Surat an-Nasr)
   - *Idha ja'a nasr Allah*
   - "When the victory of Allah comes"
   - جاء is Madhi but meaning is Mudareh

## Practice Examples

### Suddenness Examples:
1. **خرجت فإذا مدير المدرسة ناظر**
   - *Kharajtu fa-idha mudir al-madrasa nazir*
   - "I came out and suddenly the principal was watching"

2. **خرجت فإذا الكلب واقف**
   - *Kharajtu fa-idha al-kalb waqif*
   - "I came out and suddenly encountered the dog standing"

### Conditional Examples:
- **إن تذهب أذهب** (with Jazm)
  - *In tadhhab adhhab*
  - "If you go, I go"
  - Both verbs receive Jazm

### Comparison with Different Question Forms:
- **متى صليت؟** - "When did you pray?" (question)
- **متى تقرأ؟** - "When will you read?" (question)  
- **متى تجيء؟** - "When will you come?" (question)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Never confuse إذ with متى**: إذ is for statements, متى is for questions
2. **Opposite tense rules**: إذ gives Madi meaning, إذا gives Mudareh meaning
3. **Context determines meaning**: Whether إذ/إذا means "when," "suddenly," or "because/if" depends on context
4. **Default case rule**: All Zuruf default to Nasab case as they are Maf'ul Fi
5. **Jazm distinction**: إن gives Jazm to conditional sentences, إذا does not (because it uses Madhi verbs)
6. **Sentence compatibility**: Both إذ and إذا can be entered upon Jumla Ismiya and Jumla Fi'liya
7. **Study focus**: Special attention needed for **Qablu and Ba'du** (number 10 in upcoming lessons) as they are key to understanding Zuruf al-Makan
