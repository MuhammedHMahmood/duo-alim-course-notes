## Grammar Rules Covered

### Ism al-Mawsool (Relative Pronouns)
- Every Ism al-Mawsool requires silah (relative clause)
- Silah must contain a dameer (pronoun) that refers back to the Ism al-Mawsool, called al-'aid
- Dual forms follow tathniya rules: Rafa case with alif-nun, Nasab/Jarr cases with ya-nun
- All other forms are mabni (indeclinable)

### Additional Relative Pronouns
- **Man (مَنْ)**: Used for dhawi al-uqool (intelligent beings) - humans, jinn, angels
- **Ma (مَا)**: Used for non-intelligent beings - animals, objects, things
- Both work for all genders and numbers
- **Dhul (ذُو)**: In Banu Tay dialect only, functions as relative pronoun for all genders/numbers

### Shibh al-Jumla Exception
- When silah is a semi-sentence (jarr-majroor combination), no 'aid dameer is required
- Exception to the standard rule requiring a referring pronoun

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Example 1: Ja'a alladhi nasarakah
**Translation:** The one who helped you came
**Analysis:**
- Ja'a: fi'l 
- Alladhi nasarakah: fa'il (entire unit)
  - Alladhi: Ism mawsool
  - Nasarakah: silah (jumla fi'liya with fa'il mustatir + ka'af maf'ul)

### Example 2: Ra'aytu alladhayni nasarakah  
**Translation:** I saw the two who helped you
**Analysis:**
- Ra'aytu: fi'l + fa'il
- Alladhayni nasarakah: maf'ul (entire unit)
  - Alladhayni: Ism mawsool (Nasab case with ya-nun)
  - Nasarakah: silah

### Example 3: Ja'a alladhi abuhu 'alimun
**Translation:** The person whose father is knowledgeable came
**Analysis:**
- Ja'a: fi'l
- Alladhi abuhu 'alimun: fa'il (entire unit)
  - Alladhi: Ism mawsool
  - Abuhu 'alimun: silah (jumla ismiya: abuhu mubtada + 'alimun khabar)

## Key Takeaways

- Sentence analysis (tarkeeb) requires breaking down sentences into components first, then reassembling to understand grammatical relationships
- Practice with 50-100 varied sentences is recommended for mastery
- Dual forms of Ism al-Mawsool follow standard tathniya declension rules
- Man/ma distinction based on intelligence level of referent is crucial
- Shibh al-jumla (semi-sentences) have different rules than full sentences for silah requirements
- Regular practice with tarkeeb analysis strengthens overall Arabic grammar comprehension
