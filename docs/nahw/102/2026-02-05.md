## Grammar Rules Covered

### Three Special Types of Damir

**1. Damir al-Sha'an (ضمير الشأن)**
- Singular, third person masculine pronoun (مفرد غائب مذكر)
- Appears at beginning of sentence without prior antecedent (مرجع)
- Adds emphasis and beauty to the sentence
- The sentence after it clarifies the pronoun
- Example: إنه زيد قائم (Indeed, the matter is that Zaid is standing)

**2. Damir al-Qissa (ضمير القصة)**  
- Same as Damir al-Sha'an but feminine
- Example: إنها فاطمة قائمة (Indeed, the matter is that Fatima is standing)

**3. Damir al-Fasl (ضمير الفصل)**
- Separates Mubtada from Khabar for emphasis and restriction
- Must be compatible with Mubtada in number and gender  
- Creates exclusive meaning (only they, not others)
- Example: أولئك هم المفلحون (It is they who are successful)
- Structure: Mubtada + Damir al-Fasl + al-Khabar with Alif-Lam

### Al-Asma'ul-Mawsoola (الأسماء الموصولة)

**Basic Rules:**
- Always Ma'rifa (definite), cannot be made Nakira
- Must have Sila (صلة) - a complete sentence following it
- The Sila must contain A'id (عائد) - a pronoun referring back to the Ism Mawsool
- A'id must match the Ism Mawsool in number and gender

**Masculine Forms:**
- Singular: الذي (alladhi)
- Dual: اللذان (alladhani) - Rafa case / اللذين (alladhaini) - Nasab/Jar cases  
- Plural: الذين (alladheena)

**Feminine Forms:**
- Singular: التي (allati)
- Dual: اللتان (allatani) - Rafa case / اللتين (allataini) - Nasab/Jar cases
- Plural: اللاتي/اللواتي/اللائي (allati/allawati/alla'i)

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

**Example 1:** جاء الذي نصرك
- جاء: Fi'l (verb)
- الذي نصرك: Fa'il (subject) - composed of Ism Mawsool + Sila
- الذي: Ism Mawsool
- نصرك: Sila (relative clause)
- Hidden pronoun "هو" in نصر is the A'id referring to الذي

**Example 2:** رأيت الذين نصروني  
- رأيت: Fi'l + Fa'il
- الذين نصروني: Maf'ool (object) in Nasab case
- الذين: Ism Mawsool (plural masculine)
- نصروني: Sila
- The واو in نصروا represents هم (A'id) referring to الذين

**Example 3:** أولئك هم المفلحون
- أولئك: Mubtada
- هم: Damir al-Fasl (not I'rab position)
- المفلحون: Khabar in Rafa case

## Key Takeaways

1. **Special Damair add emphasis:** Damir al-Sha'an/Qissa create more elaborate and emphatic sentence structures compared to simple constructions.

2. **Damir al-Fasl creates exclusivity:** It transforms a general statement into an exclusive one, limiting the quality to specific subjects only.

3. **Relative pronouns are always definite:** Al-Asma'ul-Mawsoola cannot be made indefinite under any circumstances.

4. **Sila is mandatory:** Every Ism Mawsool must be followed by a complete sentence (Sila) to give it meaning.

5. **A'id ensures coherence:** The pronoun in the Sila must grammatically match the Ism Mawsool in number and gender.

6. **Mabni nature:** Relative pronouns don't change form based on their grammatical case - they remain constant.

7. **Fi'l number rule:** When the Fa'il comes after the Fi'l, the Fi'l remains singular regardless of the Fa'il's number.
