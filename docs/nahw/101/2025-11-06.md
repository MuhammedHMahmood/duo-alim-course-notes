## Session Overview

This session covered fi'il mudareh (present/future tense verbs) in both active voice (maroof) and passive voice (majhool). The class focused on formation rules, conjugation patterns, and the conditions under which nun al-wiqaya (protective nun) is dropped when certain particles are added to mudareh verbs.

---

## Grammar Rules Covered

### Fi'il Mudareh Formation
- **Active Voice (Maroof)**: Add one of four letters (ya, ta, hamza, nun) to the root
- **Pattern**: fa'ala → yaf'alu (ayn can take all three harakat: dhamma, kasra, fatha depending on baab)
- **Laam**: always takes dhamma in basic form

### Fi'il Mudareh Majhool (Passive Voice)
- **Harf mudareh**: always takes dhamma
- **Ayn kalima**: always takes fatha
- **Pattern**: yuf'alu, tuf'alu, uf'alu, nuf'alu

### Conjugation Distribution
- **Ya**: 5 places out of 14 conjugations
- **Ta**: 8 places out of 14 conjugations  
- **Hamza**: 1 place (af'alu)
- **Nun**: 1 place (naf'alu)

### Nun al-Wiqaya vs Nun al-Niswa
- **Nun al-Wiqaya**: Can be dropped, appears in 7 places
- **Nun al-Niswa**: Cannot be dropped, appears in 2 places (yaf'alna, taf'alna)

### Particles Affecting Mudareh
- **Lan**: Negation particle meaning "never"
  - Changes dhamma at end to fatha
  - Drops nun al-wiqaya
  - Example: yaf'aluna → lan yaf'alu

- **An**: From same family as lan, causes same changes
- **In**: Conditional particle, same effect
- **Hatta**: "Until" particle, same effect

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Quranic Examples Discussed:
1. **Surah Al-Imran**: "an la na'abuda" (that we worship not)
   - Original: na'abudu → an la na'abuda (dhamma changed to fatha)

2. **Surah Al-Baqarah**: "lan tanaloo al-birra" (you will never attain righteousness)
   - Original: tanaaluna → lan tanaalu (nun dropped, dhamma to fatha)

3. **"hatta tunfiqoo mimma tuhibboon"** (until you spend from what you love)
   - Original: tunfiquna → hatta tunfiqoo (nun dropped)

4. **"lan tanfa'akum arhamukum"** (your relatives will never benefit you)
   - Shows fatha on ayn due to lan

## Key Takeaways

1. **Formation Rule**: Passive mudareh always has dhamma on harf mudareh and fatha on ayn kalima
2. **Nun Distinction**: Nun al-wiqaya can be dropped; nun al-niswa cannot
3. **Particle Effects**: Lan, an, in, hatta all cause dhamma→fatha change and drop nun al-wiqaya
4. **Quranic Application**: Understanding these rules helps in comprehending Quranic verses
5. **Conjugation System**: 14 total forms with specific distribution of prefix letters
6. **Practical Application**: These rules are consistently applied throughout Arabic grammar and Quranic text
