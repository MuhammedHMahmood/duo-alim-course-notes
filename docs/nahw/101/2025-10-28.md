### Verb Conjugation Rules
- **Fi'l Madhi (Past Tense)**: Emphasis on memorizing complete conjugation patterns
- **Object Case (Maf'ul)**: Objects of verbs are always in nasb case
- **Preposition Usage**: 
  - Use of "min" (من) with certain verbs (e.g., saqat min qadamika - fell from your foot)
  - "Ma'a" (مع) shows companionship, not tool usage
  - "Bi" (ب) used for tools/instruments

### Sentence Structure
- **Jumla Fi'liyya**: Verbal sentences (most exercises focused on this type)
- **Mubtada and Khabar**: Subject-predicate structure (e.g., lawnuha faqiun - its color is bright yellow)

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Example 1: Object in Nasb Case
- **ركبت سيارتك** (rakibtu sayyarataka) - "I rode your car"
- سيارتك is maf'ul (object) in nasb case, hence the fatha ending

### Example 2: Preposition with Jar Case
- **سقط الحذاء من قدمك** (saqat al-hitha'u min qadamika)
- قدمك is in jar case due to preposition من (min)

### Example 3: Mubtada-Khabar Structure
- **لونها أصفر فاقع** (lawnuha asfar faqi')
- لونها (mubtada), أصفر فاقع (khabar)

## Practice Examples

### English to Arabic Translation Exercises:
1. My father - أبي (abi)
2. Her tongue - لسانها (lisanuha) 
3. Your (f.sg.) nose - أنفك (anfuki)
4. My hand - يدي (yadi)
5. Their (f.pl.) teeth - أسنانهن (asnanuhunna)
6. His chest - صدره (sadruhu)
7. Our handkerchief - منديلنا (mendeeluna)
8. I rode your car - ركبت سيارتك (rakibtu sayyarataka)
9. She broke my bike - كسرت دراجتي (kasarat darrajati)
10. The shoe fell from your foot - سقط الحذاء من قدمك (saqat al-hitha'u min qadamika)

### Complex Sentences:
- **You ate mango and apple in your garden and watermelon and cucumber in your field**
- أكلت مانجا وتفاحة في بستانك وأكلت بطيخا وخيارا في حقلك
- (akalta manjan wa tufahatan fi bustanika wa akalta bitikhan wa khiyaran fi haqlika)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Memorization is Critical**: Students must memorize conjugation patterns like Quran recitation for fluency
2. **Translation Direction**: Translating from English to Arabic is more challenging and beneficial than Arabic to English
3. **Preposition Mastery**: Understanding when to use min (من), ma'a (مع), bi (ب), and inda (عند) comes through practice
4. **Homework Assignment**: Memorize fi'l mudari' conjugation table 7.1a (100 repetitions recommended)
5. **Progressive Learning**: Students who haven't mastered fi'l madhi conjugation will struggle with fi'l mudari'
6. **Harakah Emphasis**: Always pronounce and indicate proper vowel endings (fatha, damma, kasra)
7. **Next Session Focus**: Fi'l mudari' (present/future tense) conjugation patterns and rules
