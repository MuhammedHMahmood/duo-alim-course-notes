## Grammar Rules Covered

### Mubtada and Khabar Rules
- **Mubtada (Subject)** must always be **Ma'rifa (definite)**
- **Khabar (Predicate)** must always be **Nakira (indefinite)**
- Both must agree in **gender** (masculine/feminine)
- Proper nouns are always definite by default, even with Tanween
- Common nouns become definite by adding **Alif Lam (ال)**

### Definiteness Rules
- All proper nouns (Arabic and non-Arabic names) are inherently definite
- Female names ending in **Ta Marbuta** do not take Tanween
- Names like Aisha, Zainab, Mariam are definite without Alif Lam
- Common nouns require Alif Lam to become definite when used as Mubtada

### Gender Agreement
- Masculine subjects take masculine predicates
- Feminine subjects take feminine predicates
- Example: **Al-bintu saminatun** (The girl is fat) - both feminine

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Proper Noun as Mubtada
- **حَامِدٌ أَبٌ** (Hamidun Abun) - "Hamid is a father"
  - حَامِدٌ: Mubtada, proper noun (definite by nature), Marfu' with Damma
  - أَبٌ: Khabar, common noun (indefinite), Marfu' with Damma

### Common Noun as Mubtada
- **الرَّجُلُ قَوِيٌّ** (Ar-rajulu qawiyyun) - "The man is strong"
  - الرَّجُلُ: Mubtada, made definite with Alif Lam, Marfu' with Damma
  - قَوِيٌّ: Khabar, indefinite adjective, Marfu' with Damma

### Feminine Agreement
- **الأُخْتُ جَمِيلَةٌ** (Al-ukhtu jamilatun) - "The sister is beautiful"
  - الأُخْتُ: Mubtada, feminine, definite with Alif Lam, Marfu' with Damma
  - جَمِيلَةٌ: Khabar, feminine to agree with subject, Marfu' with Damma

## Key Takeaways

1. **Vocabulary Mastery is Essential**: Regular review of word lists is crucial for translation exercises
2. **Gender Agreement**: Always ensure subject and predicate agree in gender
3. **Definiteness Rules**: Mubtada must be definite; use Alif Lam for common nouns
4. **Proper Noun Recognition**: All names are inherently definite, regardless of Tanween
5. **Pronunciation Matters**: Maintain proper Arabic pronunciation when speaking
6. **Synonym Awareness**: Multiple Arabic words can have the same meaning (e.g., حُلْوٌ and سَكَرِيٌّ for "sweet")
7. **Study Schedule**: Word lists assigned on Thursdays, due by next Tuesday class (4-day preparation period)
