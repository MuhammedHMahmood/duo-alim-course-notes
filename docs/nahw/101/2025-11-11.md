## Grammar Rules Covered

### Basic Sentence Structure
- **Fi'il + Fa'il pattern**: Simple verb-subject constructions
- **Fi'il + Fa'il + Maf'ul pattern**: Verb-subject-object constructions
- **Omission of pronouns (dama'ir)**: Arabic verbs contain subject information, making separate pronouns unnecessary unless for emphasis

### Passive Voice (Fi'il Majhul)
- **Naib al-fa'il**: The substitute subject in passive constructions
- **Rule**: The maf'ul (object) of active voice becomes naib al-fa'il in passive voice
- **I'rab**: Naib al-fa'il takes the same grammatical case (damma) as a regular fa'il
- **Key principle**: Passive voice cannot mention the doer of the action

### Definiteness with Alif Lam
- **Alif lam li'l-jins**: Definite article used for categories/general concepts
- **Example**: al-fisq wa al-kufr (sinfulness and disbelief) - referring to the general categories
- **Contrast with English**: Arabic uses alif lam for category meanings where English uses no article

### Verb Conjugation Patterns
- **Future tense marker**: Seen (س) makes verbs exclusively future tense
- **Without seen**: Verbs can indicate present or future depending on context
- **Gender agreement**: Plural irrational (non-human) nouns treated as feminine singular

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Active Voice Examples
- **سَأَقْرَأُ صَحِيفَتَكَ** (Sa'aqra'u sahifataka)
  - سَأَقْرَأُ: Fi'il mudari' with future marker seen
  - صَحِيفَتَكَ: Maf'ul with possessive pronoun

- **يَطْبَخْنَ بَطَاطَا لَكُمْ** (Yatbakhna batata lakum)
  - يَطْبَخْنَ: Fi'il mudari' for plural feminine
  - بَطَاطَا: Maf'ul (nakira/indefinite)
  - لَكُمْ: Prepositional phrase

### Passive Voice Examples
- **يُقْطَعُ الْحَبْلُ** (Yuqta'u al-hablu)
  - يُقْطَعُ: Fi'il mudari' majhul (passive)
  - الْحَبْلُ: Naib al-fa'il with damma

- **تُقْرَأُ الْجَرِيدَةُ فِي الْمَكْتَبَةِ غَدًا** (Tuqra'u al-jarida fil-maktaba ghadan)
  - تُقْرَأُ: Fi'il mudari' majhul (feminine agreement)
  - الْجَرِيدَةُ: Naib al-fa'il
  - فِي الْمَكْتَبَةِ: Prepositional phrase (location)
  - غَدًا: Adverb of time

## Practice Examples

### Translation Exercises (English to Arabic)
1. **They (masc.) came to know** → عَلِمُوا
2. **You (fem. pl.) are reading** → تَقْرَأْنَ
3. **She is cooking** → تَطْبُخُ
4. **We will drink** → نَشْرَبُ
5. **They will cook a potato for you** → يَطْبَخْنَ بَطَاطَا لَكُمْ

### Passive Voice Transformations
1. **The rope is being cut** → يُقْطَعُ الْحَبْلُ
2. **The cloth will be dyed** → يُصْبَغُ الثَّوْبُ
3. **Allah is being remembered** → يُذْكَرُ اللهُ
4. **The newspaper will be read tomorrow** → تُقْرَأُ الْجَرِيدَةُ غَدًا

### Complex Sentence Constructions
- **Your uncle will open the lock with his key** → يَفْتَحُ عَمُّكُمُ الْقُفْلَ بِمِفْتَاحِهِ
- **Are you (fem. pl.) stopping them from sinfulness and disbelief?** → هَلْ تَمْنَعْنَ هُمْ مِنَ الْفِسْقِ وَالْكُفْرِ؟

## Key Takeaways

1. **Pronoun Omission**: Avoid using unnecessary pronouns (dama'ir) as Arabic verbs already indicate the subject
2. **Future Tense**: Adding seen (س) makes verbs exclusively future; without it, context determines present or future meaning
3. **Passive Voice Structure**: In fi'il majhul, the original maf'ul becomes naib al-fa'il and takes damma
4. **Definiteness Rules**: Alif lam can indicate categories/general concepts, not just specific definiteness
5. **Gender Agreement**: Plural non-human nouns are treated as feminine singular for verb agreement
6. **Word Order Flexibility**: Time adverbs and prepositional phrases can be placed at different positions in the sentence
7. **Preposition Usage**: Certain verbs require specific prepositions (e.g., sakhita 'ala, not sakhita min)
