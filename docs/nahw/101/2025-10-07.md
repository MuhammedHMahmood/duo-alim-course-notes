## Grammar Rules Covered

### Essential Sentence Structure Rules:
1. **Jumlah Fi'liya** (Verbal Sentence): Sentences beginning with a verb
2. **Jumlah Ismiya** (Nominal Sentence): Sentences beginning with a noun
3. **Essential Components of Jumlah Fi'liya**:
   - **Fi'l** (verb) - required
   - **Fa'il** (subject/doer) - required  
   - **Maf'ul** (object) - sometimes necessary for complete meaning

### Position Rules:
- **Fa'il can never come before the fi'l**
- Fa'il can be:
  - Attached to the fi'l (as pronoun)
  - Implied in the fi'l
  - After the fi'l

### I'rab (Case) Rules:
1. **Fa'il is always in Raf' case** (no exceptions)
   - Usually marked with dhamma, but not always
2. **Maf'ul is always in Nasb case** (no exceptions)
   - Marked with fatha

### Mudaf-Mudaf Ilayh in Sentences:
- Mudaf-Mudaf Ilayh combinations can function as fa'il or maf'ul
- Case is determined by the mudaf (first word)
- Mudaf ilayh is always in jarr case (genitive)

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Active Voice Examples:
- **nasaru** (they helped) - jama' mudhakkar ghaib
- **fatahat** (she opened) - wahid mu'annas ghaib  
- **darabtum** (you all hit) - jama' mudhakkar hadir

### Passive Voice Examples:
- **nuṣiru** (I was helped) - wahid mutakallim
- **ẓulimat** (she was oppressed) - wahid mu'annas ghaib
- **khuliqtum** (you were created) - jama' mudhakkar hadir

### Sentence Structure Examples:
1. **Fataha Hamidun al-baba** (Hamid opened the door)
   - Fataha: fi'l
   - Hamidun: fa'il (raf' case)
   - al-baba: maf'ul (nasb case)

2. **Fataha khadimu Mahmudin al-baba** (Mahmud's servant opened the door)
   - khadimu Mahmudin: fa'il (mudaf-mudaf ilayh combination)
   - khadimu: mudaf in raf' case
   - Mahmudin: mudaf ilayh in jarr case

## Key Takeaways

1. **Regular attendance is crucial** - recordings cannot substitute live instruction
2. **I'rab knowledge is essential** - case endings determine grammatical function
3. **Fa'il position rule is absolute** - never precedes the verb
4. **Case rules are consistent** - fa'il always raf', maf'ul always nasb
5. **Mudaf combinations** can function as complete grammatical units
6. **Context determines meaning** - objects often necessary for complete understanding
7. **Systematic practice** with conjugations builds strong foundation
