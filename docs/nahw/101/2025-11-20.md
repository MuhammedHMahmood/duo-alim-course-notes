## Grammar Rules Covered

### Mawsuf Sifa (Adjective-Noun Constructions)
- The described noun (mawsuf) comes first, followed by the adjective (sifa)
- Both must agree in:
  - Gender (masculine/feminine)
  - Number (singular/plural)  
  - Definiteness (definite/indefinite)
  - Case (nominative/accusative/genitive)

### Tajweed Pronunciation Rule
- When a noun ends with tanween followed by a noun beginning with al-Iflam, add a tiny noon (ن) with kasra for smooth pronunciation
- Example: سعيدٌ الابن becomes سعيدُنِ الابن (Saeedun al-ibnu)
- This noon has no grammatical meaning, only aids pronunciation

### Proper Noun Descriptions
- When describing proper nouns (like Tariq), the adjective takes al-Iflam because proper nouns are definite
- Example: طارق الشجاع (Tariq ash-shuja'u) - "Brave Tariq"

### Title and Name Order
- Title first: الطبيب محمود (At-Tabeeb Mahmood) - "Dr. Mahmood"
- Name first for Mawsuf Sifa practice: محمود الطبيب (Mahmood at-tabeeb) - "Mahmood the doctor"

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Mawsuf Sifa Examples
- **أبٌ صالحٌ** (Abun salihun) - "Righteous father" (both nominative)
- **سعيدٌ الابن** (Saeedun al-ibnu) - "Saeed the son" (both nominative)
- **الرب الغفور** (Ar-rabb al-ghafoor) - "The most forgiving Lord" (both nominative)
- **مقالةً جيدةً** (Maqalatan jayyidatan) - "Good article" (both accusative, feminine)

### Complete Sentence Examples
- **ضربتُ رجلاً فاسقاً** (Darabtu rajulan fasiqan) - "I hit an immoral man"
  - ضربت: verb, first person singular past
  - رجلاً: direct object, accusative
  - فاسقاً: adjective describing رجل, accusative

- **شربت المرأة المريضة الدواء المر** (Sharibat al-imra'at al-mareeda ad-dawa' al-murr)
  - شربت: verb, third person feminine singular
  - المرأة المريضة: subject (fā'il), nominative case
  - الدواء المر: direct object (maf'ūl), accusative case

## Key Takeaways

1. **Mawsuf Sifa Agreement**: Always ensure the adjective matches the noun in gender, number, definiteness, and case

2. **Word Order**: In Arabic, the described noun comes before the describing adjective, unlike English

3. **Tajweed Rule**: Use the connecting noon when tanween meets al-Iflam for smooth pronunciation

4. **Definiteness in Descriptions**: When describing definite nouns (especially proper nouns), the adjective must also be definite

5. **Case Agreement**: In complete sentences, pay attention to whether nouns are subjects (nominative) or objects (accusative)
