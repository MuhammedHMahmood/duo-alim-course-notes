## Grammar Rules Covered

### 1. Review of Arabic Grammar Map Structure
- **Kalām** → **Mauhūmāl** (meaningful) and **Mauḍū'** (structured)
- **Mauḍū'** → **Muraqab** (compound) and **Mufred** (single words)
- **Mufred** → **Ism** (noun), **Fi'l** (verb), **Ḥarf** (particle)
- **Muraqab** → **Jumla mufīda** (meaningful sentences) and **Jumla ghayr mufīda** (non-meaningful phrases)

### 2. Muraqab Tauṣīfī vs Jumla Ismiyya Rules
- **Muraqab Tauṣīfī**: Both words agree in all four qualities (gender, number, i'rāb, definiteness)
- **Jumla Ismiyya**: Mubtada is ma'rifah (definite), khabar is nakira (indefinite)

### 3. Mubtada and Khabar Formation Rules
- **General Rule**: First word (mubtada) must be ma'rifah, second word (khabar) must be nakira, both in rafā' case
- **Proper Nouns**: Arabic male names can have tanwīn and remain ma'rifah
- **Common Nouns**: Require alif-lām to become definite
- **Gender Agreement**: Feminine mubtada requires feminine khabar (add tā' marbūṭa)

### 4. Alif-Lām and Tanwīn Rules
- **Contradiction Rule**: Alif-lām and tanwīn cannot coexist on the same word
- **Proper Noun Exception**: Arabic male names keep tanwīn without alif-lām
- **Non-Arabic Names**: No tanwīn, no alif-lām allowed (Ibrāhīm, Ismā'īl, Mūsā)
- **Female Names**: Generally no tanwīn (Zaynab, Fāṭima)

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Example Set 1: Distinguishing Phrases from Sentences
1. **Al-Masjidu al-kabīru** - "The big mosque" (Muraqab Tauṣīfī - both ma'rifah)
2. **Al-Masjidu kabīrun** - "The mosque is big" (Jumla Ismiyya - ma'rifah + nakira)  
3. **Masjidun kabīrun** - "A big mosque" (Muraqab Tauṣīfī - both nakira)
4. **Masjidu al-kabīri** - Invalid unless al-kabīr is a person's name (then Muḍāf Muḍāf ilayh)

### Example Set 2: Proper Noun Sentences
- **Maḥmūdun 'ālimun** - "Mahmoud is knowledgeable"
  - Maḥmūdun: Mubtada (proper noun, ma'rifah despite tanwīn)
  - 'Ālimun: Khabar (nakira with tanwīn)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Foundation Strength Indicator**: Understanding the difference between muraqab tauṣīfī and jumla ismiyya is crucial for advanced grammar study

2. **Critical Translation Rule**: "Give two ḍammas to both words" is misleading - the actual rule is making mubtada ma'rifah and khabar nakira, both in rafā' case

3. **Proper Noun Behavior**: Arabic male names are inherently definite even with tanwīn, while non-Arabic names follow different rules

4. **Gender Agreement Principle**: Feminine mubtada requires feminine khabar through addition of tā' marbūṭa

5. **Definiteness Hierarchy**: Alif-lām and tanwīn are mutually exclusive, with specific exceptions for proper nouns

6. **Study Method**: Master vocabulary first, then grammar application becomes manageable in class exercises
