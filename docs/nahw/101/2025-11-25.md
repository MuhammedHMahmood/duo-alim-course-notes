## Grammar Rules Covered

### Formation of Nahi (Negative Commands)

**Two-step process:**
1. **Add لا (la) for negation** - Called "la-nahi" (لا النهي)
   - Place directly before the verb
   - Example: تفعل becomes لا تفعل

2. **Apply Jazm to the final letter**
   - Replace dhamma (ضمة) with sukoon (سكون) 
   - Example: لا تفعل becomes لا تفعل

**Key Rules for Nahi:**
- Made from Fi'l Mudareh (present tense) for second person only
- When verb becomes sakin (has sukoon), drop noon al-wiqaya (نون الوقاية)
- Noon al-niswa (نون النسوة) is NOT dropped
- Much simpler than Amr formation (2 steps vs 4 steps)

**Complete Nahi Conjugation Pattern:**
- لا تفعل (don't do - masc. sing.)
- لا تفعلا (don't do - dual)
- لا تفعلوا (don't do - masc. plural)
- لا تفعلي (don't do - fem. sing.)
- لا تفعلا (don't do - fem. dual)
- لا تفعلن (don't do - fem. plural)

### Noon Al-Wiqaya vs Noon Al-Niswa
- **Noon Al-Niswa**: Only for feminine plurals (3rd and 2nd person) - never dropped
- **Noon Al-Wiqaya**: All other instances of noon - dropped when verb becomes sakin

## I'rab (Sentence Analysis) Examples

### Preposition Usage with Verbs:
- **قرب يقرب** - used with من or إلى
  - لا تقرب إلى الكرة (Don't go near the ball)
- **حذر يحذر** - used with من
  - احذر من الحية (Be cautious of the snake)
- **استمع** - direct object, no preposition needed in Arabic
  - اسمع كلام الله (Listen to Allah's speech)

### Mudaf-Mudaf Ilayh Constructions:
- نصيحة أمهاتكن (advice of your mothers)
- كلام الله (speech of Allah)
- مدير الجريدة (manager of the newspaper)

### Translation Exercises:
**Arabic to English:**
- اذهب (Go)
- لا تذهب (Don't go)
- لا تمنعوا (Don't prevent/stop - plural male)
- لا تبدئي (Don't start - fem. sing.)

**English to Arabic:**
- Don't go to the market, but go to the mosque → لا تذهبوا إلى السوق بل اذهبوا إلى المسجد
- Open the box → افتح الصندوق
- Work for yourselves → اعملوا لأنفسكم
- Don't laugh a lot → لا تضحكوا كثيراً

## Key Takeaways

1. **Nahi formation is simpler than Amr** - only 2 steps vs 4 steps for imperative
2. **Always use second person** for both Amr and Nahi (person being addressed)
3. **Preposition usage varies by verb** - must be memorized, no set rules
4. **Vocabulary memorization is crucial** for proper sentence construction
5. **Practice tables regularly** - essential for fluent conjugation
6. **Mudare knowledge required** - Nahi patterns depend on knowing present tense forms
7. **Context determines translation** - Arabic prepositions don't always match English equivalents
