## Session Overview

This session focused on the **Fi'l Mudari' Majhul** (Imperfect Passive Voice), reviewing how to form the passive from the active imperfect, and applying it to sentence parsing and translation exercises. The teacher connected Sarf concepts heavily with Nahw (grammar), emphasizing that the two disciplines are inseparable. The session also briefly introduced the **Huruf Nasb** (particles that render nasb to the imperfect verb) and previewed the upcoming topic of **Huruf Jazm**.

## Word Patterns (Awzan) Covered

### Fi'l Mudari' Majhul (Imperfect Passive Voice)

**Two rules to form the Mudari' Majhul from the Mudari' Ma'ruf:**

1. **Prefix letter** → give it a **Dhamma** (ضمة)
2. **Original middle letter** of the root → give it a **Fatha** (فتحة)

| Ma'ruf (Active) | Majhul (Passive) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| يَنْصُرُ (yanṣuru) | يُنْصَرُ (yunṣaru) | Prefix ya gets ḍamma; middle ṣad gets fatḥa |
| يَسْمَعُ (yasma'u) | يُسْمَعُ (yusma'u) | Fatḥa already on middle letter; only change prefix |
| يَشْرَبُ (yashrabu) | يُشْرَبُ (yushrabu) | Prefix ya gets ḍamma; middle rā gets fatḥa |
| يُرْسَلُ (yursalu) | يُرْسَلُ (yursalu) | Advanced pattern (Akrama/Ukrimu bāb); second-to-last letter gets fatḥa |

> **Key principle:** If the middle letter already has a fatḥa in the Ma'ruf, only change the prefix to ḍamma — no second change needed.

---

### Huruf Nasb (Particles Rendering Nasb to the Mudari')

The particles **أنْ، لنْ، كَيْ، إذَن** (an, lan, kay, idhan) render **nasb** to the following Fi'l Mudari'. The sign of nasb is:

| Form | Ma'ruf | After Huruf Nasb (Nasb) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Singular (مفرد) | يَفْعَلُ | يَفْعَلَ | Dhamma → Fatḥa on last letter |
| Dual (مثنى) | يَفْعَلَانِ | يَفْعَلَا | Nūn dropped |
| Masc. Plural (جمع مذكر سالم) | يَفْعَلُونَ | يَفْعَلُوا | Nūn dropped; alif added for clarity |
| Fem. Plural (جمع مؤنث سالم) | يَفْعَلْنَ | يَفْعَلْنَ | **No change** — nūn is not dropped |

> **Note:** The nūns of the two female plurality forms (Ghā'ib and Ḥāḍir) are **not** dropped because dropping them would cause confusion with other patterns. This brings the total to all **14 conjugation forms**.

**لَنْ (lan):** Turns the positive imperfect into a **negative future** — "will never / shall not." E.g., لَنْ يَفْعَلَ (lan yaf'ala) = "he will never do."

## Verb Conjugation Tables

### Forming Majhul from Ma'ruf — Summary Rule

> **Prefix** → ḍamma | **Original middle root letter** → fatḥa

### Example: نَصَرَ — يَنْصُرُ → يُنْصَرُ

| Person | Ma'ruf (Active) | Majhul (Passive) |
|---|---|---|
| هو (3rd m. sg.) | يَنْصُرُ | يُنْصَرُ |
| هي (3rd f. sg.) | تَنْصُرُ | تُنْصَرُ |

### Example: يُسْأَلُ (from سَأَلَ)

Used in the Quranic context: **لَا يُسْأَلُ** (he will not be asked) — passive voice, with لا as a negating particle (ḥarf nāfi).

| # | Description | Before (Ma'ruf) | After أنْ/لنْ/كي |

| 2–3 | 3rd m. dual | يَفْعَلَانِ | يَفْعَلَا (nūn dropped) |
| 4–6 | 3rd m. plural | يَفْعَلُونَ | يَفْعَلُوا (nūn dropped) |

## Root Analysis

### يُسْأَلُ
- Root: س-أ-ل
- Bāb: نَصَرَ pattern
- Mudari' Ma'ruf: يَسْأَلُ
- Mudari' Majhul: يُسْأَلُ
- Used in: لَا يُسْأَلُ عَمَّا يَفْعَلُ وَهُمْ يُسْأَلُونَ (Quranic reference)

### يُشْرَبُ
- Root: ش-ر-ب
- Bāb: نَصَرَ pattern
- Mudari' Ma'ruf: يَشْرَبُ
- Mudari' Majhul: يُشْرَبُ (prefix ḍamma, rā gets fatḥa)

### يُؤْكَلُ / يُقْتَلُ / يُفْهَمُ / يُتْرَكُ / يُرْسَلُ / يُهْزَمُ / يُفْتَحُ / يُقْرَأُ
All follow the same two-change rule (prefix ḍamma + middle root letter fatḥa).

### يُرْسَلُ (from أَرْسَلَ)
- Root: ر-س-ل — but verb is from the **Akrama/Ukrimu bāb** (Form IV — not yet formally studied)
- Mudari' Ma'ruf: يُرْسِلُ (sin has kasra)
- Mudari' Majhul: يُرْسَلُ (sin gets fatḥa — second-to-last letter rule still applies)

| لَا يُسْأَلُ عَمَّا يَفْعَلُ | He will not be asked about what He does | لا = ḥarf nāfi; يُسْأَلُ = fi'l mudari' majhūl |

| يُؤْكَلُ الْيَوْمَ السَّمَكُ وَالْأُرُزُّ | Today, fish and rice are being eaten | الْيَوْمَ = ẓarf zamān/maf'ūl fīhi; السَّمَكُ، الْأُرُزُّ = nā'ib fā'il |
| يُقْتَلُ الْعَسْكَرِيُّ فِي الْمُحَارَبَةِ | The soldier is killed in battle | فِي الْمُحَارَبَةِ = jar-majrūr (muta'alliq) |
| هَلْ يُفْهَمُ اللِّسَانُ الْهِنْدِيُّ فِي مَكَّةَ؟ | Is Hindi understood in Makkah? | هَلْ = ḥarf istifhām; اللِّسَانُ الْهِنْدِيُّ = nā'ib fā'il (ṣifa-mawṣūf); مَكَّةَ = majrūr but shows with fatḥa (ممنوع من الصرف) |

### English → Arabic Translation Exercises

| English | Arabic | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The door of the school is being opened | يُفْتَحُ الْبَابُ | الْبَابُ = nā'ib fā'il, must be marfū' (ḍamma) |
| He is being sent to Medina | يُرْسَلُ إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ | From Akrama bāb; second-to-last letter gets fatḥa |
| The disbelievers will be defeated | يُهْزَمُ الْكَافِرُونَ | Verb stays singular even though nā'ib fā'il is plural (verb-first rule) |
| The car will be left on the road | تُتْرَكُ السَّيَّارَةُ عَلَى الطَّرِيقِ | Verb is tā' (4th form) because سَيَّارَة is feminine (mu'annath); third person, not second |
| The book will be read today | يُقْرَأُ الْكِتَابُ الْيَوْمَ | الْيَوْمَ = ẓarf zamān |

> **Critical Nahw rule applied:** When the nā'ib fā'il (or fā'il) comes **after** the verb, the verb is always **singular** — it does not take a plural form. The plural of the noun is shown on the noun itself, not on the verb.

> **Gender rule:** When the nā'ib fā'il is **feminine**, use the **4th conjugation form** (tā' prefix) for the verb — e.g., تُتْرَكُ for سَيَّارَة.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Two-change rule for Mudari' Majhul:** Prefix → ḍamma; original middle root letter → fatḥa. If the fatḥa is already there, only change the prefix.

2. **Haraka changes are critical:** A single vowel change can flip an active verb to passive (or vice versa), completely changing meaning — or produce gibberish. This makes precision in tajwīd and Arabic reading essential.

3. **Nā'ib Fā'il is always marfū':** In a passive sentence, the deputy doer takes the place of the fā'il and must carry a ḍamma (or equivalent).

4. **Verb stays singular before an explicit nā'ib fā'il:** Even if the nā'ib fā'il is plural or dual, the verb preceding it remains in the singular form.

5. **Huruf Nasb (أن، لن، كي، إذن):** These particles govern the following mudari' verb, rendering it manṣūb. Sign of nasb: ḍamma → fatḥa on last letter; or deletion of the nūn (for dual and sound masculine plural). The two feminine plural forms do **not** lose their nūn.

6. **لَنْ specifically** negates the imperfect and pushes the action into the future with certainty: "will never / shall not."

7. **Sarf and Nahw are inseparable:** Sarf produces the word pattern; Nahw governs its role in the sentence. Neither discipline is complete without the other.

8. **Makkah is ghayr munsarif:** مَكَّة does not take tanwīn and shows kasra-state with a fatḥa — a foundational Nahw concept applied in these exercises.
