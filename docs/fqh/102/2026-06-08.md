## Session Overview

This session covered two major topics: (1) the invalidators of wudu (Nawaqid al-Wudu) — nine things which, if they occur, break one's wudu and place a person in a state of hadath; and (2) things that resemble invalidators of wudu but do not actually break it. The session also began the chapter on the obligatory acts (fara'id) and sunnahs of ghusl, covering the three obligatory acts and nine sunnahs of ghusl.

---

## Key Themes

1. **Nawaqid al-Wudu** — what breaks wudu and the specific conditions under which each invalidator applies
2. **Things resembling invalidators but not breaking wudu** — important distinctions in conditions (e.g., blood staying in its place vs. flowing)
3. **Fara'id al-Ghusl** — the three obligatory acts that make ghusl valid
4. **Sunnan al-Ghusl** — nine recommended acts that complete ghusl in the best manner

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Nawaqid al-Wudu (Invalidators of Wudu)

Wudu is broken (yantaqidu al-wudu) when any one of the following nine things occurs:

**1. Something exits from one of the two private parts (السَّبِيلَيْن)**
- Urine, excrement, or wind — all break wudu
- Applies to exit from the front or back private part

**2. Blood, pus, or wound fluid that moves from its place**
- If blood, pus, or clear wound liquid comes out and crosses (تَجَاوَزَ) to an area beyond the wound itself (i.e., reaches skin that would normally require cleaning), wudu is broken
- **Key condition:** The substance must move on its own from its place
- If the blood stays within the wound and does not flow outward, wudu remains intact
- This same ruling applies to pus and the clear fluid that seeps from a healing or unhealed wound

**3. Blood from the mouth that equals or exceeds the saliva**
- If blood from anywhere in the mouth is more than (غَلَبَ) the saliva, or equal to it (أَوْ سَوَاهُ), wudu is broken
- If saliva is more than the blood, wudu remains intact
- Practical example: brushing teeth after wudu — if gums bleed heavily (blood ≥ saliva), wudu must be remade

**4. Vomiting a mouthful**
- Wudu is broken if one vomits: food, water, a blood clot (عَلَق), bile (مَرَّة), or phlegm — provided it is a mouthful (مِلْءُ الْفَمِ)
- **Definition of mouthful:** the vomit fills the mouth to the point that speech is difficult or impossible
- A small burp that brings up a little food is NOT a mouthful — wudu is not broken
- Stomach acid is included in this ruling, as it is something being vomited up

**5. Falling asleep such that the buttocks leave the ground**
- If a person falls asleep while sitting and their backside does not remain stable on the ground (they keep tipping/swaying over), wudu is broken
- Likewise, if the buttocks of a sleeping person rises off the ground before they awaken (i.e., they are in a deep sleep and fall over), wudu is broken
- The key criterion: buttocks are no longer firm and stable on the ground

**6. Fainting (الإغماء)**
- If a person loses consciousness (أُغْمِيَ عَلَيْهِ), wudu is broken

**7. Losing one's sanity (الجنون)**
- If a person becomes insane (جُنَّ), wudu is broken

**8. Becoming intoxicated (السُّكر)**
- If a person becomes intoxicated (سَكِرَ), wudu is broken

**9. Laughing out loud during salah (القَهْقَهَة)**
- Conditions for this to break wudu:
  1. The person must be **mature** (بَالِغ — has reached puberty)
  2. The person must be **awake** (يَقْظَان — not asleep)
  3. It must occur **during salah** (فِي صَلَاة)
  4. The salah must contain **bowing and prostration** (ذَاتُ رُكُوعٍ وَسُجُود) — i.e., any fard or nafl salah (not Salat al-Janaza or Sajdat al-Tilawa)
- Wudu is **not** broken by laughing out loud in the following cases:
  - A **child** laughs out loud
  - A **sleeping person** laughs out loud during salah (even in a lighter sleep, as it is not a conscious act)
  - Laughing out loud during **Salat al-Janaza** (no ruku/sujud)
  - Laughing out loud during **Sajdat al-Tilawa** (no ruku/sujud)

### Things That Resemble Nawaqid but Do NOT Break Wudu

**1. Blood appears but does not move from its place**
- Blood comes to the surface of a wound but stays within it — wudu intact

**2. Flesh falls from the body without blood flowing**
- Example: 'Irq al-Madani (عِرْقُ الْمَدَنِي / "Naru") — a skin disease, common in hot regions, where pieces of flesh fall off
- As long as no blood flows from the wound, wudu is intact
- The main condition is: blood does not come out (لَمْ يَسِلْ مِنْهُ الدَّم)

**3. A worm exits from a wound or from the ear**
- This does not break wudu

**4. Vomiting less than a mouthful**
- If a person vomits, but it is not a mouthful, wudu does not break

**5. Vomiting phlegm (بَلْغَم)**
- Whether the phlegm is a little or a lot, vomiting phlegm alone does not break wudu

**6. A person performing salah falls asleep while in a valid prayer position**
- If a person falls asleep while standing, sitting, bowing (ruku), or prostrating (sujud), and remains in the correct form (على صِفَةِ السُّنَّة) — i.e., still appears to be praying — wudu does not break
- If, however, his hands drop loose (no longer in proper form while standing), wudu breaks, and therefore salah also breaks

**7. A person with wudu falls asleep with their buttocks firm on the ground**
- If a person with wudu falls asleep while sitting and their backside remains firm (مُتَمَكِّنَة) on the ground, wudu does not break

**8. Touching one's own private part**
- Touching one's own private part with the hand does not break wudu (Hanafi position)

**9. Touching a woman**
- Touching a woman does not break wudu (Hanafi position)

**10. Swaying while asleep**
- If a sleeping person sways (as long as their backside remains on the ground), wudu does not break

### Fara'id al-Ghusl (Obligatory Acts of Ghusl)

There are **three** obligatory acts of ghusl. If any one is missed, ghusl is invalid:

1. **Al-Madmada (الْمَضْمَضَة)** — Rinsing the mouth
2. **Al-Istinshaq (الِاسْتِنْشَاق)** — Cleaning/sniffing water into the nose
3. **Isal al-Ma' ila Jami' al-Badan (إِيصَالُ الْمَاءِ إِلَى جَمِيعِ الْبَدَنِ)** — Making water reach every part of the body, such that not a single dry spot remains

---

### Sunnan al-Ghusl (Recommended Acts of Ghusl)

Observing these sunnahs completes the ghusl in the best manner (عَلَى وَجْهٍ أَكْمَل):

1. **Reciting Bismillah** before beginning the ghusl
2. **Making the intention (niyyah)** for ghusl — to attain purity (tahara)
3. **Washing the hands up to and including the wrists** first, just as one does in wudu
4. **Washing away any impurities** on the body or clothing before beginning the ghusl
5. **Performing wudu before ghusl** — however, if standing in a lowered/steep place where water gathers (مُنْخَفِض) at the feet, one delays washing the feet until the end (so the feet are washed clean after all impure pooled water has been cleared)
6. **Pouring water over the entire body three times**
7. **Pouring water in the following order:** first over the head, then right shoulder (مَنْكِب أَيْمَن), then left shoulder (مَنْكِب أَيْسَر)
8. **Rubbing the body (دَلْك)** while washing — not merely pouring water over it
9. **Washing the body continuously (مُتَوَالِيًا)** — such that the first limb/area does not dry before the second is washed

**Note on flowing water:** If a person enters a flowing body of water (river, stream) or a large reservoir that is considered in the ruling of flowing water, remains in it, and rubs their body — they have completed the sunnah of ghusl.

## Conditions & Categories

### Conditions for Laughing Out Loud (Qahqaha) Breaking Wudu

All four conditions must be met simultaneously:

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Maturity (بُلُوغ) | Must be a mature person (post-puberty); does not apply to children |
| Wakefulness (يَقَظَة) | Must be awake; sleeping person laughing is not a conscious act |
| During Salah | Must occur while in the prayer |
| Prayer contains Ruku & Sujud | Applies to all fard and nafl salah; excludes Janaza and Sajdat al-Tilawa |

### Conditions for Blood/Pus Breaking Wudu

| State of Blood/Pus | Ruling |
|---|---|
| Appears on wound surface but stays in place | Wudu intact |
| Moves (flows) from its place by itself | Wudu broken |
| From the mouth — saliva is more | Wudu intact |
| From the mouth — blood = or > saliva | Wudu broken |

### Conditions for Sleep Breaking Wudu

| Sleep Scenario | Ruling |
|---|---|
| Asleep sitting, backside firm on ground | Wudu intact |
| Asleep sitting, swaying/tipping (backside unstable) | Wudu broken |
| Asleep, buttocks rise off ground before waking | Wudu broken |
| Asleep lying down | Wudu broken |
| Asleep in salah in correct form (standing, sitting, ruku, sujud) | Wudu intact |
| Asleep in salah, hands fall (loses sunnah form) | Wudu broken |

## Scholarly Opinions

- The rulings covered follow the **Hanafi madhab**, as is the standard for this course.
- The ruling that **touching a woman** or **touching one's own private part** does not break wudu is specific to the Hanafi position (contrasting with Shafi'i and Maliki positions where these do break wudu).
- The ruling on **qahqaha** (loud laughter in salah breaking wudu) is a position particular to the Hanafi school. Other madhabs do not hold this view.

## Lessons & Practical Application

1. **Brushing teeth after wudu:** Check the amount of blood vs. saliva when brushing. If gums bleed lightly and saliva is clearly more, wudu remains. If there is a significant amount of blood (equal to or more than saliva), remake wudu before salah.

2. **Wounds during wudu/salah:** A cut or wound does not break wudu unless the blood actually flows out from the wound. As long as blood stays within the wound site, one may continue salah.

3. **Vomiting:** A small reflux or burp with a little food is not a mouthful and does not break wudu. Only when the vomit fills the mouth (making speech difficult) is it considered a mouthful.

4. **Dozing off:** Nodding off while seated with one's backside on the ground (e.g., during a lecture or after salah) does not break wudu. Falling into a deep sleep where one falls over does.

5. **Salah with drowsiness:** If a person is very sleepy in salah and sways but maintains proper form, their wudu and salah are intact. The moment the proper form is lost (e.g., hands drop while standing), wudu breaks.

6. **Ghusl practice:** Begin ghusl with Bismillah and niyyah, wash hands first, remove any impurities, perform wudu (delaying feet if water pools), then pour water over the head, right shoulder, and left shoulder — rubbing the body throughout — and wash continuously without letting any part dry.

7. **Ghusl in a shower:** The sunnah of delaying washing the feet applies when water pools at one's feet (e.g., a clogged drain). Wash feet last so that accumulated used water on the floor can be washed away cleanly.
