## Session Overview

This session continued the study of Tahara (purification) by covering the five categories of water and their rulings (ahkam). The lesson focused on understanding different types of water that can or cannot be used for purification, building upon previous lessons on the introduction to purification and types of water through which purity can be obtained.

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Category 1: Pure, Purifying, Not Disliked
- **Water type:** Absolute water (ma'ul mutlaq)
- **Ruling:** Pure (tahir), purifying (mutahir), not disliked (ghayr makruh)
- **Usage:** Can be used for all purification purposes without any dislike

### Category 2: Pure, Purifying, but Disliked
- **Water type:** Water drunk from by cats, chickens, carnivorous birds, or snakes
- **Ruling:** Pure and purifying but mildly disliked (makruh tanzihi) to use
- **Condition:** Only disliked if absolute water is available as an alternative
- **Exception:** No dislike if no other water is found

### Category 3: Pure but Doubtful in Purification
- **Water type:** Water drunk from by donkeys or mules
- **Ruling:** Pure without doubt, but doubt exists whether it's purifying
- **Solution:** If no other water available, person has option (khiyar) to perform either wudu first then tayammum, or tayammum first then wudu

### Category 4: Pure but Not Purifying
- **Water type:** Used water (ma'ul musta'mal)
- **Ruling:** Pure in itself but cannot purify others; wudu and ghusl invalid with this water
- **Definition:** Water used for wudu or ghusl either to remove ritual impurity (raf' hadath) or for reward (qurbah)

### Category 5: Impure Water
- **Water type:** Small amount of stagnant water mixed with impurity
- **Ruling:** Impure (najis)
- **Scope:** Whether the impurity is apparent in the water or not

## Conditions & Categories

### Used Water (Ma'ul Musta'mal) Conditions:

**Water becomes used (musta'mal) when:**
1. Used for wudu or ghusl to remove ritual impurity
2. Used for wudu upon wudu with intention of reward
3. Water separates from the body (fasala 'an jasad) - not when it touches, but when it drips off

**Water does NOT become used when:**
- Person with wudu uses water to cool down or teach wudu
- But if person without wudu uses water to cool down or teach, it becomes musta'mal

### Levels of Dislike:
- **Makruh Tanzihi:** Mildly disliked (can still be used)
- **Makruh Tahrimi:** Prohibitively disliked (should be avoided)

## Lessons & Practical Application

### Key Practical Points:
1. **Hierarchy of water usage:** Use absolute water when available; resort to other categories only when necessary
2. **Emergency situations:** In absence of pure water, specific procedures (wudu + tayammum) provide valid alternatives
3. **Teaching and cooling:** Intentions matter in determining whether water becomes used or remains usable
4. **Water conservation:** Understanding when water can be reused helps in resource management during purification

### Modern Applications:
- Understanding these categories helps in travel situations or areas with limited water access
- Provides framework for dealing with uncertainty about water purity
- Guides proper water usage and conservation in Islamic contexts
- Clarifies when alternatives like tayammum become necessary
