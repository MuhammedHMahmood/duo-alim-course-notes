## Session Overview

This session covered the rulings of water mixed with pure substances and the classifications of leftover water (su'r) from different animals. The discussion focused on determining when water remains valid for wudu based on the qualities (taste, color, smell) that are changed, and the specific rulings for water left over after various creatures have drunk from it.

---

## Key Themes

- **Water Purity Classifications**: Understanding when mixed water can still be used for purification vs. wudu
- **Quality-Based Rulings**: How the number of changed characteristics (taste, color, smell) determines water's usability
- **Weight Determination**: Using weight ratios to determine validity when qualities aren't distinguishable
- **Animal-Based Su'r Categories**: Four distinct categories of leftover water based on the animal that drank from it
- **Practical Applications**: Real-world scenarios involving detergent, milk, vinegar, and rose water

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Mixed Water Classifications:

**Two-Quality Substances (like milk):**
- If one quality (color, taste) changes → water invalid for wudu
- Example: Milk has color and taste but no smell

**Three-Quality Substances (like vinegar):**
- If two out of three qualities change → water invalid for wudu
- Vinegar has color, taste, and smell

**No-Quality Substances (like used water):**
- Determination by weight ratio (تُعْتَبَرُ الْغَلَبَةُ بِالْوَزْنِ)
- If pure water outweighs impure → valid for wudu
- If impure water outweighs pure → invalid for wudu

**Used Water Status:**
- Pure but cannot be reused for wudu/ghusl
- Can still remove najasa from objects
- تُزَالُ بِهِ النَّجَاسَةُ وَلَكِنْ لَا تَحْصُلُ طَهَارَةٌ بِهِ الْحُكْمِيَّةُ

## Conditions & Categories

### Four Categories of Su'r (Leftover Water):

**Category 1 - Pure and Permissible:**
- Human beings (Muslim or kafir, pure or junub) - if no najasa in mouth
- Horses - without any karaha
- Animals whose meat is permissible (camel, cow, goat)

**Category 2 - Pure but Makruh Tanzihi:**
- Cats (if no najasa in mouth and other water available)
- Predatory birds (falcon, eagle)
- House-dwelling animals (mice)

**Category 3 - Doubtful Purifying Ability:**
- Mules and donkeys
- Pure water but doubt about validity for wudu
- If no other water: perform wudu + tayammum, then pray

**Category 4 - Impure (Najis):**
- Pigs
- Dogs  
- All predatory land animals (lions, leopards, wolves)
- Cannot be used for any purification

## Scholarly Opinions

The text presents the Hanafi position on these matters without extensive discussion of other madhabs. The rulings appear to be established scholarly consensus within the Hanafi school, particularly regarding:

- The weight-based determination for substances without distinguishable qualities
- The four-category classification system for animal leftover water
- The principle that animals whose su'r is pure also have pure sweat, and vice versa

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Daily Life Applications:**
- Detergent mixed with water: can clean clothes but not valid for wudu due to overwhelming qualities
- Milk spilled in water: if taste changes, cannot use for wudu
- Old rose water: if scent gone, weight determines usability

**Travel Considerations:**
- When only questionable water available (mule/donkey leftover): combine wudu + tayammum
- Prioritize finding pure water sources when possible
- Understanding animal categories helps in rural/camping situations
