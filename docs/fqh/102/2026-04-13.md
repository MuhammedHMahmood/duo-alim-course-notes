## Session Overview

This session covered **آداب قضاء الحاجة** (etiquettes of using the restroom). The class began with a hadith where Prophet Muhammad ﷺ compared himself to a father figure when teaching sensitive matters to his companions. The session systematically covered 22 specific etiquettes and rulings related to relieving oneself, emphasizing privacy, cleanliness, respect for sacred directions, and avoiding harm to oneself and others. The class concluded with a discussion on **istibra** (post-urination cleansing for males).

---

## Key Themes

- **Privacy and Modesty**: Going away from people's sight and covering oneself appropriately
- **Cleanliness and Purity**: Proper positioning, avoiding contamination, and post-relief cleansing
- **Respect for Sacred Space**: Avoiding facing the qibla and not defiling places of worship or gathering
- **Consideration for Others**: Not using areas where people benefit or gather
- **Health and Safety**: Avoiding positions or places that could cause harm or splash-back

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Primary Hadith**: "إِنَّمَا أَنَا لَكُمْ بِمَنْزِلَةِ الْوَالِدِ أُعَلِّمُكُمْ" - "Verily I am for you at the level of a father, I teach you..." (Abu Dawud, narrated by Abu Huraira)

**Key Prohibition**: "فَلَا يَسْتَقْبِلِ الْقِبْلَةَ وَلَا يَسْتَدْبِرَهَا وَلَا يَسْتَطِبْ بِيَمِينِهِ" - "Do not face the qibla nor turn your back to it, and do not clean with the right hand"

**Warning Hadith**: "اسْتَنْزِهُوا مِنَ الْبَوْلِ فَإِنَّ عَامَّةَ عَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ مِنْهُ" - "Be careful/keep safe from urine, for verily most of the torment of the grave is because of it"

**Quranic Foundation**: "فِيهِ رِجَالٌ يُحِبُّونَ أَن يَتَطَهَّرُوا وَاللَّهُ يُحِبُّ الْمُتَطَهِّرِينَ" - "In it are men who love to purify themselves, and Allah loves those who purify themselves"

## Conditions & Categories

**Mandatory Etiquettes (واجب)**:
- Going away from people's sight until no one can see, hear sounds, or smell odors
- Entering with left foot, exiting with right foot
- Not facing qibla front or back (makruh tahrimi - prohibitively disliked)
- Performing istibra (for males) before considering oneself pure

**Recommended Etiquettes (مستحب)**:
- Choosing soft, lower ground to avoid splash-back
- Saying the dua: "أعوذ بالله من الخبث والخبائث"
- Sitting while leaning on left foot (traditional toilets)
- Covering the head during relief and istinja
- Exiting with the dua: "الحمد لله الذي أذهب عني الأذى وعافاني"

**Prohibited/Disliked Locations**:
- Holes in the ground (danger from animals/creatures)
- Pathways and roads
- Graveyards
- Shaded areas where people gather
- Places where people converse
- Under fruit-bearing trees
- Near wells, rivers, or water basins
- Small amounts of stagnant water
- Areas where ghusl is performed

## Scholarly Opinions

**Modern Applications Discussed**:
- For contemporary toilets that face qibla: slightly turn body away if renovation impossible
- Standing urination: strongly discouraged due to splash-back concerns
- Shower urination: makruh due to potential water stagnation, though modern drainage may mitigate this
- Traditional vs. modern toilet positioning: squatting position aids complete evacuation

**Exception to Speaking Prohibition**: Speaking is **obligatory** if one sees a blind person walking toward danger (like a ditch) while using the restroom - duty to warn overrides the general prohibition.

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Parental Guidance**: The Prophet's approach shows the importance of parents or guardians providing proper education about private matters in appropriate, fatherly/motherly manner, especially regarding purity for those reaching maturity.

**Health Benefits**: Many etiquettes have practical health benefits (proper positioning aids elimination, avoiding splash-back prevents contamination, choosing appropriate locations prevents harm).

**Istibra Process**: Males must ensure complete removal of remaining urine through methods like:
- Walking briefly
- Light coughing 
- Gentle pressure/squats
- Whatever method works for the individual

**Modern Considerations**: 
- Use of "squatty potty" type devices aligns with traditional beneficial positioning
- Toilet paper placement in water bowl can reduce splash-back
- Drainage functionality affects rulings about urinating in ghusl areas

**Post-Relief Dua**: "الحمد لله الذي أذهب عني الأذى وعافاني" (All praise to Allah who has removed impurity from me and granted me good health) - expressing gratitude for bodily functions and health.
