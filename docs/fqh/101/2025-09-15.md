## Session Overview

This session covered the rulings for wiping over casts and bandages during wudu, followed by a comprehensive discussion of menstruation (hayd), postnatal bleeding (nifas), and dysfunctional uterine bleeding (istihada). The class detailed the conditions, durations, and legal implications of each state, including what actions are permissible or prohibited during these periods.

---

## Key Themes

- Flexibility in Islamic law for those with injuries requiring medical attention
- Detailed categorization of different types of bleeding and their rulings
- The importance of understanding minimum and maximum durations for each condition
- Distinction between religious obligations that are suspended versus those that must be made up
- The concept of ma'udur (excused person) in Islamic jurisprudence

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Wiping Over Casts
- **Permissible** to wipe over cast, bandage, or dressing for as long as the excuse remains
- **Hierarchy of washing**: 1) Wash injured limb if possible, 2) Wipe limb directly if washing harmful, 3) Wipe over cast/bandage, 4) Leave entirely if wiping causes harm
- **No purity condition required**: Unlike khuf, person can be in state of major or minor impurity when applying cast
- **Majority wiping suffices**: Not necessary to wipe entire cast, wiping most of it is adequate
- **Cast removal**: If cast falls off before healing, previous prayers remain valid

### Menstruation (Hayd)
- **Definition**: Blood from uterus of non-pregnant, disease-free woman after adolescence
- **Duration**: Minimum 3 days (72 hours), Maximum 10 days (240 hours), Average 5 days
- **Intermittent bleeding**: Breaks within 10-day period still count as menstruation
- **Six prohibitions**: 1) Intercourse, 2) Tawaf, 3) Entering masjid, 4) Touching between navel and knee without barrier, 5) Reciting Quran, 6) Touching Quran without non-attached barrier

### Postnatal Bleeding (Nifas)
- **Definition**: Bleeding after delivery or after most of baby has exited, including after miscarriage with fetal development
- **Duration**: No minimum, Maximum 40 days (960 hours)
- **Same rulings as menstruation** apply during nifas period

### Dysfunctional Uterine Bleeding (Istihada)
- **Cases**: 1) Pregnant woman's bleeding, 2) Bleeding after 40 days postpartum, 3) Bleeding past normal habit exceeding maximum duration, 4) Pre-adolescent girl's bleeding
- **Ruling**: Like chronic nosebleed - does not prevent prayer, fasting, intercourse, or tawaf
- **Wudu requirement**: New wudu for each prayer time

## Conditions & Categories

### Touching Quran Barriers
- **Attached barriers** (impermissible): Gloves, sleeves, book covers attached to Quran
- **Non-attached barriers** (permissible): Separate cloth, hijab not being worn, detached book cover

### Masjid Entry During Menstruation
- **Hanafi position**: Complete prohibition from entering designated masjid boundaries
- **Exception**: Areas not designated as masjid (lobbies, non-prayer rooms, rented halls/gymnasiums)
- **Maliki difference**: Some permit entry with restrictions (sitting on chairs, not prayer rugs)

### Habit Establishment and Changes
- **Fluctuating cycles**: Look to previous month's pattern
- **Exceeding habit but under 10 days**: All days count as menstruation
- **Exceeding maximum (10 days)**: Days beyond habit until day 10 are istihada, requiring makeup prayers

## Scholarly Opinions

### Differences Between Schools
- **Hanafi**: Gloves considered attached barrier for Quran touching
- **Maliki**: May permit gloves for Quran handling in teaching contexts
- **Hanafi**: Strict masjid entry prohibition during menstruation
- **Maliki**: More lenient masjid entry with conditions

### Wiping Over Medical Devices
- **Majority opinion**: Wiping most of cast/bandage suffices
- **Minority opinion**: Must wipe entire cast (considered weaker)

## Lessons & Practical Application

### For Those with Medical Conditions
- Consult qualified Muslim doctors about wound exposure to water
- Follow staged approach: wash if possible → wipe directly → wipe over covering → leave entirely
- Cast falling off doesn't invalidate previous prayers or current prayer

### For Women's Worship
- **Permitted during menstruation/nifas**: Du'as in Arabic, dhikr, listening to Quran, reading hadith (skipping Quran portions), attending non-masjid religious programs
- **Makeup requirements**: Fasts must be made up, prayers are not made up
- **Intimacy guidelines**: Prohibited during menstruation/nifas, permitted during istihada

### Habit Tracking and Recognition
- Establish patterns month by month, using previous cycle as reference
- Any bleeding under maximum duration counts as menstruation if within 10-day limit
- Bleeding beyond personal habit but under 10 days still counts as menstruation
- Medical consultation may be needed for unusual bleeding patterns

### Community Considerations
- Different schools may have varying practices in mixed communities
- Rented prayer spaces (musallas) have different rules than designated masjids
- Teaching contexts may have practical accommodations within scholarly boundaries
