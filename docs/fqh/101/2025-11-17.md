## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Prayer of the Sick Person
1. **Standing difficulty**: Pray sitting with normal bowing and prostration
2. **Prostration difficulty**: Use head movements, making prostration lower than bowing
3. **Sitting impossible**: Pray lying down (back or side) facing qibla with head movements
4. **No head movement possible**: Absolved from prayer during that state
5. **Duration rule**: If unable to move head for 24+ hours, most scholars say no makeup required
6. **Consciousness rule**: If unconscious for more than 5 prayers, no makeup required; less than 5 prayers must be made up

### Fidya (Expiatory Payment)
- **Amount**: Half sa'a (2.2 kg) of wheat or one full sa'a (4.4 kg) of barley, or cash equivalent
- **Source**: Taken from one-third of deceased's estate
- **Recipients**: Must be given to indigent (fuqara)
- **Method**: Cannot pray on behalf of deceased; only monetary compensation allowed

### Sahibu Tarteeb Rules
- **Definition**: Person who has missed less than six salawat
- **Order requirement**: Must make up missed prayers before current ones
- **Four or less rule**: If praying 4 or fewer salawat after missed prayer, they become invalid when makeup is done
- **Five or more rule**: If praying 5+ salawat after missed prayer, previous prayers remain valid
- **Exceptions**: Time constraint or forgetfulness nullify the order requirement

## Conditions & Categories

### Witr Prayer Conditions
- **Mandatory (Wajib)**: 3 rakah with one salam
- **Qunut placement**: Before ruku in third rakah
- **Congregation**: Permissible only in Ramadan for groups
- **Makeup rule**: If joining imam in 3rd rakah (even after qunut), no qunut needed in makeup

### Emphasized Sunnah (Sunnah Mu'akkadah)
**Two Rakah Prayers:**
- Before Fajr
- After Zuhr (2 rakah)
- After Maghrib (2 rakah)
- After Isha before Witr (2 rakah)

**Four Rakah Prayers:**
- Before Zuhr (4 rakah with one salam)
- Before and after Jummah

### Non-Emphasized Sunnah
- 4 rakah before Asr
- 4 rakah before Isha
- 4 rakah after Isha
- 6 rakah after Maghrib (with 3 salams)
- Tahiyyat al-Masjid (2 rakah)
- After Wudu (2 rakah)
- Salat al-Duha (4-12 rakah)
- Istikharah prayer
- Night prayers during last 10 nights of Ramadan
- Night prayers during first 10 nights of Dhul-Hijjah

## Scholarly Opinions

### Tarawih Prayer
- **Hanafi position**: 20 rakah in sets of 2 with 10 salams
- **Maliki opinion**: 36 rakah total (20 in congregation + 16 during rest periods)
- **All four madhabs**: Agree on 20 rakah as the standard
- **Prophetic practice**: Evidence suggests Prophet ﷺ prayed 20 rakah during the three nights he led tarawih

### Primary vs. Temporary Residence
- Multiple scholars' opinions on determining primary residence
- Consideration of where most time is spent throughout the year
- University students' residence classification depends on duration and intention
- Work-related travel and its impact on residence determination

## Lessons & Practical Application

### Travel and Prayer Adaptations
- University students living on campus for 4+ years: campus becomes primary residence
- Coming home for breaks less than 15 days: pray qasr at family home
- Mail delivery location and work base help determine primary residence
- Flight delays don't immediately change traveler status if departure intention remains

### Cultural Events and Islamic Boundaries
- Culture should not override Islamic principles
- Advise event organizers with wisdom (hikmah) about Islamic guidelines
- Consider mahram/non-mahram relationships even within family gatherings
- Separate seating arrangements recommended for mixed family events
- Gentle guidance preferred over harsh confrontation

### Making Up Missed Prayers
- No need to specify exact dates when making intention for qada prayers
- General intention of "making up a missed prayer" is sufficient
- New Muslims who lived in non-Muslim lands may be excused from pre-conversion makeup prayers
- Priority should be given to making up prayers immediately to avoid complications

### Prayer During Illness
- Islam provides flexibility while maintaining the obligation
- Adapt prayer method to physical ability rather than abandoning prayer
- Only complete inability to move head absolves from prayer temporarily
- Recovery periods require making up missed prayers unless specific conditions apply
