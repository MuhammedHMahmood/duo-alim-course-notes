## Session Overview

This session covered a comprehensive review of the four farāʾiḍ (obligations) of wudu and then proceeded to the emphasized sunan (recommended acts) and adab (etiquette) of wudu. The class began with individual questioning to test students' understanding of water types permissible for purification and the requirements for washing different body parts during wudu. The instructor then introduced 17 emphasized sunan of wudu and 8 mustaḥabbāt (preferred etiquettes), explaining the practical application and flexibility of these recommendations based on circumstances.

---

## Key Themes

- **Distinction between obligations and recommendations**: Clear differentiation between farāʾiḍ (must be done), sunan muʾakkadah (emphasized recommendations), and mustaḥabbāt (preferred etiquette)
- **Practical flexibility in worship**: Sunan and mustaḥabbāt can be omitted when water is limited or when following them would cause difficulty or harm
- **Comprehensive purification**: Beyond the basic requirements, Islam encourages thorough and mindful purification with specific techniques and sequences
- **Natural water sources**: Emphasis on understanding what constitutes natural vs. artificial alterations to water

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Water Permissibility Rules
- **Natural alterations are permissible**: Seawater (salty), pond water (murky), well water with mineral taste - all remain valid for wudu as these are natural characteristics
- **Artificial alterations invalidate**: Rose water, heavily chlorinated pool water - invalid because external substances have artificially altered the water's qualities
- **Two-quality rule**: If two of the three qualities (color, taste, smell) are altered, water becomes invalid for wudu

### Thick vs. Thin Beard Ruling
- **Thin beard**: Water must reach the skin of the chin
- **Thick beard**: Water must reach the hair connected to the skin, but doesn't need to penetrate to the actual skin of the chin
- **Sunnah method**: Perform khilāl (running wet fingers through the beard) after washing the face

### Head Wiping Definition
- **Legal definition**: Only hair growing above the ears counts as "head"
- **Minimum requirement**: One-fourth of the head area
- **Hair below ears**: Cannot count toward the masḥ requirement, even if tied above the head

## Conditions & Categories

### The 17 Emphasized Sunan of Wudu:
1. **Intention** (niyyah)
2. **Using miswāk**
3. **Saying Bismillah**
4. **Washing hands to wrists initially**
5. **Maintaining correct order of limbs**
6. **Continuity** (not letting previous limb dry before washing next)
7. **Washing limbs three times each**
8. **Rinsing mouth three times**
9. **Rinsing soft part of nose three times**
10. **Khilāl of the beard**
11. **Khilāl between fingers and toes**
12. **Wiping entire head once and ears with same water**
13. **Rubbing limbs when washing**
14. **Starting with right limb for arms and feet**
15. **Starting from fingertips/toes when washing**
16. **Starting from front when wiping head**
17. **Wiping back of neck** (mandūb, not sunnah muʾakkadah)

### The 8 Etiquettes (Adab) of Wudu:
1. **Facing qibla** (when practically possible)
2. **Avoiding water splashing on body/clothes**
3. **Making prescribed duʿās**
4. **Mentioning Allah's name with each limb**
5. **Performing wudu independently**
6. **Performing before prayer time** (unless maʿdhūr)
7. **Reciting shahādah after completion**
8. **Drinking leftover water**

## Scholarly Opinions

### Miswāk Usage
- **Prophetic saying**: "As-siwāku mitharatun lil-fami wa murḍātun lil-rabbi" (The miswāk purifies the mouth and pleases Allah)
- **Toothbrush ruling**: Does not substitute for miswāk in fulfilling the sunnah
- **Proper holding**: Between fingers with pinky support from behind

### Neck Wiping Classification
- **Difference of opinion noted**: Some sources classify wiping back of neck as sunnah muʾakkadah, others as mandūb
- **Method**: Use back of fingers, no new water required
- **Area**: Flat part of neck only, not the throat or curved areas

## Lessons & Practical Application

### Flexibility in Practice
- When water is limited, prioritize farāʾiḍ over sunan
- When following adab would cause harm or difficulty (e.g., facing qibla in poorly designed wudu areas), safety takes precedence
- Practical considerations allow for adaptation while maintaining the spirit of the practice

### Daily Implementation
- **Continuity**: Don't let limbs dry between washing
- **Order matters for reward**: Following sequence increases spiritual benefit
- **Thoroughness**: Khilāl ensures complete coverage of beard, fingers, and toes
- **Mindfulness**: Dhikr during wudu maintains focus on worship rather than routine

### Water Conservation
- Sunan can be omitted when water conservation is necessary
- The practice encourages efficiency while maintaining spiritual mindfulness
- Natural water sources are preferred and generally permissible even with natural variations
