# Fiqh 101 - Study Notes
**Date:** 2025-12-15

---

## Session Overview

This session covered the intention (niyyah) for fasting, types of fasts, moon sighting for determining Ramadan, actions that invalidate fasting, exemptions from fasting, and the basics of spiritual retreat (i'tikaf). The lesson emphasized the critical importance of proper intention and provided detailed categorization of actions affecting the validity of fasting.

---

## Key Themes

- Proper intention (niyyah) is essential for all fasts and must be made at specific times depending on the type of fast
- Distinction between specified (muayyan) and unspecified (ghayr muayyan) fasts determines timing requirements for intention
- Moon sighting follows prophetic tradition with specific witness requirements
- Actions that invalidate fasting are categorized by their consequences (makeup, expiation, or both)
- Islamic calendar follows lunar months of either 29 or 30 days
- Certain individuals have exemptions from fasting with obligation to make up missed days

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Intention Requirements:**
- All fasts require proper intention
- Specified fasts (muayyan): intention can be made until midpoint of day (zahwatul kubra)
- Unspecified fasts (ghayr muayyan): intention must be made between Maghrib of previous day and Fajr of fasting day

**Moon Sighting Rules:**
- Based on Prophetic hadith: "Start your fast by sight of the moon and break your fast by sight of the moon"
- If clouds obstruct: complete 30 days of Sha'ban
- For beginning of Ramadan with obstruction: one upright witness sufficient (including slave or woman)
- For Eid al-Fitr with obstruction: requires two male witnesses OR one male and two females
- Without obstruction: large group must sight the moon

**Fasting Violations - Four Categories:**
1. Require makeup + expiation: eating, drinking, intercourse (when done intentionally)
2. Require makeup only: suppository use, accidentally swallowing water during wudu, coercion
3. Require nothing, not disliked: blood cupping (if not weakening), miswak, mouth/nose rinsing without swallowing
4. Require nothing, but disliked: tasting food without swallowing, showing affection to spouse without security

## Conditions & Categories

**Types of Fasts:**
- Specified (Muayyan): Ramadan fasts, voluntary fasts for specific days, specified vows
- Unspecified (Ghayr Muayyan): makeup fasts, expiation fasts, unspecified vows

**Exemptions from Fasting:**
- Sick person fearing illness will worsen
- Pregnant or nursing woman fearing harm to self or baby
- Person fearing death from severe thirst
- Traveler (though preferable to fast if able)

**I'tikaf Categories:**
- Mandatory (wajib): when vowed
- Communal sunnah (kifaya): last ten nights of Ramadan
- Recommended: any time in masjid with intention

---

## Scholarly Opinions

**Forgetfulness vs. Mistake:**
- Forgetfulness (nisyan): eating/drinking while forgetting one is fasting - no consequences
- Mistake (khata'): accidentally swallowing water during wudu - requires makeup only

**Voluntary Fast Breaking:**
- One narration allows breaking voluntary fast without excuse
- If broken, mandatory to make up (except on prohibited days: two Eids, days of Tashreeq)

**Fidya Payment:**
- For elderly unable to fast: half sa' of wheat or monetary equivalent per day
- Only applicable when person is genuinely unable to fast due to age/permanent condition

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Daily Practice:**
- Make intention before Fajr for unspecified fasts
- Can make intention until midday for Ramadan and voluntary fasts
- Eat suhoor close to Fajr time
- Break fast immediately when Maghrib enters (unless cloudy day requiring certainty)

**When Fast is Broken:**
- Must practice imsak (withholding) for remainder of day
- Applies to: those who broke fast, returning travelers not previously fasting, women becoming pure from menstruation, new Muslims, children reaching puberty

**Making Up Fasts:**
- Need not be consecutive (except for kaffara)
- Should not be delayed unnecessarily
- Reasonable estimation acceptable for calculating missed days over years

**I'tikaf Guidelines:**
- Men: must be in designated masjid
- Women: designated prayer area in home
- Can eat, drink, sleep, talk appropriately
- Cannot leave except for necessity (bathroom, emergency, Friday prayer)
- Avoid idle talk and inappropriate speech
