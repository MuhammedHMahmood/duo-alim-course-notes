## Session Overview

This session focused on the nullifiers of wudu and the obligations and methods of ghusl (ritual bath). The instructor covered nine specific actions that invalidate wudu, ranging from bodily emissions to loss of consciousness. The class then transitioned to ghusl requirements, detailing both the minimum obligatory (fard) method and the complete sunnah method. Three categories of ghusl were discussed: obligatory (fard), mandatory (wajib), and recommended (nafl). The session concluded with clarifications on situations that do NOT require ghusl and addressed numerous practical questions from students.

## Key Themes

- Distinction between minor ritual impurity (requiring wudu) and major ritual impurity (requiring ghusl)
- Detailed understanding of what constitutes "exiting" from different body openings
- The concept of istibra and istinja for males
- Categories of ghusl and their respective requirements
- Practical applications of fiqh rulings in daily life

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Nullifiers of Wudu (Nine Categories):

1. **Anything exiting from the two openings** - Even appearance at the tip is considered exiting
2. **Filth flowing from other openings** - Must flow beyond point of exit; mere appearance at surface doesn't break wudu
3. **Vomiting a mouthful or more** - Amount that cannot be contained without strain
4. **Sleeping without buttocks firmly planted** - Lying down or leaning invalidates wudu
5. **Loss of consciousness**
6. **Insanity** - Loss of perception of reality
7. **Drunkenness**
8. **Laughing out loud during salah by adults** - Breaks both wudu and salah
9. **Contact between erect male and female private parts** - Direct contact nullifies wudu

### Obligatory Elements of Ghusl (Minimum Requirements):
1. Rinsing mouth entirely once
2. Rinsing nose up to the bone once  
3. Washing entire body once without undue difficulty

### Sunnah Method of Ghusl (In Order):
1. Making intention (niyyah)
2. Washing hands to wrists
3. Washing private parts front and back
4. Washing off any filth from body
5. Performing complete wudu
6. Pouring water over entire body three times, starting with head

## Conditions & Categories

### Categories of Ghusl:

**1. Obligatory (Fard)** - Required when:
- Emission of sexual fluid (mani) with or without pleasure
- Penetration of male private part into female private part or back area
- End of menstruation or postnatal bleeding

**2. Mandatory (Wajib)**:
- Person becoming Muslim while in state of major impurity
- (Note: Sounder opinion considers this still fard)

**3. Recommended (Nafl)** - For:
- Friday prayer (Jumu'ah)
- Two Eid prayers
- Entering state of ihram
- Day of Arafah
- Entering Makkah or Madinah
- Visiting the Prophet ﷺ

### Ma'zur (Chronically Excused) Conditions:
- Excuse must persist for entire prayer time
- Person makes wudu at beginning of each prayer time
- Wudu remains valid until that prayer time ends
- Can pray even if excuse occurs during salah

## Scholarly Opinions

### Imam Abu Hanifa's Position:
- Female vaginal discharge is not considered impure but categorized as similar to sweat
- Does not require ghusl but may require wudu depending on circumstances

### Beard Washing Differences:
- In wudu: Thickness of beard determines washing method
- In ghusl: All beards require water to reach the skin regardless of thickness

### Movement During Salah:
- Amal Kathir (excessive movement) determined by whether person appears to be praying from distance
- If observer cannot tell person is praying due to movement, salah is invalid

## Lessons & Practical Application

### For Males - Restroom Etiquette:
- Must perform istibra before istinja
- Istibra ensures complete removal of fluids through pressure methods (coughing, standing, tissue placement)
- Prevents later invalidation of wudu from residual fluids

### During Salah Situations:
- If wudu breaks during congregational prayer, exit carefully (preferably during sajda)
- If leading prayer and wudu breaks, appoint deputy to continue
- Laughing during prayer: Silent laughter breaks salah only; loud laughter breaks both salah and wudu

### Medical Considerations:
- Chronic conditions (frequent gas, incontinence) fall under ma'zur ruling
- Temporary dietary issues don't constitute chronic excuse
- Person can delay prayer within time limit to manage temporary conditions

### Fasting Considerations:
- When performing ghusl while fasting, be careful with mouth and nose rinsing
- Avoid excessive water that might enter throat/stomach

### Mixed Gathering Guidelines:
- Accidental contact doesn't break wudu through touch
- Proper Islamic etiquette should prevent such situations

### Blood and Wound Care:
- Blood appearing at wound surface without flowing doesn't break wudu
- Blood-stained clothing from non-flowing blood remains pure
- Once blood flows from its place, wudu is broken and area becomes impure
