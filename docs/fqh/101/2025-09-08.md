**Date:** 2025-09-08

## Session Overview

This session covered two major topics in Islamic jurisprudence: Tayammum (dry ablution) and wiping over foot coverings (Khuf). The lesson detailed the conditions, procedures, and rulings for both practices as alternatives to regular wudu when circumstances require them. The class concluded with a review quiz covering previous lessons on ghusl and wudu.

---

## Key Themes

- Tayammum as a substitute for wudu when water is unavailable or inaccessible
- The three obligatory components of tayammum: intention, striking earth and wiping face, striking earth and wiping arms
- Conditions and time limits for wiping over khuf (foot coverings)
- The principle of reasonable likelihood in determining valid excuses
- Distinction between prayers with qada (makeup) and those without

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Tayammum Permissibility
Tayammum is valid when there is a legitimate excuse:
1. **Distance from water**: Roughly 1.16 miles (1 legal mile) or 1.86 kilometers away
2. **Extreme cold**: Risk of frostbite or limb damage with no means to heat water
3. **Sickness**: Where water use would worsen illness or prolong recovery
4. **Wounds on most of body**: Majority of body has wounds
5. **Fear of thirst**: Risk of dehydration from using available water
6. **Fear of enemy or wild beast**: Water available but inaccessible due to danger
7. **Lack of apparatus**: No means to extract water (rope, bucket, etc.)
8. **Fear of missing funeral/Eid prayer**: Risk of missing prayers without qada

### Tayammum Procedure
Three obligatory components:
1. **Intention** - to remove state of impurity or enable prayer
2. **First strike**: Strike earth, wipe entire face
3. **Second strike**: Strike earth, wipe arms up to and including elbows

### Khuf (Foot Covering) Rules
- Valid for both residents (24 hours) and travelers (72 hours)
- Time period begins when wudu is nullified, not when khuf is worn
- Must be worn in state of ritual purity
- Can wash feet first, put on khuf, then complete wudu

## Conditions & Categories

### Tayammum Substances
**Permitted**: Any pure substance of earth that is:
- Not malleable (excludes metals)
- Cannot burn to ashes (excludes wood, plants)
- Examples: dirt, sand, limestone, stones, dust on clothes (if visible after rubbing)

### Khuf Conditions
Must be:
1. Made of thick material (wool, broadcloth, etc.)
2. Stay on foot without external tying
3. Not see-through
4. Cover foot up to and including ankles
5. Able to walk 3.48 miles without tearing
6. Water should not seep through when wiping

### Invalidation of Khuf Wiping
1. Anything that nullifies wudu
2. Removing even one khuf
3. Expiration of time period (unless extreme cold conditions)
4. Most of foot leaving the khuf

## Scholarly Opinions

- **Forgetting water availability**: If one performs tayammum forgetting they had water, prayers don't need to be repeated upon remembering
- **Imitation prayer**: When neither water nor earth substances are available, one performs prayer motions without intention, then makes up the prayer later (masalah of faqid al-tahurain)
- **Wiping area**: Minimum area equivalent to three smallest fingers must be wiped on top of each khuf
- **Hole tolerance**: Holes smaller than the area of three smallest toes are permissible

## Lessons & Practical Application

### Contemporary Applications
- Using GPS/maps to determine water availability while traveling
- Understanding that multiple Eid jamaats may affect tayammum rulings
- Colostomy bag patients fall under ma'zur ruling for wudu
- Modern waterproof socks can qualify as valid khuf if meeting all conditions

### Practical Scenarios Discussed
- Traffic delays during Eid with crowded wudu facilities
- Extreme weather conditions and water heating capabilities
- Medical conditions like arthritis affecting water use
- Breaking wudu during prayer and rejoining congregation

### Key Principles
- Reasonable likelihood standard for determining valid excuses
- Priority of maintaining prayer times while seeking proper purification
- Flexibility in Islamic law for genuine hardship situations
- Importance of intention in all acts of worship
