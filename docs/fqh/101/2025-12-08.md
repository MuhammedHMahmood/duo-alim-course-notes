## Session Overview

This session covered the final chapter of the salah section, focusing on the complete funeral prayer (janazah) process. The lesson comprehensively addressed the three main components: ghusl (ritual washing), kafan (shrouding), salat al-janazah (funeral prayer), and burial procedures. The session concluded with an introduction to the chapter on fasting (sawm), including its legal definition and seven types of fasting.

- Complete funeral procedures as acts of worship and community obligation

- Fasting as the next pillar of Islam after completing the salah chapters

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Pre-Death Procedures:**
- Direct dying person toward Qibla on right side or with feet toward Qibla
- Recite shahada in their presence (talqeen) without commanding them directly
- Close eyes and jaws after death with specific dua

**Ghusl Requirements:**
- Perform wudu except rinsing mouth and nose
- Three complete washes with warm water and sidr if available
- Wash right side first, then left side, then complete body wash
- No grooming - no cutting nails, combing hair, or trimming

**Kafan (Shrouding) Rules:**
- Males: 3 cloths (qamees, izar, lifafa)
- Females: 5 cloths (same as males plus khimar and khirqah)
- White cotton preferred
- Right side always on top when wrapping
- Hair of females divided into two braids across chest

**Salat al-Janazah:**
- 4 takbirat with specific recitations
- After 1st takbir: thana
- After 2nd takbir: salawat on Prophet
- After 3rd takbir: dua for deceased (different for adults vs children)
- After 4th takbir: salam

**Burial Procedures:**
- Lahad (L-shaped) grave preferred over shiq (straight down)
- Body placed on right side facing Qibla
- Grave depth: minimum half body length
- Dirt mounded like camel's hump

## Conditions & Categories

**Who Requires Ghusl:**
- All deceased Muslims
- Martyrs are exceptions (buried in their clothes without washing unless in state of janabah)

**Shrouding Categories:**
1. Kafan al-sunnah: Optimal shrouding (3 cloths male, 5 cloths female)
2. Kafan al-kifaya: Minimum sufficient (2 cloths + khimar for females)
3. Kafan al-darura: Emergency shrouding (whatever available)

**Types of Fasting:**
1. Obligatory (fard): Ramadan, makeup days, expiation, vowed fasts
2. Mandatory (wajib): Making up voluntary fasts
3. Sunnah: Day of Ashura (10th Muharram)
4. Recommended (mustahab): 3 days monthly, Mondays/Thursdays, 6 days Shawwal
5. Voluntary (nafl): Any permissible day
6. Prohibitively disliked (makruh tahrimi): Eid days, days of tashriq
7. Mildly disliked (makruh tanzihi): Singling out Saturdays, Persian holidays, consecutive fasting

## Scholarly Opinions

**Talqeen After Burial:**
- Difference of opinion among Hanafi scholars whether to continue reciting shahada after burial
- Authors' position: permissible if one can do it

**Martyrdom Definition:**
- Legal martyrs (for burial purposes): killed by enemy combatants, rebels, highway brigands, robbers, or unjustly killed by Muslim with sharp object
- Distinction between legal ruling and spiritual martyrdom (only Allah knows true martyrs)
- Time factor: if conscious for entire prayer time after injury, treated as natural death

**Grave Visitation:**
- Recommended to visit graves and recite Quran (especially Surah Yasin)
- Disliked to sit at graves except for Quran recitation or dhikr

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Community Responsibilities:**
- Funeral prayer is fard al-kifaya (communal obligation)
- Participation in funeral rites provides significant reminders about hereafter
- Proper procedures ensure dignity and respect for deceased

**Practical Considerations:**
- Adjustments may be needed due to autopsy, accidents, or medical conditions
- Ghusl still counts even if some steps must be modified due to circumstances
- Transportation and burial logistics must maintain respect for deceased

**Fasting Preparation:**
- Understanding different categories helps proper observance
- Legal definition: withholding from eating, drinking, intimacy during daylight hours with intention
- Must be performed by one who is capable and required to do so

**Key Reminders:**
- Shrouds should not contain Quranic verses or names of Allah (will be disrespected underground)
- No grooming of deceased - maintain natural state
- Funeral procedures serve as powerful reminders of our temporary nature in this life
