## Session Overview

This class covered the conditions (shara'it) and integrals (arkan) of prayer, establishing the foundation for valid salah. The instructor explained the differences between conditions that must be met before prayer begins and integrals that are part of the prayer itself. Key discussions included prayer requirements, physical purity, facing the qibla, and the mandatory requisites (wajibat) that complete the prayer structure.

- **Conditions vs. Integrals**: Clear distinction between pre-prayer requirements and in-prayer obligations
- **Physical and Spiritual Preparation**: Emphasis on ritual purity, cleanliness, and proper covering
- **Prayer Structure**: Understanding the bare minimum requirements versus complete prayer format
- **Flexibility in Exceptional Circumstances**: Allowances when normal conditions cannot be met
- **Gender Considerations**: Different prayer requirements and modesty considerations for men and women

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

### Conditions (Shara'it) of Prayer
**Seven essential conditions must be met before prayer begins:**

1. **Ritual Purity**: Must be in state of wudu; if in janabah, must perform ghusl
2. **Physical Cleanliness**: Person, clothing, and prayer place must be free from filth (except excused amounts)
3. **Covering Nakedness (Aura)**:
   - **Men**: From below navel to below knees (knees included)
   - **Women**: Entire body except face, hands, and feet
   - **Slave women**: Same as men except abdomen and back must also be covered
4. **Facing Qibla**: 
   - In Makkah: Face Kaaba directly
   - Elsewhere: Face direction of Qibla (±45 degrees acceptable due to distance)
5. **Prayer Time**: Must be within designated time for each prayer
6. **Intention**: Must know which prayer is being performed (place: heart)
7. **Tahrima**: Opening takbir "Allahu Akbar" must be uttered aloud (place: tongue)

### Integrals (Arkan) of Prayer
**Six essential components that if missed invalidate the prayer:**

1. **Tahrima**: Opening takbir (according to Imam Muhammad; others consider it a condition)
2. **Standing (Qiyam)**: If physically able (not required for voluntary prayers)
3. **Quranic Recitation**: Minimum one verse in any two rakahs of obligatory prayer, all rakahs of witr/voluntary
4. **Bowing (Ruku)**
5. **Prostration (Sujud)**: Must touch ground with forehead, both hands, both knees, bottom of toes
6. **Final Sitting**: For duration of tashahud recitation

### Key Principle
**Prayer must be ended by one's own action, and it is wajib that this action be saying "as-salam"**

## Conditions & Categories

### Exceptional Circumstances - Conditions May Be Overlooked:
- Lack of water for purification when needed for survival
- Insufficient clothing to cover aura
- Inability to face qibla due to captivity, sickness, or safety concerns
- Unknown qibla direction (make educated guess using sun, stars, etc.)

### Prayer Categories by Recitation:
- **Silent Prayers (Sirran)**: Zuhr, Asr
- **Loud Prayers (Jahran)**: Fajr, Maghrib, Isha (first two rakahs only)

### Recitation Rules:
- **Imam**: Recites aloud in designated prayers, silently in others
- **Followers**: No recitation required (imam's recitation suffices for all)
- **Individual Prayer**: Must recite in all rakahs

## Scholarly Opinions

### Tahrima Classification:
- **Imam Muhammad**: Considers tahrima an integral (rukn) of prayer
- **Imam Abu Hanifa & Abu Yusuf**: Consider it a condition (shart)
- **Practical Effect**: Same in both opinions - cannot be omitted

### Bismillah in Prayer:
- **Difference of Opinion**: Whether "Bismillahir Rahmanir Rahim" is a verse of Quran or separator between surahs
- **Practice**: Some recite it aloud, others silently, based on this difference

## Lessons & Practical Application

### Prayer Preparation:
- Ensure ritual and physical purity before beginning
- Use available technology (apps, compass) to find qibla direction
- Dress appropriately to cover aura requirements

### During Prayer:
- Practice **ta'adil al-arkan**: Don't rush through prayer components
- Maintain proper posture and stillness in each position
- Follow imam closely without getting ahead

### Special Considerations:
- **Women's Prayer**: More covered positions to maintain modesty
- **Travel Situations**: Make educated guesses when exact conditions unavailable
- **Following Imam**: Trust that imam's recitation covers followers' obligation

### Completing Prayer:
- Must end with salam ("As-salamu alaykum wa rahmatullah")
- Turn right first, then left
- Don't get ahead of imam when following in congregation

### Mandatory Requisites Include:
- Specific "Allahu Akbar" for tahrima
- Surah Fatiha plus additional verses/surah in first two rakahs
- Proper prostration with forehead and nose
- Complete tashahud in both sittings
- Six extra takbirs in Eid prayers
- Qunoot recitation in Witr prayer
