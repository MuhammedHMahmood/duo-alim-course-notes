**Date:** 2025-10-13

## Session Overview

This session introduced the chapter on Salah (ritual prayer), beginning with an explanation of why purification (Tahara) is studied before prayer in Islamic jurisprudence. The class covered the five daily prayer times, their scriptural basis, and scholarly differences regarding specific timing boundaries. Key focus was placed on the differences of opinion between Imam Abu Hanifa and other scholars regarding Dhuhr/Asr timing boundaries and Maghrib/Isha transitions.

---

## Key Themes

- **Prerequisite relationship between purification and prayer**: Tahara must precede Salah
- **Divine establishment of prayer times**: Allah has ordained specific times for each prayer
- **Scholarly methodology in determining prayer boundaries**: Using shadow measurements and celestial observations
- **Jurisprudential differences**: Various valid opinions among classical scholars on timing boundaries
- **Practical application**: Following local mosque practices while understanding different madhab positions

## Fiqh Rulings & Evidence

**Primary Evidence for Prayer Obligation:**
- Quran: "Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers a decree of specified times" (Surah An-Nisa, 4:103)
- Hadith of Isra wal Mi'raj: Originally 50 prayers reduced to 5 with reward of 50
- Hadith of Jibreel's Imamah: Angel Jibreel taught prayer times by leading Prophet for two days

**Prayer Time Determinations:**
1. **Fajr**: From true dawn (Sadiq) until right before sunrise
2. **Dhuhr**: From after midday (Zawal) until shadow length varies by opinion
3. **Asr**: Begins when Dhuhr ends, continues until right before sunset
4. **Maghrib**: From sunset until twilight disappears (red or white - scholarly difference)
5. **Isha**: From end of Maghrib time until right before Fajr

---

## Conditions & Categories

**Shadow Measurement Method for Midday:**
- Place stick vertically in even ground
- Observe shadow length
- When shadow stops shortening = midday has passed
- Dhuhr time begins immediately after

**Categories of Dawn:**
- **False Dawn (Kadhib)**: Vertical light extending upward
- **True Dawn (Sadiq)**: Horizontal white light across horizon
- Time difference: Approximately 12 minutes (3 degrees of sun's angle)

**Twilight Categories:**
- **White Twilight**: Appears first after sunset
- **Red Twilight**: Appears after white twilight disappears

---

## Scholarly Opinions

**Dhuhr Ending Time (When Asr Begins):**
- **Imam Abu Hanifa**: Shadow equals twice object's length (excluding original shadow)
  - Results in longer Dhuhr time
- **Imam Abu Yusuf, Imam Muhammad, Imam Shafi'i**: Shadow equals object's length (excluding original)
  - Results in earlier Asr time
- **Imam Tahawi**: Preferred the second opinion (companions' view)

**Maghrib Ending Time:**
- **Imam Abu Hanifa**: Ends when white twilight disappears (shorter Maghrib time)
- **Imam Abu Yusuf & Imam Muhammad**: Ends when red twilight disappears (longer Maghrib time)

**Practical Application:**
- Followers may pray according to local mosque timing
- No contradiction when following valid scholarly opinions
- Different madhabs represent legitimate interpretations

## Lessons & Practical Application

**Spiritual Reflection:**
- Human weakness acknowledged: "And mankind was created weak" (Quran)
- Contrast with pet owners' dedication: Non-Muslims wake early for pets while Muslims struggle with Fajr
- Gratitude principle: Remember Allah's countless blessings when feeling lazy about prayer

**Practical Guidelines:**
- When traveling or with excuse: Some flexibility in following different timing opinions
- As residents: Generally follow your chosen madhab's timing
- Local mosque attendance: Acceptable to follow mosque's established prayer times
- Witr prayer: Must be prayed after Isha, not before (sequential order required)

**Contemporary Challenges:**
- Modern tendency to prioritize worldly matters over prayer obligations
- Weekly vs. daily mosque attendance disparity (Jumu'ah vs. daily prayers)
- Need for consistent commitment despite seasonal challenges (cold weather, comfortable bedding)
