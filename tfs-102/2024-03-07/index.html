    TFS 102 - Study Notes

    Date: March 7, 2024 Surah Covered: Surah Al-Asr (Completion of Tafseer)

    Session Overview

    This session completes the tafseer of Surah Al-Asr, which was started in the previous class (TFS 101). The instructor finishes the explanation of the surah's core message and its four conditions for escaping loss. The session also includes a Q&A segment covering topics such as jinn, Ramadan, lineage of the prophets, student loans, and other matters.

    Key Themes and Topics

    1. The Oath of Time (Al-Asr)

    • Allah takes an oath by time because time moves in one direction only -- it is irreversible.
    • Time itself serves as a witness that human beings are, by default, in a state of loss.
    • The framework: humans are "guilty until proven innocent" -- everyone is in loss unless they meet the four conditions of exception.

    2. The Four Conditions for Success (Minimum Requirements)

    These are the minimum -- not maximum -- requirements for success in this life and the next:

    1. Iman (Faith)
    2. Amal Salih (Righteous Deeds)
    3. Tawasaw bil-Haqq (Mutual Enjoining of Truth)
    4. Tawasaw bil-Sabr (Mutual Enjoining of Patience)

    3. The Centrality of Iman

    • Iman answers the three fundamental existential questions every conscious human asks:
    • Where did I come from? -- Created by Allah.
    • Where am I going? -- To the next life, as Allah has willed.
    • What is the purpose of life? -- To please the Creator and live according to His will.
    • Without iman, a person lives in confusion and cannot satisfactorily answer these questions.

    4. Critique of the Atheist Position

    • The atheist claim of "no evidence for the afterlife" commits a logical fallacy: absence of evidence is not evidence of absence.
    • The claim that "survival" is life's purpose fails because no one survives permanently, meaning no one achieves the purpose, making the concept of a role model impossible.
    • Claiming life is purposeless while acknowledging everything within life has purpose is an internal contradiction.

    5. Iman as an Anchor in Hardship

    • The example of a woman in Gaza who lost her family: without iman, such a person would be driven to despair or insanity. With iman and tawakkul, she has conviction that justice will come on the Day of Judgment.
    • An anecdote about a man from India who left Islam and became an atheist: he himself admitted to his wife that she would die with peace (believing in the afterlife), while he would die with only confusion and regret.

    6. The Inseparability of Iman and Amal Salih

    • True iman necessarily leads to righteous action. If a person believes Allah is worthy of worship but does not worship Him, the iman is weak or compromised.
    • Analogy: claiming to love a country's constitution while never following its laws is hypocritical. Similarly, believing in Allah without practicing His commands is contradictory.

    7. Tawasaw bil-Haqq -- Enjoining Truth

    • Al-Haqq requires two qualities simultaneously: a statement must be both true and good.
    • Backbiting (ghibah) is true but not good -- therefore it is not haqq.
    • False praise is good in appearance but not true -- therefore it is not haqq.
    • If a negative statement about someone is not even true, it is not ghibah but slander (buhtan), which is worse.
    • Speaking truth is difficult; people fear losing their jobs, status, or safety.

    8. The Link Between Haqq and Sabr

    • When a person speaks the truth, hardship and opposition follow.
    • Therefore, patience (sabr) is the necessary companion of truth (haqq) -- this is why Allah mentions them back to back.
    • This link is demonstrated in the life of the Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wasallam): he proclaimed the truth, faced boycott, assassination attempts, and was eventually forced to make hijrah, but he exercised patience throughout.

    9. Azimah vs. Rukhsah in Speaking Truth

    • Azimah (full conformity): Speaking truth despite the threat of imprisonment or death -- this earns immense reward.
    • Rukhsah (partial conformity / concession): If there is a genuine fear for one's life or severe persecution, remaining silent while hating the wrongdoing in the heart is permissible. This exception is established in the Quran itself (one may even utter words of kufr under threat of death).
    • However, if the fear is merely losing a job or mild inconvenience, that is not a valid excuse to conceal the truth.

    10. Surah Al-Asr as a Table of Contents

    • The four conditions (iman, amal salih, tawasaw bil-haqq, tawasaw bil-sabr) serve as a framework that Allah expands upon in the surahs that follow: Al-Humazah, Al-Fil, Quraysh, Al-Ma'un, and onward to the end of the Quran.

    Main Tafseer Points

    1. Default state of humanity is loss. The surah establishes that every human being is in a state of loss (khusr) by default. Success must be actively earned.

    2. The four exceptions are interconnected. They form a chain: iman leads to amal salih, which includes tawasaw bil-haqq, which necessitates tawasaw bil-sabr. Each one flows into the next.

    3. Iman is the foundation. Without it, a person cannot answer the most basic existential questions and lives in perpetual confusion -- a loss in both dunya and akhirah.

    4. Amal salih is the proof of iman. Belief without action is empty, just as claiming to respect a constitution while violating its laws is contradictory.

    5. Haqq has a precise definition. It is not merely "truth" -- it must be both true and good. This filters out backbiting (true but harmful) and flattery (seemingly good but false).

    6. Sabr is the cost of haqq. Speaking truth invites hardship, so patience is the required response. Allah placed them together deliberately.

    7. The Prophetic model. The Prophet's life is the living demonstration of these four principles: he had iman, performed righteous deeds, spoke the truth in the face of opposition, and endured with patience.

    8. Hadith cited: "Ashaddu al-naasi bala'an al-anbiya, thumma al-amthal fal-amthal" -- The most severe trials befall the prophets, then those closest to their path, and so on.

    Lessons and Practical Takeaways

    • Iman provides psychological resilience. Even in the worst worldly circumstances (loss of family, oppression, injustice), a believer's faith provides hope and a framework for meaning. Without it, despair is the logical outcome.
    • Action validates belief. Claiming faith while being passive about worship and righteous deeds weakens iman over time. A person may eventually lose iman altogether through persistent inaction.
    • Speaking truth is a communal obligation. The surah uses the reciprocal form "tawasaw" (they enjoin each other), indicating that standing for truth is not an individual act alone but a collective responsibility.
    • Expect difficulty when standing for truth. It is the natural consequence. The question is not whether hardship will come, but whether one will be patient through it.
    • Know the difference between genuine fear and mere inconvenience. Fear for one's life is a valid excuse for silence, but fear of mild professional or social consequences is not a sufficient reason to abandon truth.
    • This surah is a checklist. One can evaluate oneself regularly: Do I have iman? Am I performing righteous deeds? Am I speaking and encouraging truth? Am I patient through the difficulties that follow?

    Q&A Highlights

    Jinn and Ruqyah

    • Jinn exist and can attack humans regardless of the jinn's or the human's religious background.
    • The remedy lies in Ruqyah Shar'iyyah (Quranic healing) performed by someone trained in this specific science -- not just any imam.
    • The reciter must be seeking help from Allah, not from shayateen.

    Lineage (Nasab) of the Prophets and the Father of Ibrahim

    • The hadith about the purity of prophetic lineage refers to the absence of zina (illegitimate birth) in the chain -- purity of lineage.
    • The Quranic reference to mushrikeen as impure refers to impurity of creed (aqidah).
    • There is no contradiction: the lineage is pure in terms of legitimacy, while ancestors may have held incorrect beliefs.
    • Azar was the father of Ibrahim (alayhi al-salam), and there should be no difficulty accepting this, just as the wives of Nuh and Lut and the son of Nuh were disbelievers.

    Parents of the Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wasallam)

    • They passed away before prophethood during the period of Fatrah al-Wahi.
    • Ash'ari and Maturidi position: People of the Fatrah period will be in Jannah.
    • Ibn Taymiyyah's position: They will be given a test on the Day of Judgment, and their fate decided accordingly.
    • Both views lead to the conclusion that the Prophet's parents will be in Jannah.

    Abu Talib's Punishment Being Reduced

    • The Prophet initially made du'a for Abu Talib before the ayah forbidding istighfar for mushrikeen was revealed.
    • Per a hadith in Sahih Muslim, Abu Talib's punishment was lightened, but he is still in the lightest form of hellfire punishment, yet he will think he is in the worst condition.
    • This is an exceptional case due to Abu Talib's defense of the Prophet and Islam -- it is not a general rule. Du'a of istighfar for mushrikeen does not benefit them as a general principle.

    Student Loans

    • Interest-free student loans (e.g., government loans without interest) are permissible.
    • Interest-bearing loans from banks or universities involve sin due to the interest component.
    • However, a sinful transaction does not make all subsequent earnings haram. The sin is in the interest payment itself, not in every dollar earned after obtaining the degree.

    Talismans and Numerology Plaques

    • Decorative designs are permissible.
    • Numbers representing Quranic surahs or ayat (numerology) are not haram, though some scholars are uncomfortable with the practice.
    • If the numbers represent something unknown or involve seeking help from shayateen, then it would be haram.

    Sleep Paralysis

    • There is no specific mention of sleep paralysis in the Quran or hadith.
    • It is possible that it occurs due to jinn, but there is no definitive Islamic ruling on it.

    Witr Prayer in Saudi Arabia (Hanafi Following Hanbali Jama'ah in Ramadan)

    • In Ramadan, praying witr with the jama'ah (even following a different madhhab's method, including raising hands and making du'a) is preferred.
    • One can pray the additional 10 raka'at of taraweeh individually afterwards.
